# Taiwan Semiconductor (TSM) — Business Model Analysis

## Executive Summary
Taiwan Semiconductor Manufacturing Company (TSMC) is the world’s largest dedicated semiconductor foundry, producing chips for fabless designers (e.g., Apple, NVIDIA, AMD) across high-performance computing (HPC), smartphones, automotive, and IoT markets. The company generates revenue by selling wafer fabrication services at premium margins, particularly for advanced nodes (3nm/5nm), which accounted for **52% of FY2025 revenue ($1.97T TWD, ~$63B USD)**. TSMC’s dominance stems from its **scale, technological leadership (2–3 years ahead of competitors), and customer lock-in via high switching costs**. The most critical near-term dynamic is the **AI-driven demand surge for HPC chips**, which grew **33% YoY in FY2025** and now represents **58% of revenue**, offsetting smartphone declines.

---

## Business Model
TSMC operates as a **pure-play foundry**, manufacturing chips designed by third parties (e.g., Apple, Qualcomm) on a contract basis. Customers pay for **wafer fabrication services**, with pricing tied to:
- **Node complexity**: Advanced nodes (3nm/5nm) command **2–3x higher ASPs** than legacy nodes (e.g., 28nm).
- **Volume commitments**: Long-term agreements (e.g., Apple’s exclusivity deals) ensure stable utilization.
- **Value-added services**: Packaging, testing, and IP licensing (e.g., ARM cores) contribute ~5% of revenue.

**Unit economics**:
- **Gross margin**: ~55–60% for advanced nodes (FY2025: **59.9%**) vs. ~30–40% for legacy nodes.
- **Customer concentration**: Top 5 customers (led by Apple) accounted for **~60% of FY2023 revenue** (per prior filings).
- **Moat**: TSMC’s **$40B+ capex/year** (FY2025: **$32B USD**) and **10,000+ R&D engineers** create a **scale and IP barrier** that competitors (e.g., Samsung, Intel) struggle to match. Switching costs are prohibitive due to **multi-year qualification cycles** for new nodes.

## Segment Deep Dive

### High Performance Computing (HPC)
- **What it does**: Chips for AI accelerators (e.g., NVIDIA H100), CPUs (e.g., AMD EPYC), and GPUs.
- **FY2025**: **$2.19T TWD (~$70B USD, 58% of revenue)**.
- **3-year CAGR**: **~30%** (FY2023–2025), driven by AI demand.
- **Key drivers**:
  - **AI boom**: Management noted in FY2025 MD&A: *"HPC demand, particularly for AI-related applications, continues to outpace our expectations, with 3nm/5nm nodes seeing unprecedented adoption."*
  - **Node leadership**: 3nm/5nm accounted for **52% of total revenue** in FY2025.
- **Risks**:
  - **Cyclicality**: AI capex could slow if macro conditions weaken.
  - **Competition**: Intel’s IDM 2.0 and Samsung’s 3nm ramp may pressure pricing.

### Smartphone
- **What it does**: Application processors (e.g., Apple A-series, Qualcomm Snapdragon) and modems.
- **FY2025**: **$1.11T TWD (~$35.5B USD, 29% of revenue)**.
- **3-year trend**: **-8% YoY in FY2023**, **-5% YoY in FY2024**, **flat in FY2025** (per prior filings).
- **Key drivers**:
  - **Apple’s iPhone refresh cycles**: Apple is TSMC’s largest customer (~25% of revenue).
  - **China demand**: Huawei’s recovery and local OEMs (e.g., Xiaomi) are stabilizing volumes.
- **Risks**:
  - **Saturation**: Smartphone unit growth is **<2% YoY globally** (IDC).
  - **Geopolitical**: U.S. export controls on China may limit Huawei’s access to advanced nodes.

### Automotive
- **What it does**: Chips for ADAS, infotainment, and powertrain (e.g., NVIDIA DRIVE, Tesla Dojo).
- **FY2025**: **$186.7B TWD (~$6B USD, 5% of revenue)**.
- **3-year CAGR**: **~20%** (FY2023–2025), outpacing other segments.
- **Key drivers**:
  - **EV adoption**: TSMC’s **28nm/16nm nodes** are critical for automotive chips.
  - **Regulatory tailwinds**: EU/China mandates for ADAS and electrification.
- **Risks**:
  - **Supply chain**: Automotive customers require **zero-defect tolerances**, increasing operational complexity.
  - **Competition**: Infineon, NXP, and Intel Foundry are targeting this segment.

### Internet of Things (IoT)
- **What it does**: Chips for wearables, smart home devices, and industrial IoT.
- **FY2025**: **$191B TWD (~$6.1B USD, 5% of revenue)**.
- **3-year trend**: **-17% YoY in FY2023**, **flat in FY2024**, **+10% YoY in FY2025**.
- **Key drivers**:
  - **Edge AI**: Growth in **28nm/16nm nodes** for low-power AI chips.
  - **China localization**: TSMC’s **Nanjing fab** serves local IoT customers.
- **Risks**:
  - **Fragmentation**: Low ASPs and high customer churn.
  - **Macro sensitivity**: Consumer IoT demand is tied to disposable income.

### Advanced Nodes (3nm/5nm)
- **FY2025**: **$1.97T TWD (~$63B USD, 52% of revenue)**.
- **3nm**: **$794.3B TWD (~$25.3B USD, 21% of revenue)**, up **~50% YoY** (FY2024–2025).
- **5nm**: **$1.18T TWD (~$37.7B USD, 31% of revenue)**, up **~20% YoY**.
- **Key drivers**:
  - **AI/HPC**: NVIDIA’s **Blackwell GPUs** and Apple’s **M-series chips** are 3nm/5nm-based.
  - **Pricing power**: ASPs for 3nm are **~$20,000/wafer** vs. **~$5,000/wafer for 28nm**.
- **Risks**:
  - **Yield challenges**: 3nm yields improved to **~80%** in FY2025 (per management), but defects remain a risk.
  - **Competition**: Samsung’s 3nm ramp (though delayed) could erode share.

### Legacy Nodes (28nm and above)
- **FY2025**: **$623.3B TWD (~$19.9B USD, 16% of revenue)**.
- **28nm**: **$225.1B TWD (~$7.2B USD, 6% of revenue)**, flat YoY.
- **Key drivers**:
  - **Automotive/IoT**: 28nm is the **workhorse for non-AI chips**.
  - **China demand**: TSMC’s **Nanjing fab** supplies local customers (e.g., MediaTek).
- **Risks**:
  - **Pricing pressure**: ASPs declining **~5% YoY** as customers migrate to 16nm.
  - **Overcapacity**: SMIC and UMC are expanding 28nm capacity.

## Growth Trajectory
- **Total revenue**:
  - **FY2023**: $69.5B USD (-4.5% YoY).
  - **FY2024**: $77.5B USD (+11.5% YoY).
  - **FY2025**: $121.4B USD (+56.6% YoY).
- **Structural vs. cyclical**:
  - **Structural**: AI/HPC demand is **secular**, with TSMC forecasting **20%+ CAGR through 2027** (per FY2025 MD&A).
  - **Cyclical**: Smartphone/PC demand remains tied to **consumer spending** and **inventory cycles**.
- **M&A**: No major deals, but **$32B capex in FY2025** (up **~40% YoY**) to expand 3nm/2nm capacity.

---

## Margin Profile
- **Gross margin**:
  - **FY2023**: 54.4%.
  - **FY2024**: 56.1%.
  - **FY2025**: **59.9%** (highest in 5 years).
- **Operating margin**:
  - **FY2023**: 42.9%.
  - **FY2024**: 45.7%.
  - **FY2025**: **50.8%**.
- **Segment margins**:
  - **Advanced nodes (3nm/5nm)**: **~65% gross margin** (per management estimates).
  - **Legacy nodes (28nm+)**: **~35–40% gross margin**.
- **Operating leverage**:
  - **R&D intensity**: **6.5% of revenue** in FY2025 (up from 6.0% in FY2023), but **operating margins expanded** due to **higher ASPs** and **utilization rates** (FY2025: **~90%** vs. **~80% in FY2023**).

## Capital Allocation & Strategy
- **Priorities** (per FY2025 MD&A):
  1. **Capex**: **$32B in FY2025** (up **40% YoY**) to expand **3nm/2nm capacity** (Arizona, Japan, Taiwan).
  2. **R&D**: **$7.9B in FY2025** (6.5% of revenue) to develop **2nm/1.4nm nodes** and **packaging technologies** (e.g., SoIC).
  3. **Dividends**: **$3.3B USD in FY2025** (3% yield), with **payout ratio ~50%**.
  4. **M&A**: No large deals, but **minor investments** in packaging (e.g., **ASE JV**) and materials (e.g., **TSMC Materials**).
- **3-year outlook**:
  - **Revenue CAGR**: **15–20%** (driven by AI/HPC).
  - **Gross margin target**: **53–58%** (FY2025–2027).
  - **Capex**: **$30B+ annually** to maintain node leadership.

## Key Risks
1. **Geopolitical**: U.S.-China tensions could disrupt **supply chains** (e.g., TSMC’s Arizona fab delays) or **customer demand** (e.g., Huawei restrictions). Management warned: *"Prolonged trade barriers may negatively impact our U.S. operations and global expansion."*
2. **Customer concentration**: Top 5 customers (led by Apple) account for **~60% of revenue**. A slowdown in iPhone demand or NVIDIA’s AI capex could hit earnings.
3. **Technological disruption**: Intel’s **IDM 2.0** and Samsung’s **3nm ramp** could erode TSMC’s **node leadership** (currently **2–3 years ahead**).
4. **Capex intensity**: **$32B in FY2025 capex** (26% of revenue) risks **overcapacity** if AI demand slows.
5. **FX exposure**: **NTD appreciation** (vs. USD) could pressure margins, as **~60% of revenue is USD-denominated** but costs are in NTD. FY2025 saw **$1.9B USD FX loss** (vs. $10B USD gain in FY2024).
