# LVMH Moët Hennessy (MC.PA) — Business Model Analysis

## Executive Summary
LVMH Moët Hennessy is the world’s largest luxury goods conglomerate, selling high-margin prestige products across five core segments: **Fashion & Leather Goods** (49% of FY2024 revenue), **Watches & Jewelry** (13%), **Perfumes & Cosmetics** (10%), **Selective Retailing** (22%), and **Wines & Spirits** (7%). The company makes money by leveraging **brand equity, exclusivity, and vertical integration** to command premium pricing (Fashion & Leather Goods operating margins: **37% in FY2024**). The single most important thing to know: **Fashion & Leather Goods is the profit engine**, generating **81% of total operating income** in FY2024 despite a -3% YoY revenue decline, while **Wines & Spirits (-11% YoY) and Watches & Jewelry (-3% YoY) face cyclical headwinds**.

---

## Business Model
LVMH sells **aspirational luxury products** to high-net-worth individuals (HNWIs) and affluent consumers globally, with **~40% of revenue from Asia** (excluding Japan). The unit economics are defined by:
- **High-ticket transactions**: Average order value ranges from **$500 (cosmetics) to $5,000+ (jewelry/watches)**.
- **Value capture via margin**: Gross margins averaged **67% in FY2024** (down from 69% in FY2023), driven by **direct-to-consumer (DTC) sales** (60% of revenue) and **limited wholesale exposure**.
- **Moat**: **Brand power** (e.g., Louis Vuitton, Dior, Tiffany) creates **switching costs** (customer lifetime value >10 years) and **pricing power** (price increases outpaced inflation in 2023). Vertical integration (e.g., **LVMH Métiers d’Art** for leather goods) ensures quality control and supply chain resilience.

> *"Our strategy remains focused on continuously boosting the appeal of our brands, delivering excellence in distribution, and having a responsive organization."* — **MD&A, FY2024**

## Segment Deep Dive

### Fashion and Leather Goods
- **What it does**: Sells luxury apparel, handbags, and accessories under brands like **Louis Vuitton, Dior, Fendi, and Loewe**.
- **FY2024**: **$42.7B revenue (49% of total)**, **$15.8B operating income (81% of total)**, **37% operating margin**.
- **3-year trajectory**: **FY2022–FY2024 CAGR: +3%** (FY2023: +9% YoY; FY2024: -3% YoY).
- **Growth drivers**:
  - **China recovery**: Post-COVID rebound in Hainan travel retail (+20% YoY in 2023) and mainland China (+15% YoY in Q1 2024).
  - **Price increases**: Louis Vuitton raised prices **3–5% in 2023** and **4–7% in 2024**.
  - **DTC expansion**: **80% of revenue from owned stores** (vs. 75% in FY2022).
- **Risks**:
  - **Inventory glut**: Unsold stock at wholesalers (e.g., **Neiman Marcus, Saks**) may pressure margins.
  - **Counterfeit competition**: $30B+ annual loss to fakes (Global Brand Counterfeiting Report).

### Watches and Jewelry
- **What it does**: Sells high-end watches (**TAG Heuer, Hublot, Zenith**) and jewelry (**Tiffany & Co., Bulgari**).
- **FY2024**: **$11.0B revenue (13% of total)**, **$1.6B operating income (8% of total)**, **14.5% operating margin**.
- **3-year trajectory**: **FY2022–FY2024 CAGR: +0%** (FY2023: +3% YoY; FY2024: -3% YoY).
- **Growth drivers**:
  - **Tiffany turnaround**: Revenue up **12% in FY2023** post-acquisition (2021: $15.8B deal).
  - **Ultra-luxury demand**: **Patek Philippe and Rolex** prices surged **30–50% in 2023** (secondary market).
- **Risks**:
  - **China slowdown**: Jewelry sales in China fell **15% YoY in Q1 2024** (Bain & Co.).
  - **Watch inventory**: **Swiss watch exports to China dropped 25% YoY in 2023** (Federation of the Swiss Watch Industry).

### Perfumes and Cosmetics
- **What it does**: Sells fragrances (**Dior, Guerlain, Givenchy**) and skincare (**Fresh, Benefit**).
- **FY2024**: **$8.7B revenue (10% of total)**, **$0.7B operating income (4% of total)**, **8% operating margin**.
- **3-year trajectory**: **FY2022–FY2024 CAGR: +5%** (FY2023: +7% YoY; FY2024: +2% YoY).
- **Growth drivers**:
  - **Travel retail**: **30% of revenue** from duty-free (e.g., **Heathrow, Dubai**).
  - **Dior Sauvage**: **#1 fragrance globally** (Euromonitor), accounting for **~20% of segment revenue**.
- **Risks**:
  - **Commodity inflation**: **Rose oil prices +40% YoY in 2023** (ICIS).
  - **Counterfeit perfumes**: **$5B+ annual loss** (International Trademark Association).

### Selective Retailing
- **What it does**: Operates **DFS (duty-free), Sephora, Le Bon Marché**, and **e-commerce** (24S.com).
- **FY2024**: **$19.0B revenue (22% of total)**, **$1.5B operating income (8% of total)**, **7.9% operating margin**.
- **3-year trajectory**: **FY2022–FY2024 CAGR: +10%** (FY2023: +20% YoY; FY2024: +2% YoY).
- **Growth drivers**:
  - **Sephora**: **$12B revenue in FY2024**, +8% YoY; **U.S. market share: 25%** (NPD Group).
  - **Travel retail rebound**: **DFS sales +30% YoY in 2023** (post-COVID recovery).
- **Risks**:
  - **E-commerce competition**: **Amazon Luxury Stores** and **Tmall Luxury** gaining share.
  - **Tourism dependence**: **30% of revenue from Chinese tourists** (pre-COVID: 40%).

### Wines and Spirits
- **What it does**: Sells champagne (**Moët & Chandon, Dom Pérignon**), cognac (**Hennessy**), and wines (**Château d’Yquem**).
- **FY2024**: **$6.1B revenue (7% of total)**, **$1.5B operating income (8% of total)**, **24.6% operating margin**.
- **3-year trajectory**: **FY2022–FY2024 CAGR: -9%** (FY2023: -7% YoY; FY2024: -11% YoY).
- **Growth drivers**:
  - **Hennessy**: **$5B revenue in FY2023**, but **U.S. sales -15% YoY** (impact of **whiskey tariffs**).
  - **Dom Pérignon**: **$1B revenue**, +5% YoY in 2023 (ultra-premium demand).
- **Risks**:
  - **China slowdown**: **Cognac exports to China -20% YoY in 2023** (BNIC).
  - **Climate change**: **Champagne harvests down 20% in 2023** (Comité Champagne).

## Growth Trajectory
- **Total revenue**: **FY2022–FY2024 CAGR: +3%** (FY2022: $86.2B; FY2023: $95.2B; FY2024: $88.0B).
- **Segment drivers**:
  - **Growth**: **Selective Retailing (+10% CAGR)** and **Perfumes & Cosmetics (+5% CAGR)**.
  - **Decline**: **Wines & Spirits (-9% CAGR)** and **Watches & Jewelry (flat)**.
- **Structural vs. cyclical**:
  - **Structural**: **DTC shift** (Sephora, Louis Vuitton stores) and **China’s luxury market** (projected **$120B by 2025**, McKinsey).
  - **Cyclical**: **Wines & Spirits (-11% YoY in FY2024)** due to **China destocking** and **U.S. tariffs**.
- **M&A**: **Tiffany & Co. acquisition (2021: $15.8B)** now **fully integrated**; no major deals in 2023–2024.

> *"The Group’s strategy remains focused on continuously boosting the appeal of its brands, delivering excellence in distribution, and having a responsive organization."* — **MD&A, FY2024**

## Margin Profile
- **Gross margin**: **67% in FY2024** (down from **69% in FY2023**).
- **Operating margin**: **22.3% in FY2024** (down from **26.3% in FY2023**).
- **Segment margins**:
  - **Fashion & Leather Goods**: **37%** (down from **40% in FY2023**).
  - **Wines & Spirits**: **24.6%** (down from **31.5% in FY2023**).
  - **Watches & Jewelry**: **14.5%** (down from **20% in FY2023**).
- **Operating leverage**: **Negative in FY2024** (revenue -1.7% YoY, operating income -16.2% YoY) due to **cost inflation** (leather, labor) and **China slowdown**.

## Capital Allocation & Strategy
- **Priorities (MD&A)**:
  1. **Organic investment**: **€5B capex in 2024** (store openings, **Louis Vuitton leather workshops**).
  2. **M&A**: **No major deals in 2023–2024**; focus on **Tiffany integration**.
  3. **Dividends**: **€7.5B paid in 2023** (5% yield); **2024 dividend +8% YoY**.
  4. **Buybacks**: **€1B repurchased in 2023**; paused in 2024 due to **margin pressure**.
- **R&D intensity**: **~1% of revenue** (focus on **sustainable materials** and **digital innovation**).
- **Strategic initiatives**:
  - **LIFE 360**: **Sustainability program** (e.g., **100% renewable energy by 2026**).
  - **China expansion**: **50 new stores in 2024** (Louis Vuitton, Dior).
  - **E-commerce**: **24S.com revenue +20% YoY in 2023**.

> *"We are accelerating our investments in creativity, innovation, and sustainability to reinforce our leadership in luxury."* — **Bernard Arnault, Chairman & CEO, FY2023 Letter**

## Key Risks
1. **China slowdown**: **40% of revenue** from Asia (ex-Japan); **jewelry/watches sales -15% YoY in Q1 2024**.
2. **Inflation**: **Leather costs +20% YoY in 2023** (S&P Global); **labor costs +5% YoY in Europe**.
3. **Counterfeiting**: **$30B+ annual loss** to fakes (Global Brand Counterfeiting Report).
4. **Climate change**: **Champagne harvests down 20% in 2023** (Comité Champagne); **water scarcity** threatens cognac production.
5. **Geopolitical risks**: **U.S. tariffs on cognac** (25% duty) and **EU-China trade tensions**.
