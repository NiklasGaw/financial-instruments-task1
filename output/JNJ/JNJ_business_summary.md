# Johnson & Johnson (JNJ) — Business Model Analysis

## Executive Summary
Johnson & Johnson (JNJ) is a global healthcare conglomerate operating in two primary segments: **Innovative Medicine** (pharmaceuticals) and **MedTech** (medical devices). The company generates revenue through high-margin prescription drugs and medical devices, with **Innovative Medicine contributing ~64% of FY2025 revenue ($60.4B) and MedTech ~36% ($33.8B)**. The most critical near-term dynamic is the **STELARA biosimilar erosion**, which dragged Immunology sales down **11.8% YoY in FY2025**, offset by **22.1% growth in Oncology** (led by DARZALEX and CARVYKTI). JNJ’s moat lies in **patent-protected blockbusters, R&D scale, and global distribution**, but faces **biosimilar competition, Medicare Part D redesign pressures, and MedTech pricing headwinds in China**.

---

## Business Model
### **Products & Customers**
- **Innovative Medicine**: Sells **patent-protected prescription drugs** to hospitals, pharmacies, and payors (e.g., Medicare, private insurers). Key therapeutic areas include **Oncology (DARZALEX, CARVYKTI), Immunology (STELARA, TREMFYA), Neuroscience (SPRAVATO), and Pulmonary Hypertension (OPSUMIT)**.
- **MedTech**: Sells **medical devices and surgical tools** to hospitals and surgeons, including **orthopaedics (hips/knees), cardiovascular devices (Abiomed, Shockwave), and vision products (ACUVUE contact lenses)**.

### **Unit Economics**
- **Innovative Medicine**: High-margin **recurring revenue** from chronic therapies (e.g., DARZALEX for multiple myeloma). Gross margins ~75-80% (FY2025: **$63.9B gross profit on $94.2B revenue**).
- **MedTech**: Lower-margin **capital equipment + consumables** model (e.g., surgical robots + single-use tools). Gross margins ~60-65%.
- **Pricing Power**: Innovative Medicine commands **premium pricing** (e.g., DARZALEX at ~$150K/year), while MedTech faces **procedure-based pricing pressure** (e.g., China volume-based procurement).

### **Value Capture**
- **Innovative Medicine**: **Patent exclusivity** (e.g., DARZALEX until 2030) and **switching costs** (oncology patients rarely change therapies).
- **MedTech**: **Regulatory barriers** (FDA approvals) and **surgeon training** (e.g., VELYS robotic knee system).
- **Moat**: **R&D scale ($14.7B spent in FY2025)**, **global distribution (3 wholesalers = 48.4% of revenue)**, and **brand trust** (e.g., ACUVUE, Tylenol).

## Segment Deep Dive

### **Innovative Medicine**
- **What it does**: Develops and sells **branded pharmaceuticals** across Oncology, Immunology, Neuroscience, and Infectious Diseases.
- **FY2025 Revenue**: **$60.4B (64% of total)**, +6.0% YoY.
- **3-Year CAGR (FY2023-2025)**: **5.0%** (dragged by STELARA biosimilars).
- **Operating Income**: **$22.3B (36.9% margin)**.
- **Key Growth Drivers**:
  - **Oncology**: +22.1% YoY (FY2025), driven by **DARZALEX (+23%) and CARVYKTI (+96%)**. Management: *"DARZALEX continued share gains and market growth"* (FY2025 MD&A).
  - **Neuroscience**: +10.1% YoY, led by **SPRAVATO (+57%)** for treatment-resistant depression.
  - **Pulmonary Hypertension**: +3.6% YoY (OPSUMIT/UPTRAVI).
- **Key Risks**:
  - **STELARA biosimilars**: -41.3% YoY (FY2025), eroding **$4.3B in sales**. Management: *"Expects continued launches of biosimilars globally"* (FY2025 MD&A).
  - **Medicare Part D redesign**: Pressured **IMBRUVICA (-7.1%) and XARELTO (+11%)**.
  - **SIMPONI biosimilars**: At least two competitors pursuing FDA approval.

### **MedTech**
- **What it does**: Sells **medical devices** (orthopaedics, cardiovascular, surgery, vision).
- **FY2025 Revenue**: **$33.8B (36% of total)**, +6.1% YoY.
- **3-Year CAGR (FY2023-2025)**: **6.8%**.
- **Operating Income**: **$4.1B (12.2% margin)**.
- **Key Growth Drivers**:
  - **Cardiovascular**: +15.8% YoY (FY2025), led by **Abiomed (+17%) and Shockwave (+103%)**. Management: *"Shockwave growth driven by Coronary and Peripheral portfolios"* (FY2025 MD&A).
  - **Vision**: +6.3% YoY, driven by **ACUVUE OASYS 1-Day lenses**.
  - **Surgery**: +3.0% YoY (Advanced Surgery +2.0%).
- **Key Risks**:
  - **China volume-based procurement**: Negatively impacted **orthopaedics (-2.5% in Spine/Sports)**.
  - **Orthopaedics restructuring**: Announced separation of the business (target: 18-24 months).
  - **OPSUMIT generic competition**: Expected in **2026**, per management.

---

## Growth Trajectory
- **Total Revenue 3-Year CAGR (FY2023-2025)**: **4.3%** ($85.2B → $94.2B).
- **Drivers**:
  - **Structural**: Oncology growth (+22.1% YoY), MedTech acquisitions (Shockwave, Abiomed).
  - **Cyclical**: STELARA biosimilar erosion (-$4.3B YoY), Medicare Part D redesign.
- **M&A**: **Shockwave ($13.1B, May 2024)** and **Intra-Cellular ($1.9B, April 2025)** added **$1.1B in revenue** (FY2025).
- **Divestitures**: **Consumer Health (Kenvue) spun off in 2023**.

---

## Margin Profile
- **Gross Margin**: **67.8% (FY2025)** vs. **69.1% (FY2024)**. Decline due to **STELARA mix shift** and **MedTech tariffs**.
- **Operating Margin**: **42.6% (FY2025)** vs. **41.3% (FY2024)**. **Innovative Medicine (36.9%)** carries the franchise.
- **Operating Leverage**: **SG&A declined to 25.2% of revenue (FY2025) from 25.8% (FY2024)**, driven by **corporate cost rationalization**.

---

## Capital Allocation & Strategy
- **Priorities (MD&A)**:
  1. **R&D**: **$14.7B (15.6% of revenue)** in FY2025. Focus on **Oncology (bispecifics) and Neuroscience (CAPLYTA)**.
  2. **M&A**: **Shockwave, Intra-Cellular, Halda Therapeutics** to bolster MedTech and Innovative Medicine.
  3. **Dividends**: **61-year streak of increases**; **$12.7B paid in FY2025 (47% of net income)**.
  4. **Buybacks**: **$5B authorized in FY2025** (minimal execution).
- **Capex**: **$3.5B (FY2025)**, focused on **manufacturing capacity (CARVYKTI)** and **digital surgery tools (VELYS)**.
- **Outlook**: Management targets **mid-single-digit revenue growth** and **margin expansion** via **R&D leverage and MedTech restructuring**.

---

## Key Risks
1. **Biosimilar Erosion**: STELARA (-41.3% YoY) and **upcoming SIMPONI biosimilars** could pressure Immunology.
2. **Medicare Part D Redesign**: **IMBRUVICA (-7.1%) and XARELTO (+11%)** impacted by **catastrophic coverage phase changes**.
3. **China Pricing Pressure**: **Orthopaedics (-2.5% in Spine/Sports)** due to **volume-based procurement**.
4. **Litigation**: **$7B talc reserve reversal in FY2025**, but **Auris shareholder litigation ($0.8B charge)** remains.
5. **Orthopaedics Separation**: **Execution risk** in spinning off the business (target: 18-24 months).
