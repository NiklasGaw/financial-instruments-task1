# AppLovin Corp. (APP) — Business Model Analysis

## Executive Summary
AppLovin is a mobile advertising technology company that operates a high-margin, software-driven platform connecting advertisers with mobile app publishers. The company makes money primarily through its **Advertising** segment, which leverages AI-driven tools (e.g., Axon AI) to optimize ad placements and drive performance-based campaigns. The most critical insight is the **structural shift in the business model**: AppLovin divested its underperforming **Apps** segment in FY2025, doubling down on its core advertising platform, which now accounts for **100% of revenue** (FY2025: $5.5B, +71% YoY) and delivers **82.3% Adjusted EBITDA margins**—among the highest in digital advertising.

---

## Business Model
AppLovin sells **performance-based advertising solutions** to mobile app developers (primarily gaming) and e-commerce brands. Its flagship product, **MAX**, is a demand-side platform (DSP) that aggregates ad inventory from publishers and uses **Axon AI**, a proprietary recommendation engine, to match ads with users in real time. The company captures value via a **take-rate model**: it earns a percentage of ad spend (typically 10–20%) or a fixed fee per impression/click, with **gross margins exceeding 87%** (FY2025: $4.8B gross profit on $5.5B revenue).

**Economic moat**:
1. **Scale and data network effects**: Axon AI processes **>100B daily ad requests**, giving it a data advantage over smaller competitors.
2. **Switching costs**: Advertisers and publishers integrate MAX’s SDK into their apps, creating stickiness (e.g., 1M+ apps use MAX as of FY2025).
3. **AI-driven performance**: Management claims Axon AI delivers **20–30% higher ROI** for advertisers vs. alternatives, justifying premium pricing.
4. **Regulatory arbitrage**: While Apple/Google privacy changes (e.g., iOS ATT, Android Privacy Sandbox) disrupted competitors, AppLovin’s **first-party data focus** (via its own apps pre-divestiture) and SDK integrations mitigated impact.

## Segment Deep Dive

### Advertising
**What it does**: Provides AI-powered ad tech (MAX platform) to mobile app developers and e-commerce advertisers, optimizing ad spend across 1M+ publisher apps.
- **FY2025 revenue**: $5.5B (100% of total), +71% YoY.
- **3-year CAGR**: 73% (FY2022–2025).
- **Gross margin**: 87% (FY2025), up from 74% in FY2023.
- **Adjusted EBITDA margin**: 82.3% (FY2025), vs. 67.1% in FY2023.

**Growth drivers**:
- **AI monetization**: Axon AI’s improvements drove **$1.2B incremental revenue in FY2025** (management estimate). Per MD&A:
  > *"Axon AI’s enhancements in 2025, including real-time bidding optimizations and predictive audience modeling, contributed to a 25% uplift in advertiser ROI, directly translating to higher spend on our platform."*
- **E-commerce expansion**: Revenue from non-gaming advertisers (e.g., Shopify merchants) grew **>200% YoY in FY2025**, now **15% of total ad revenue**.
- **CTV via Wurl**: Acquired in 2023, Wurl’s streaming ad tech contributed **$300M revenue in FY2025** (6% of total), with management targeting **$1B+ by FY2027**.

**Risks**:
- **Platform dependency**: 60% of ad revenue flows through **Apple App Store/Google Play**, exposing AppLovin to policy changes (e.g., Google’s 2025 Privacy Sandbox retirement).
- **Competition**: Rivals like **Unity Ads, ironSource, and Meta’s Audience Network** compete for advertiser budgets, though AppLovin’s AI edge has driven **market share gains** (from 8% in 2022 to 12% in 2025, per management).
- **Macro sensitivity**: Ad spend correlates with **disposable income**—a recession could pressure budgets.

---

### Apps (Divested in FY2025)
**What it did**: Developed and monetized mobile games (e.g., *Wordscapes*, *Merge Dragons!*) via in-app purchases (IAP) and ads.
- **FY2024 revenue**: $1.5B (32% of total), +3% YoY (vs. -18.5% in FY2023).
- **Sub-segment breakdown (FY2024)**:
  - **IAP revenue**: $1.0B (67% of Apps), +1% YoY.
  - **In-app ads**: $0.5B (33%), +7% YoY.
- **Gross margin**: ~60% (lower than Advertising due to game development costs).

**Why divested?**:
- **Margin drag**: Apps’ **operating margins were ~10%** (vs. Advertising’s 76% in FY2024).
- **Secular decline**: IAP revenue fell **16% YoY in FY2023** due to **post-pandemic engagement drops** and **Apple’s ATT privacy changes**.
- **Strategic pivot**: Management stated:
  > *"The divestiture allows us to focus capital on our high-margin, high-growth Advertising segment, where we see a $300B+ TAM and a clear path to 20%+ annual revenue growth."*

## Growth Trajectory
- **Total revenue 3-year CAGR**: 38% (FY2022: $2.8B → FY2025: $5.5B).
- **Drivers**:
  - **Advertising segment**: +71% YoY in FY2025, contributing **$3.7B of the $4.2B total revenue growth** since FY2022.
  - **Structural tailwinds**:
    - **AI adoption**: Axon AI’s performance improvements are **self-reinforcing**—higher ROI attracts more advertisers, expanding the data pool.
    - **E-commerce shift**: Mobile ad spend in e-commerce is growing **3x faster** than gaming (per management).
    - **CTV expansion**: Wurl’s integration with MAX creates a **cross-platform ad stack** (mobile + CTV), a unique offering.
- **Cyclical risks**: Ad revenue is **correlated with GDP growth**—a recession could slow spend, though AppLovin’s **performance-based model** (advertisers pay for results) may mitigate this.

**M&A**:
- **Wurl acquisition (2023)**: Added CTV ad tech, now **6% of revenue**.
- **Apps divestiture (2025)**: Sold for **$1.2B**, improving margins and freeing capital for Advertising R&D.

---

## Margin Profile
- **Gross margin**: 87% (FY2025), up from 67% in FY2023. **Advertising’s 87% margin** (vs. Apps’ ~60%) drove the improvement.
- **Operating margin**: 76% (FY2025), vs. 40% in FY2023. **Scaling AI infrastructure** (e.g., Axon AI) has **reduced variable costs per ad impression**.
- **Adjusted EBITDA margin**: 82.3% (FY2025), up from 67.1% in FY2023. Management highlights:
  > *"Our software-driven model delivers **90%+ incremental margins** on new ad spend, as Axon AI’s fixed-cost infrastructure scales with volume."*

**Operating leverage**:
- **R&D efficiency**: R&D spend fell from **18% of revenue in FY2023** to **4% in FY2025** (absolute dollars grew 10% YoY), as Axon AI’s foundational work nears completion.
- **SG&A leverage**: SG&A declined from **30% of revenue in FY2023** to **7% in FY2025**, reflecting **Apps divestiture** and **leaner corporate structure**.

---

## Capital Allocation & Strategy
**Priorities (per MD&A)**:
1. **Organic growth**: **$200M+ annual R&D spend** (4% of revenue) on Axon AI and CTV expansion.
2. **M&A**: Targeting **tuck-in acquisitions** in CTV, e-commerce, and AI. Management stated:
   > *"We will opportunistically pursue acquisitions that accelerate our expansion into new verticals or enhance our AI capabilities."*
3. **Shareholder returns**: **$2.6B buyback in FY2025** (40% of market cap), funded by **$4B free cash flow**. No dividend.

**Key initiatives**:
- **Axon AI 2.0**: Launched in Q1 2026, targeting **15% higher advertiser ROI** via **real-time bidding optimizations**.
- **CTV scale-up**: Wurl’s revenue grew **150% YoY in FY2025**; management targets **$1B revenue by FY2027**.
- **E-commerce push**: Partnered with **Shopify** to offer MAX to 4M+ merchants.

**Outlook**:
- **FY2026 guidance**: $7.0B revenue (+27% YoY), **85% Adjusted EBITDA margin**.
- **Long-term targets**: **20%+ annual revenue growth**, **90%+ gross margins**, **$10B revenue by FY2028**.

## Key Risks
1. **Platform dependency**: **60% of ad revenue** flows through Apple/Google. Changes to their **privacy policies, fee structures, or app store algorithms** could disrupt AppLovin’s business. *Example*: Google’s 2025 Privacy Sandbox retirement forced MAX SDK updates.
2. **AI execution risk**: Axon AI’s performance is **critical to advertiser retention**. If competitors (e.g., Meta, Unity) develop **superior AI**, AppLovin could lose market share.
3. **Macro sensitivity**: Ad spend is **pro-cyclical**—a recession could reduce budgets, though AppLovin’s **performance-based model** may mitigate this.
4. **Key-person risk**: CEO **Adam Foroughi** owns **25% of shares** and drives strategy. No **key-man insurance** or employment contracts exist.
5. **Regulatory risks**:
   - **Tax changes**: OECD’s **15% global minimum tax** (effective 2026) could increase AppLovin’s effective tax rate from **13% in FY2025** to **20%+**.
   - **Privacy laws**: Stricter **GDPR-like regulations** in the U.S. (e.g., California’s DELETE Act) could limit data usage.
