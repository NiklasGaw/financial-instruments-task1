# Booking Holdings Inc. (BKNG) — Business Model Analysis

## Executive Summary
Booking Holdings Inc. (BKNG) is the world’s largest online travel agency (OTA), operating five primary brands—Booking.com, Priceline, Agoda, KAYAK, and OpenTable—that connect travelers with accommodations, flights, car rentals, and restaurant reservations. The company generates revenue primarily through **merchant transactions** (70% of gross bookings in FY2025), where it acts as the principal and earns revenue on the spread between consumer payments and supplier payouts, and **agency transactions** (30%), where it earns commissions. The **merchant segment is the heavy lifter**, driving 66% of FY2025 revenue ($17.8B, +25.5% YoY) and benefiting from secular shifts toward direct booking and payment facilitation. The most critical insight: **BKNG’s operating margins are under pressure** from rising merchant-related costs (payment processing, fraud, and personnel) and a strategic pivot toward lower-margin alternative accommodations (36% of Booking.com’s room nights in FY2025), despite strong revenue growth.

---

## Business Model
Booking Holdings operates a **two-sided marketplace** connecting travelers with travel service providers (hotels, alternative accommodations, airlines, restaurants) and advertisers. The company monetizes through:

1. **Merchant Model (66% of FY2025 revenue, $17.8B)**:
   - BKNG acts as the principal, purchasing inventory (e.g., hotel rooms) and reselling it to consumers at a markup. Revenue is recognized on a **gross basis** (full consumer payment), with costs (supplier payouts, payment processing, fraud) expensed separately.
   - **Unit economics**: Average take rate of ~15–20% (implied by gross bookings of ~$150B in FY2025 and merchant revenue of $17.8B). Merchant transactions carry **higher revenue per booking** but **lower margins** due to incremental costs (e.g., payment card rebates, chargebacks).
   - **Key value capture**: Payment facilitation (e.g., "Book Now, Pay Later" options) and dynamic pricing tools for suppliers.

2. **Agency Model (30% of FY2025 revenue, $8.0B)**:
   - BKNG acts as an intermediary, earning **commission revenue** (typically 10–20% of booking value) when a traveler completes a stay. Revenue is recognized **net of supplier payouts** (i.e., only the commission is recorded).
   - **Unit economics**: Lower revenue per booking but **higher margins** (no payment/fraud costs). Agency revenue declined 6.5% YoY in FY2025 due to the shift toward merchant bookings.

3. **Advertising & Other (4% of FY2025 revenue, $1.2B)**:
   - Includes **KAYAK’s meta-search advertising** (e.g., cost-per-click fees from OTAs/airlines) and **OpenTable’s restaurant reservation/subscription fees**.
   - **Unit economics**: High-margin (advertising) but low-scale (OpenTable’s revenue is <$500M annually).

### Economic Moat
- **Network Effects**: Booking.com’s 4.4M properties (FY2025) create a **virtuous cycle**—more suppliers attract more travelers, which attracts more suppliers. This scale deters new entrants.
- **Brand & Switching Costs**: Booking.com is the #1 most downloaded OTA app globally (mid-50% of room nights booked via mobile in FY2025). Direct bookers (mid-50% of room nights) exhibit **higher repeat rates**, reducing reliance on paid marketing.
- **Scale Advantages**: BKNG’s $8.2B in marketing spend (FY2025) dwarfs competitors (e.g., Expedia spent ~$6B). This scale enables **preferred placement** on Google and meta-search platforms.
- **AI & Personalization**: The "Connected Trip" strategy leverages AI to **cross-sell ancillary services** (flights, cars, restaurants), increasing wallet share per traveler.

## Segment Deep Dive

### Merchant Revenues
- **What it does**: BKNG purchases inventory (hotels, alternative accommodations) and resells it to consumers, earning revenue on the spread. Includes payment facilitation fees (e.g., card rebates).
- **FY2025**: $17.8B (66% of total revenue), +25.5% YoY.
- **3-year trajectory**: FY2023–FY2025 CAGR of **28%**, driven by:
  - Shift from agency to merchant bookings (70% of gross bookings in FY2025 vs. 63% in FY2024).
  - Expansion of alternative accommodations (36% of Booking.com’s room nights in FY2025, up from 35% in FY2024).
- **Key growth drivers (MD&A)**:
  - *"We continue to expand our merchant service offerings as part of a broader strategy to provide more payment options to travelers and travel service providers... The mix of our total gross bookings generated on a merchant basis across the company was 70% in 2025, an increase from 63% in 2024."*
  - *"The incremental revenues from facilitating payments were greater than the associated incremental variable expenses [in 2025]."*
- **Headwinds/Risks**:
  - **Margin compression**: Merchant transactions incur **higher costs** (payment processing, fraud, personnel), which are expensed in "Sales and other expenses." FY2025 operating expenses grew 12.4% YoY to $18.1B, outpacing revenue growth (13.4% YoY).
  - **Regional mix shift**: Growth in Asia (lower ADRs) negatively impacts revenue per booking. *"Our global ADRs on a constant currency basis were about in line with the prior year... slightly negatively impacted by a higher mix of room nights in Asia."*
  - **Fraud/chargeback risk**: Merchant transactions expose BKNG to **payment fraud**, which management highlights as a growing cost.

---

### Agency Revenues
- **What it does**: BKNG earns commissions (net revenue) when travelers book through its platforms, with suppliers handling payments and fulfillment.
- **FY2025**: $8.0B (30% of total revenue), **-6.5% YoY**.
- **3-year trajectory**: FY2023–FY2025 CAGR of **-7%**, reflecting the **structural decline** as Booking.com shifts to merchant bookings.
- **Key growth drivers (MD&A)**:
  - *"The mix of our total gross bookings generated on a merchant basis... was 70% in 2025, an increase from 63% in 2024 due to the ongoing shift from agency to merchant bookings at Booking.com."*
- **Headwinds/Risks**:
  - **Secular decline**: Agency revenue is **shrinking as a % of total revenue** (39% in FY2023 → 30% in FY2025). This trend is **irreversible** without a strategic pivot.
  - **Lower margins**: While agency transactions have **higher gross margins** than merchant, they lack the **upsell potential** (e.g., payment fees, ancillary services) of merchant bookings.

---

### Advertising and Other Revenues
- **What it does**: Includes **KAYAK’s meta-search advertising** (cost-per-click fees) and **OpenTable’s restaurant reservation/subscription fees**.
- **FY2025**: $1.2B (4% of total revenue), +11.3% YoY.
- **3-year trajectory**: FY2023–FY2025 CAGR of **10%**, driven by:
  - KAYAK’s recovery post-pandemic (though FY2025 saw a **$180M goodwill impairment** due to rising customer acquisition costs).
  - OpenTable’s growth in **subscription fees** (higher-margin than reservation commissions).
- **Key growth drivers (MD&A)**:
  - *"Advertising and other revenues... include KAYAK referrals and advertising, and OpenTable restaurant reservation services and subscription fees."*
- **Headwinds/Risks**:
  - **KAYAK impairment**: FY2025’s $180M goodwill impairment and $277M intangible asset impairment reflect **declining meta-search economics** due to higher customer acquisition costs.
  - **Low scale**: Advertising/other revenue is **<5% of total revenue**, limiting its impact on consolidated margins.

## Growth Trajectory
- **Total revenue**: FY2023–FY2025 CAGR of **12%**, with FY2025 at $26.9B (+13.4% YoY).
- **Segment drivers**:
  - **Merchant revenue** (+28% CAGR) drove **80% of total revenue growth** over the period.
  - **Agency revenue** (-7% CAGR) was a **drag**, declining from 44% of revenue in FY2023 to 30% in FY2025.
  - **Advertising/other** (+10% CAGR) contributed modestly.
- **Structural vs. Cyclical**:
  - **Structural**: Shift to merchant bookings (70% of gross bookings in FY2025) and alternative accommodations (36% of room nights) is **long-term and secular**.
  - **Cyclical**: FY2025’s 8% room night growth was supported by **expanding booking windows** and lower cancellation rates, but macroeconomic risks (e.g., recession, geopolitical shocks) could dampen demand.
- **M&A**: No major acquisitions in the period. OpenTable and KAYAK were acquired in 2014–2016 and are now **marginal contributors** (advertising/other revenue is <5% of total).

---

## Margin Profile
- **Operating margin**: **32.7% in FY2025** (down from 33.2% in FY2024 and 35.5% in FY2023), reflecting:
  - **Merchant-related cost pressure**: Payment processing, fraud, and personnel expenses grew faster than merchant revenue.
  - **Mix shift to lower-margin services**: Alternative accommodations (36% of room nights) and mobile bookings (mid-50% of room nights) have **lower ADRs and shorter stays**, compressing revenue per booking.
- **Segment margins (implied)**:
  - **Merchant**: Lower margins due to **incremental costs** (payment processing, fraud). Management notes: *"To the extent more of our business is generated on a merchant basis, we incur a greater level of these merchant-related expenses, which negatively impacts our operating margins."*
  - **Agency**: Higher margins (no payment/fraud costs) but **declining scale**.
  - **Advertising/Other**: Highest margins (KAYAK’s meta-search is asset-light), but **small scale**.
- **Operating leverage**: **Negative**—operating expenses grew 12.4% YoY in FY2025 vs. 13.4% revenue growth. The **Transformation Program** ($550M annual run-rate savings by FY2026) aims to improve efficiency, but merchant-related costs are **structurally higher**.

---

## Capital Allocation & Strategy
- **Priorities (MD&A)**:
  1. **Organic investment**: *"We intend to continue to invest in marketing and promotion, technology, and personnel... to create an ideal AI-powered traveler experience [Connected Trip]."*
  2. **Cost optimization**: The **Transformation Program** (launched Q4 2024) targets **$550M in annual run-rate savings** by FY2026, with restructuring costs <1x savings.
  3. **Shareholder returns**: No dividend; **$6.5B in share buybacks** in FY2025 (vs. $5.0B in FY2024). Buybacks are **opportunistic** and funded by free cash flow.
- **R&D intensity**: Not disclosed, but **AI/ML investment** is a priority (e.g., Connected Trip, dynamic pricing).
- **Capex**: Minimal (OTAs are asset-light). Most investment is in **software development** (capitalized as intangible assets).
- **Outlook**: Management avoids formal guidance but emphasizes:
  - *"We continue to take a long-term view, staying focused on delivering value to our travelers and partners, maintaining disciplined cost management, and making strategic investments as appropriate."*

## Key Risks
1. **Margin Compression from Merchant Shift**:
   - The shift to merchant bookings (70% of gross bookings in FY2025) **increases revenue but lowers margins** due to payment processing, fraud, and personnel costs. *"To the extent more of our business is generated on a merchant basis, we incur a greater level of these merchant-related expenses, which negatively impacts our operating margins."*

2. **Dependence on Google and Meta-Search**:
   - **60% of marketing spend** goes to performance marketing (Google, meta-search, social media). *"Our performance marketing expenses... are primarily related to the use of online search engines (primarily Google)."* Changes in bidding algorithms or ad pricing could **erode ROI**.

3. **Regulatory Scrutiny in Europe**:
   - Booking.com is designated a **"Very Large Online Platform"** under the EU’s Digital Services Act, exposing it to **higher compliance costs** and potential **operational restrictions**. *"We are subject to rules and regulations that may not apply to our competitors."*

4. **Alternative Accommodations Margin Pressure**:
   - Alternative accommodations (36% of Booking.com’s room nights in FY2025) have **lower ADRs and higher customer service costs**, compressing margins. *"We may experience lower profit margins due to additional costs from offering alternative accommodations."*

5. **Macroeconomic Sensitivity**:
   - Travel demand is **cyclical** and vulnerable to recessions, geopolitical shocks, or FX volatility. *"While the geopolitical and macroeconomic environment can impact global travel demand, we believe our diversified global portfolio... helps us navigate a range of scenarios."*

6. **KAYAK Impairment and Meta-Search Decline**:
   - FY2025’s **$457M impairment** ($180M goodwill, $277M intangibles) reflects **rising customer acquisition costs** in meta-search. *"KAYAK’s meta-search business [is] impacted by expected increases in customer acquisition costs."*

---

## Conclusion
Booking Holdings is a **high-growth, high-margin OTA** with a **dominant global footprint**, but its business model is **evolving in ways that pressure margins**. The **merchant segment is the engine of growth** (66% of revenue, +25.5% YoY in FY2025), but it carries **higher costs** and **lower margins** than the legacy agency business. The **Connected Trip strategy** (AI-powered cross-selling) and **Transformation Program** ($550M in savings) are critical to offsetting these pressures, but execution risk remains. **Key watch items**:
- **Merchant segment margins**: Can BKNG improve payment/fraud efficiency?
- **Alternative accommodations mix**: Will growth continue to outpace hotels, and at what cost?
- **Marketing ROI**: Can BKNG reduce reliance on Google without sacrificing growth?
- **Regulatory landscape**: How will EU scrutiny impact Booking.com’s operations?

**Long-term thesis**: BKNG’s **network effects and scale** are unmatched, but the **shift to merchant bookings and alternative accommodations** is a **double-edged sword**—driving revenue growth but compressing margins. The stock is a **hold for growth investors** but requires close monitoring of **margin trends and regulatory risks**.
