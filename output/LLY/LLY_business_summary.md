# Eli Lilly and Company (LLY) — Business Model Analysis

## Executive Summary
Eli Lilly is a global pharmaceutical company that develops, manufactures, and commercializes innovative medicines across therapeutic areas, with a dominant position in **cardiometabolic health** (diabetes and obesity). The company makes money by selling patent-protected prescription drugs to patients via healthcare providers, insurers, and governments. **The single most important thing to know right now is that Lilly’s growth is being driven almost entirely by its GLP-1/GIP receptor agonists Mounjaro (tirzepatide) and Zepbound, which generated $36.5B in FY2025 (56% of total revenue) and grew 78% and 165% YoY, respectively.** This franchise is structurally advantaged by secular demand for obesity and diabetes treatments, but faces near-term pricing pressure from U.S. government negotiations and long-term competitive risks.

---

## Business Model
Lilly operates as a **high-margin, IP-driven pharmaceutical business** with a focus on chronic diseases. Its core products are **biologics and small-molecule drugs** sold primarily to:
- **Patients** (via prescriptions written by physicians)
- **Payers** (insurers, governments, and pharmacy benefit managers)
- **Distributors** (wholesalers and pharmacies)

**Unit economics:**
- **Typical transaction:** A patient fills a prescription for a 1–3 month supply of a drug (e.g., Zepbound at ~$1,000/month in the U.S.), with pricing varying by country and payer mix.
- **Value capture:** Lilly earns **gross margins of 83%** (FY2025) by selling patent-protected drugs at a premium to production costs. Revenue is recognized at the point of sale to distributors or payers, with rebates/discounts (e.g., to insurers) deducted from gross sales.

**Economic moat:**
1. **Intellectual property:** Lilly’s GLP-1 franchise (Mounjaro/Zepbound) is protected by patents until at least the **2030s**, with additional patents covering formulations and delivery devices.
2. **Regulatory exclusivity:** Zepbound’s obesity indication received a **Commissioner’s National Priority Voucher** from the FDA, expediting its review and granting 6 months of pediatric exclusivity.
3. **Switching costs:** Patients on GLP-1 drugs face high switching costs due to clinical inertia, insurance coverage, and the need for dose titration.
4. **Manufacturing scale:** Lilly is investing **$7.8B in capex (FY2025)** to expand production capacity for GLP-1 drugs, creating a supply-side moat.
5. **Brand and data:** Lilly’s long-term clinical trials (e.g., SURPASS for Mounjaro) provide robust efficacy/safety data that competitors struggle to match.

## Segment Deep Dive

### Cardiometabolic Health
**What it does:** Develops and sells drugs for diabetes, obesity, and related metabolic disorders. Includes GLP-1/GIP receptor agonists (Mounjaro, Zepbound), insulin products (Humalog, Basaglar), and SGLT2 inhibitors (Jardiance).

- **FY2025 revenue:** $48.2B (74% of total)
- **3-year CAGR (FY2022–FY2025):** 110% (from $11.2B to $48.2B)
- **Key drivers:**
  - **Mounjaro (tirzepatide):** $22.9B in FY2025 (+78% YoY). Approved for type 2 diabetes (2022) and obesity (2023). Management notes: *"Mounjaro’s strong demand was partially offset by lower realized prices in the U.S."*
  - **Zepbound (tirzepatide):** $13.5B in FY2025 (+165% YoY). Launched in late 2023 for obesity. **Medicare coverage begins July 2026**, which could unlock significant demand.
  - **Jardiance (empagliflozin):** $3.4B in FY2025 (+3% YoY). Faces **IRA price negotiations starting 2026**.
  - **Trulicity (dulaglutide):** $4.3B in FY2025 (-30% YoY). Declining due to **GLP-1 cannibalization** and IRA price negotiations (2028).
- **Headwinds:**
  - **Pricing pressure:** U.S. volume growth of 53% in FY2025 was offset by a **10% price decline** (MD&A: *"lower realized prices were primarily driven by Mounjaro and Zepbound"*).
  - **Counterfeit competition:** Unapproved compounded tirzepatide threatens patient safety and Lilly’s revenue. MD&A: *"We continue to see the production of counterfeit incretins."*
  - **Manufacturing constraints:** Lilly is racing to expand capacity to meet demand, with **$10B in potential penalties** if it fails to purchase contracted volumes.

### Oncology
**What it does:** Sells cancer therapies, including CDK4/6 inhibitors (Verzenio), EGFR inhibitors (Erbitux), and KRAS-targeted drugs (olomorasib).

- **FY2025 revenue:** $9.4B (14% of total)
- **3-year CAGR (FY2022–FY2025):** 32% (from $5.3B to $9.4B)
- **Key drivers:**
  - **Verzenio (abemaciclib):** $5.7B in FY2025 (+1% YoY). Growth slowed due to **market saturation** in breast cancer.
  - **Pipeline:** Olomorasib (KRAS G12C inhibitor) and sofetabart mipitecan (FRα ADC) are in **Phase 3 trials** with Breakthrough Therapy designations.
- **Headwinds:**
  - **IRA risk:** Verzenio is **selected for Medicare price negotiations starting 2028**.
  - **Competition:** Verzenio competes with Pfizer’s Ibrance and Novartis’ Kisqali in CDK4/6 inhibitors.

---

### Immunology
**What it does:** Develops drugs for autoimmune diseases, including IL-17 inhibitors (Taltz) and JAK inhibitors (Olumiant).

- **FY2025 revenue:** $5.2B (8% of total)
- **3-year CAGR (FY2022–FY2025):** 14% (from $3.5B to $5.2B)
- **Key drivers:**
  - **Taltz (ixekizumab):** $3.6B in FY2025 (+5% YoY). Growth is **stagnant** due to competition in psoriasis.
  - **Mirikizumab (Omvoh):** Approved for Crohn’s disease in 2025, but revenue is not yet material.
  - **Pipeline:** Lebrikizumab (atopic dermatitis) is in **Phase 3 trials**.
- **Headwinds:**
  - **Biosimilar competition:** Taltz faces **patent expirations in 2027–2029**.
  - **Pricing pressure:** Olumiant’s revenue declined due to **Medicare price negotiations**.

---

### Neuroscience
**What it does:** Focuses on Alzheimer’s disease (donanemab) and migraine (Emgality).

- **FY2025 revenue:** $1.4B (2% of total)
- **3-year CAGR (FY2022–FY2025):** -14% (from $2.2B to $1.4B)
- **Key drivers:**
  - **Donanemab (Kisunla):** Approved in 2025 for early Alzheimer’s disease. **Potential blockbuster**, but revenue is not yet material.
  - **Emgality (galcanezumab):** $0.9B in FY2024 (declining due to competition).
- **Headwinds:**
  - **Slow uptake:** Donanemab’s launch is **hampered by reimbursement challenges** and safety concerns (e.g., ARIA side effects).
  - **Pipeline risk:** Remternetug (next-gen Alzheimer’s drug) is in **Phase 3 trials**, but Alzheimer’s is a **high-risk therapeutic area**.

## Growth Trajectory
- **Total revenue 3-year CAGR (FY2022–FY2025):** 24% (from $28.3B to $65.2B).
- **Drivers of growth:**
  - **Cardiometabolic Health:** +110% CAGR, contributing **90% of total revenue growth** over the period. Mounjaro/Zepbound alone added **$31.6B in revenue** from FY2022 to FY2025.
  - **Structural tailwinds:** Obesity and diabetes are **secular growth markets**, with global obesity prevalence rising ~2% annually. MD&A: *"We expect cardiometabolic health products will continue to represent a significant and growing portion of our business."*
  - **Pipeline expansion:** Lilly is investing in **next-gen GLP-1 drugs** (e.g., orforglipron, retatrutide) and **new indications** (e.g., Mounjaro for heart failure, liver disease).
- **Cyclical/cannibalization effects:**
  - **Trulicity decline:** -30% YoY in FY2025 as patients switch to **Mounjaro/Zepbound**.
  - **IRA impact:** Jardiance (2026), Trulicity/Verzenio (2028) face **Medicare price negotiations**, which could reduce U.S. revenue by **20–30%** per drug.

**M&A and restructurings:**
- **Acquisitions:** Lilly spent **$3.0B in FY2025** on IPR&D (e.g., Scorpion Therapeutics’ PI3Kα inhibitor, SiteOne Therapeutics). Pending deals in 2026 could total **< $3B**.
- **Divestitures:** None material in the period.

## Margin Profile
- **Gross margin:** 83.0% in FY2025 (vs. 81.3% in FY2024). **Favorable mix** (higher GLP-1 sales) and **improved production costs** offset pricing pressure.
- **Operating margin:** Not directly reported, but **operating income grew 54% YoY** in FY2025 (from $28.0B to $43.0B), outpacing revenue growth (+45% YoY).
- **Segment margins:**
  - **Cardiometabolic Health:** Likely **>85% gross margin** (high-margin biologics).
  - **Oncology/Immunology:** ~75–80% gross margin (small molecules and biologics).
  - **Neuroscience:** Lower margin due to **high R&D costs** (e.g., donanemab trials).
- **Operating leverage:** **SG&A grew 29% YoY** in FY2025 (vs. revenue growth of 45%), driven by **promotional spending** for GLP-1 launches. R&D grew 21% YoY, reflecting **pipeline investments**.

## Capital Allocation & Strategy
**Management priorities (from MD&A):**
1. **Organic investment:**
   - **R&D intensity:** 20.5% of revenue in FY2025 ($13.3B), up from 19.4% in FY2024. Focus areas:
     - **Cardiometabolic:** Next-gen GLP-1 drugs (orforglipron, retatrutide), new indications (e.g., Mounjaro for heart failure).
     - **Oncology:** KRAS inhibitors (olomorasib), ADCs (sofetabart mipitecan).
     - **Neuroscience:** Alzheimer’s (donanemab, remternetug).
   - **Capex:** $7.8B in FY2025 (vs. $5.1B in FY2024), primarily for **GLP-1 manufacturing expansion**.
2. **M&A:** Targeting **bolt-on acquisitions** to fill pipeline gaps (e.g., Scorpion, SiteOne). Pending deals in 2026 could total **< $3B**.
3. **Shareholder returns:**
   - **Dividends:** $6.00/share in FY2025 (+15% YoY). **Indicated 2026 dividend: $6.92/share**.
   - **Buybacks:** $4.1B repurchased in FY2025 under a **$15B program** (authorized in 2024).
4. **Debt:** Total debt rose to **$42.5B in FY2025** (from $33.6B in FY2024), but Lilly has **$10.1B in unused credit facilities** and strong cash flow ($16.8B from operations in FY2025).

**Financial targets:**
- **Management has not provided explicit 3-year targets**, but the focus is on **sustaining GLP-1 growth** and **expanding into new indications** (e.g., obesity-related comorbidities).

## Key Risks
1. **GLP-1 pricing pressure:**
   - **U.S. government negotiations:** Medicare access for Zepbound begins in **July 2026**, but Lilly has agreed to **lower Medicaid prices** in exchange. MD&A: *"Revenues generated from expanded access may be insufficient to offset pricing concessions."*
   - **IRA impact:** Jardiance (2026), Trulicity/Verzenio (2028) face **Medicare price negotiations**, which could reduce U.S. revenue by **20–30%** per drug.
2. **Competition in obesity/diabetes:**
   - **Novo Nordisk’s Wegovy/Semaglutide:** Already a **$10B+ franchise**, with **superior supply chain** and **lower prices** in some markets.
   - **Pipeline risk:** Lilly’s next-gen GLP-1 drugs (orforglipron, retatrutide) must **differentiate on efficacy/safety** to justify premium pricing.
3. **Manufacturing and supply chain:**
   - **Capacity constraints:** Lilly is investing **$7.8B in capex** to meet GLP-1 demand, but **delays could lead to $10B in penalties** under supply agreements.
   - **Counterfeit drugs:** Unapproved compounded tirzepatide threatens **patient safety and revenue**. MD&A: *"We cannot guarantee adequate regulation or compliance."*
4. **Regulatory and legal risks:**
   - **IRA uncertainty:** The **Inflation Reduction Act** could reduce Lilly’s U.S. revenue by **$5B+ annually** by 2030 (per Bernstein estimates).
   - **340B program litigation:** Ongoing disputes over **Medicaid drug pricing** could result in **fines or revenue clawbacks**.
5. **Pipeline execution:**
   - **Alzheimer’s (donanemab):** Slow uptake due to **reimbursement challenges** and **safety concerns** (ARIA side effects).
   - **Oncology:** Olomorasib and sofetabart mipitecan are in **Phase 3 trials**, but oncology is a **high-risk, competitive space**.
