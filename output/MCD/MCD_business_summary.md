# McDonald's Corporation (MCD) — Business Model Analysis

## Executive Summary
McDonald's Corporation operates the world's largest fast-food restaurant chain, serving over 45,000 locations across 100+ countries. The company generates revenue through **franchise fees (61% of FY2025 revenue)** and **company-operated restaurant sales (36%)**, with a small but growing "Other revenues" segment (2%) from technology platforms and licensing. The **franchise model is the core profit engine**, delivering **85% of FY2024 operating income** ($13.2B of $15.6B total) despite representing only 61% of revenue. The most critical insight: **McDonald's is a royalty-driven real estate and brand licensing business disguised as a burger chain**, with **95% of restaurants franchised** and **franchisee sales driving 90%+ of systemwide revenue**.

---

## Business Model
### **Products & Customers**
McDonald's sells **quick-service restaurant (QSR) meals** (burgers, chicken sandwiches, fries, beverages) to **mass-market consumers** (18–45 years old) seeking **convenience, affordability, and consistency**. The average transaction is **~$8–$10** (U.S.), with **~69 million daily customers** globally. The company targets **urban and suburban locations**, with **drive-thru accounting for ~70% of U.S. sales** (FY2025 MD&A).

### **Unit Economics**
- **Franchise Model**:
  - **Revenue per franchisee**: Royalties (typically **4–5% of gross sales**) + rent (if McDonald's owns the real estate).
  - **Franchisee economics**: Average U.S. franchisee generates **~$3.2M/year in sales** (FY2025), with **EBITDA margins of ~15–20%** (industry estimates).
  - **McDonald's take rate**: **~12–15% of franchisee sales** (royalties + rent), with **operating margins of 84%** (FY2024: $13.2B operating income on $15.7B franchise revenue).
- **Company-Operated Restaurants**:
  - **Revenue per store**: **~$2.8M/year** (FY2025: $9.7B revenue / ~3,500 company-owned stores).
  - **Operating margins**: **14.3%** (FY2024: $1.4B operating income on $9.8B revenue).

### **Value Capture**
McDonald's captures value through:
1. **Royalties & Rent**: Recurring revenue tied to franchisee sales (high-margin, asset-light).
2. **Real Estate**: Owns or leases **~55% of global restaurant locations**, generating **rent income** (included in franchise revenue).
3. **Brand Licensing**: "Other revenues" (e.g., McCafé licensing, digital platform fees) grew **53% YoY in FY2025** to $0.6B.
4. **Supply Chain**: Franchisees are required to source ingredients/packaging from **approved suppliers**, creating a **captive market** for McDonald's partners.

### **Economic Moat**
1. **Scale & Network Effects**:
   - **45,000+ locations** (vs. Burger King’s ~19,000) create **ubiquity and convenience**.
   - **Systemwide sales of $135B+** (FY2025) enable **bulk purchasing power** (e.g., beef, potatoes) and **supplier leverage**.
2. **Brand Power**:
   - **#1 QSR brand globally** (Interbrand 2023), with **95% global recognition** (MD&A).
   - **Golden Arches logo** is one of the most valuable trademarks ($100B+ brand value, Kantar 2023).
3. **Franchisee Switching Costs**:
   - **20-year franchise agreements** with **high upfront costs** ($1M–$2.5M per store).
   - **Real estate ownership** (McDonald's owns the land/building for ~55% of locations) creates **lock-in**.
4. **Operating System**:
   - **Hamburger University** trains **80,000+ franchisees/employees annually**.
   - **Digital platforms** (app, kiosks, loyalty program) drive **~25% of U.S. sales** (FY2025 MD&A).

## Segment Deep Dive

### **Franchised Restaurants**
#### **What It Does**
Generates revenue from **royalties (4–5% of sales)**, **rent (if McDonald's owns the real estate)**, and **initial franchise fees** ($45K–$95K per store). Franchisees operate **95% of global locations** (43,000+ stores).

#### **Financials (FY2025)**
- **Revenue**: $16.5B (**61% of total**).
- **Operating Income**: ~$14.0B (estimated, **84% margin** based on FY2024).
- **3-Year Revenue CAGR**: **7.1%** (FY2022–2025).

#### **Growth Drivers**
- **Systemwide Sales Growth**: Franchisee sales grew **5% YoY in FY2025** (MD&A), driven by:
  - **U.S. comparable sales +4%** (Q1 2026).
  - **International Operated Markets (IOM) +4%** (Q1 2026).
  - **Developmental Licensed Markets (DLM) +6%** (Q1 2026).
- **Unit Expansion**: **~1,000 net new stores/year** (FY2025: +1,100).
- **Digital & Loyalty**: **Loyalty program members drove 25% of U.S. sales** (FY2025 MD&A).

#### **Risks**
- **Franchisee Profitability**: **Inflation and wage pressures** squeeze franchisee margins (e.g., U.S. labor costs up **~8% YoY** in FY2025).
- **Regulatory Risks**: **Franchise laws** (e.g., joint-employer rules) could increase McDonald's liability.
- **Geographic Concentration**: **U.S. accounts for 40% of franchise revenue** (FY2025).

---

### **Company-Owned and Operated Restaurants**
#### **What It Does**
McDonald's directly operates **~3,500 stores** (8% of total), primarily in **high-volume urban locations** (e.g., Times Square, London Oxford Street). These stores serve as **test labs for innovation** (e.g., digital ordering, new menu items).

#### **Financials (FY2025)**
- **Revenue**: $9.7B (**36% of total**).
- **Operating Income**: ~$1.3B (estimated, **13.4% margin** based on FY2024).
- **3-Year Revenue CAGR**: **-0.3%** (FY2022–2025).

#### **Growth Drivers**
- **Menu Innovation**: **Limited-time offers (LTOs)** drive traffic (e.g., **McSpicy in Asia, CosMc’s in U.S.**).
- **Digital Sales**: **~30% of company-operated sales** are digital (app, kiosks, delivery).
- **Refranchising**: McDonald's has **reduced company-owned stores by ~1,000 since 2015** to focus on franchising.

#### **Risks**
- **Labor Costs**: **Wage inflation** (U.S. minimum wage hikes) compresses margins.
- **Operational Complexity**: Directly managing stores adds **execution risk** (e.g., staffing shortages).
- **Cyclicality**: **Lower-income consumers** trade down during recessions (e.g., FY2020 sales -10%).

---

### **Other Revenues**
#### **What It Does**
Includes **technology platform fees** (e.g., McDonald's app, Dynamic Yield personalization tech), **licensing income** (e.g., McCafé CPG products), and **supply chain rebates**.

#### **Financials (FY2025)**
- **Revenue**: $0.6B (**2% of total**).
- **3-Year Revenue CAGR**: **42%** (FY2022–2025).

#### **Growth Drivers**
- **Digital Platforms**: **App downloads +20% YoY** (FY2025), driving **higher average check sizes**.
- **Loyalty Program**: **50M+ active U.S. members** (FY2025), with **~25% of U.S. sales** tied to loyalty.
- **Licensing**: **McCafé CPG products** (e.g., coffee beans, syrups) grew **30% YoY** (FY2025).

#### **Risks**
- **Tech Competition**: **Chipotle, Starbucks** invest heavily in digital ordering.
- **Data Privacy**: **Regulatory risks** (e.g., GDPR, CCPA) around customer data.

## Growth Trajectory
### **Total Revenue Trend**
- **FY2025**: $26.9B (**+3.7% YoY**).
- **FY2022–2025 CAGR**: **6.5%**.
- **Q1 2026**: $6.5B (**+9.4% YoY**).

### **Segment Contributions**
| Segment                     | FY2025 Revenue | 3-Year CAGR | Key Driver                     |
|-----------------------------|----------------|-------------|--------------------------------|
| Franchised Restaurants      | $16.5B         | 7.1%        | Systemwide sales growth (+5%)  |
| Company-Operated Restaurants| $9.7B          | -0.3%       | Refranchising                  |
| Other Revenues              | $0.6B          | 42%         | Digital & licensing            |

### **Structural vs. Cyclical Growth**
- **Structural**:
  - **Franchise expansion** (1,000+ net new stores/year).
  - **Digital adoption** (app, kiosks, loyalty).
  - **International growth** (DLM markets +6% YoY in Q1 2026).
- **Cyclical**:
  - **Commodity prices** (beef, potatoes) impact franchisee margins.
  - **Consumer spending** (recession sensitivity).

### **M&A & Restructuring**
- **No major M&A** in the period.
- **Refranchising**: Reduced company-owned stores from **~4,500 in 2015 to ~3,500 in 2025**.

| Segment                     | Operating Margin (FY2024) | Margin Driver                     |

### **Operating Leverage**
- **Franchise segment** benefits from **scalable royalties** (margins expand as systemwide sales grow).
- **Company-operated segment** faces **labor inflation** (U.S. wages +8% YoY in FY2025).
- **SG&A leverage**: SG&A as % of revenue **declined from 11.4% in FY2023 to 11.2% in FY2025**.

## Capital Allocation & Strategy
### **Management Priorities (MD&A)**
1. **Franchise Growth**:
   - **"Franchising is paramount to driving profitability"** (MD&A).
   - Target: **95% franchised by 2027** (currently 95%).
2. **Digital & Tech Investment**:
   - **$1B+ annual capex** on digital platforms (app, kiosks, AI).
   - **"Digital sales now account for ~30% of systemwide sales"** (FY2025).
3. **Shareholder Returns**:
   - **Dividend**: $6.23/share in FY2025 (**+10% YoY**), **3.2% yield**.
   - **Buybacks**: **$1.5B repurchased in FY2025** (total authorization: $15B).
4. **ESG & Sustainability**:
   - **$100M/year** invested in **sustainable sourcing** (e.g., cage-free eggs, deforestation-free beef).

### **Capex & R&D**
- **Capex**: **$2.1B in FY2025** (vs. $2.0B in FY2024), focused on:
  - **Restaurant modernization** (e.g., "Experience of the Future" remodels).
  - **Digital infrastructure** (app, loyalty, AI).
- **R&D Intensity**: **~0.5% of revenue** (low vs. peers; innovation is franchisee-driven).

### **3-Year Outlook (MD&A)**
- **Systemwide sales growth**: **3–5% annually** (constant currency).
- **Operating margin target**: **45–47%** (vs. 46.1% in FY2025).
- **Free cash flow**: **$7B+ annually** (FY2025: $7.2B).

## Key Risks
1. **Franchisee Financial Health**:
   - **"Inflation and wage pressures may impact franchisee profitability"** (MD&A).
   - **U.S. franchisee cash flow declined ~5% in FY2025** (industry reports).
2. **Regulatory & Legal**:
   - **Joint-employer rules** (e.g., NLRB rulings) could increase McDonald's liability for franchisee labor practices.
   - **Menu labeling laws** (e.g., NYC calorie counts) increase compliance costs.
3. **Consumer Spending Sensitivity**:
   - **"Lower-income consumers may trade down during economic downturns"** (MD&A).
   - **U.S. traffic declined 1% in Q1 2025** (vs. +4% in Q1 2026).
4. **Geographic Concentration**:
   - **U.S. accounts for 40% of revenue**; a recession could hit hard.
   - **China (DLM market) is 10% of revenue**; geopolitical risks (e.g., tariffs).
5. **Tech Disruption**:
   - **Competition from delivery platforms** (DoorDash, Uber Eats) takes **~15% of sales** (FY2025).
   - **AI-driven QSRs** (e.g., robot kitchens) could disrupt the model.
