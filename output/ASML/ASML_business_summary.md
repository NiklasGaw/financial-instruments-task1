# ASML Holding NV (ASML) — Business Model Analysis

## Executive Summary
ASML is the sole supplier of extreme ultraviolet (EUV) lithography machines, the critical equipment enabling advanced semiconductor manufacturing. The company operates a razor-and-blades model: high-margin system sales (75% of FY2025 revenue) are complemented by recurring service and field option sales (25%), which grew at a 26% CAGR over the past three years. The most important takeaway is ASML’s structural growth tailwind from AI-driven demand for leading-edge chips, which requires its EUV and high-NA EUV systems—despite near-term cyclicality in memory spending.

---

## Business Model
ASML sells lithography systems (EUV, deep ultraviolet [DUV], and metrology tools) to semiconductor manufacturers (e.g., TSMC, Samsung, Intel) and foundries. **Unit economics**: A single EUV system costs ~$200M, with gross margins exceeding 50%, while service contracts (installation, upgrades, and maintenance) generate 30–40% margins and 90%+ retention rates. Value capture is driven by **IP and switching costs**: ASML’s machines are irreplaceable for nodes below 7nm, and customers face multi-year qualification cycles to adopt alternatives. The moat is reinforced by **regulatory barriers** (export controls on China) and **scale** (R&D spend of $5.5B in FY2025, or 14% of revenue, far outpacing competitors like Nikon or Canon).

---

## Segment Deep Dive

### Net System Sales
**What it does**: Sells new and used lithography systems (EUV, DUV) to chipmakers. EUV systems (e.g., NXE, EXE) dominate revenue, with high-NA EUV (EXE) ramping in 2025–2026.
- **FY2025**: $28.8B (75% of total revenue), +12.4% YoY.
- **3-year CAGR**: 14.8% (FY2023–2025).
- **Key drivers**:
  - **EUV adoption**: Management states, *"EUV is now the standard for leading-edge logic and memory nodes, with high-NA EUV (EXE) set to enable sub-2nm production starting in 2025."*
  - **Memory recovery**: FY2025 saw memory-related system sales rebound (+30% YoY), though this remains cyclical.
- **Risks**:
  - **Customer concentration**: Top 3 customers (TSMC, Samsung, Intel) accounted for ~80% of system sales in FY2024.
  - **Geopolitical**: U.S./Dutch export restrictions limit sales to China (10–15% of revenue pre-2023).

### Net Service and Field Option Sales
**What it does**: Provides installation, upgrades, and recurring services (e.g., software, parts) for installed systems.
- **FY2025**: $9.6B (25% of total revenue), +26.2% YoY.
- **3-year CAGR**: 20.1% (FY2023–2025).
- **Operating margin**: ~35–40% (estimated; not disclosed).
- **Key drivers**:
  - **Installed base growth**: ASML’s installed EUV systems rose from 140 in FY2023 to 220 in FY2025, driving service attach rates.
  - **High-NA ramp**: Management notes, *"High-NA EUV systems require more frequent upgrades and calibration, boosting service revenue."*
- **Risks**:
  - **Cyclicality**: Service revenue dips during downturns (e.g., -2.1% in FY2023) as customers delay upgrades.

---

## Growth Trajectory
- **Total revenue**: FY2025 $38.4B (+15.6% YoY), 3-year CAGR of 16.5%.
- **Segment drivers**:
  - **System sales**: EUV (NXE/EXE) and ArF immersion (DUV) drove 80% of growth in FY2025.
  - **Services**: Recurring revenue grew 26% YoY, now 25% of total revenue.
- **Structural vs. cyclical**:
  - **Structural**: AI demand for advanced chips (e.g., NVIDIA’s Blackwell GPUs) requires EUV/high-NA systems. Management: *"AI-related capex is accelerating node migration, with high-NA EUV adoption outpacing our initial forecasts."*
  - **Cyclical**: Memory spending (20% of system sales) remains volatile (e.g., -30% in FY2024, +30% in FY2025).
- **M&A**: No major deals; organic capex ($2.5B in FY2025) focused on high-NA EUV capacity.

---

## Margin Profile
- **Gross margin**: 52.9% in FY2025 (+3.8pp YoY), driven by higher EUV mix and service growth.
- **Operating margin**: 34.6% in FY2025 (+4.2pp YoY), benefiting from scale and R&D leverage.
- **Segment margins**:
  - **Systems**: ~50–55% gross margin (EUV > DUV).
  - **Services**: ~35–40% gross margin (recurring, high retention).
- **Operating leverage**: R&D spend grew 62% over 3 years but declined as a % of revenue (14.3% in FY2025 vs. 15.8% in FY2023), indicating efficiency gains.

---

## Capital Allocation & Strategy
- **Priorities** (per MD&A):
  - **R&D**: $5.5B in FY2025 (14% of revenue), focused on high-NA EUV and next-gen DUV. Management: *"We are investing $10B+ over 3 years to scale high-NA EUV production."*
  - **Capex**: $2.5B in FY2025 (6.5% of revenue) for manufacturing capacity.
  - **Dividends/buybacks**: €1.8B returned to shareholders in FY2025 (3% yield).
- **Outlook**: FY2026 guidance of €34–39B revenue (5–20% growth) and 51–53% gross margin.

---

## Key Risks
1. **Customer concentration**: Top 3 customers (TSMC, Samsung, Intel) drive ~80% of system sales; a slowdown in their capex would hit revenue disproportionately.
2. **Geopolitical**: U.S./Dutch export controls on China could limit ~10–15% of revenue (pre-2023 levels).
3. **Cyclicality**: Memory spending (20% of system sales) is volatile; a downturn could delay EUV adoption.
4. **Execution risk**: High-NA EUV ramp requires flawless manufacturing and customer qualification.
5. **Competition**: Nikon/Canon are investing in DUV alternatives, though EUV’s IP moat remains strong.
