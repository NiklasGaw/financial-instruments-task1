# Applied Materials Inc. (AMAT) — Business Model Analysis

## Executive Summary
Applied Materials is the world’s largest supplier of semiconductor fabrication equipment and services, selling high-precision tools and software to chipmakers and display manufacturers. The company makes money by selling capital equipment (73% of FY2025 revenue) and recurring services (23%). The single most important thing to know is that **Semiconductor Systems is the profit engine**—it delivered 89% of FY2025 operating income on 35.5% margins, while growth is being driven by AI-driven demand for leading-edge logic chips and advanced packaging.

---

## Business Model
Applied Materials sells **capital equipment** (etch, deposition, ion implantation, and inspection tools) and **services** (spares, upgrades, and factory automation software) to semiconductor manufacturers. Customers are primarily **foundries** (TSMC, Samsung, Intel) and **memory producers** (SK Hynix, Micron). Typical transactions are **multi-million-dollar equipment sales** with 6–12 month lead times, followed by **multi-year service contracts** that generate 28% operating margins.

Value capture is a **hybrid model**:
- **Equipment sales** (73% of revenue) carry 45–50% gross margins but are cyclical.
- **Services** (23% of revenue) are recurring (60% of AGS revenue is under long-term agreements) and carry 28% operating margins.

The economic moat is **IP and switching costs**:
- **1,000+ patents** in materials engineering create a 3–5 year lead in process technology.
- **Installed base of 50,000+ tools** locks customers into AMAT’s service ecosystem (AGS revenue grew at 3% CAGR over 3 years).
- **Scale**: AMAT’s R&D budget ($3.6B in FY2025, 12.6% of revenue) dwarfs competitors’ (Lam Research: $1.5B, ASML: $3.2B).

## Segment Deep Dive

### Semiconductor Systems
**What it does**: Sells capital equipment for semiconductor fabrication (etch, deposition, ion implantation, inspection).
- **FY2025**: $20.8B revenue (73% of total), $7.4B operating income (89% of total), 35.5% margin.
- **3-year revenue CAGR**: 2.5% (FY2023–FY2025).
- **Sub-segment mix (FY2025)**:
  - Foundry/logic: 67% ($13.9B), +18% YoY (Q2 FY2026).
  - DRAM: 26% ($5.4B), flat YoY.
  - NAND: 7% ($1.5B), +75% YoY.

**Growth drivers**:
- **AI demand**: "Foundry and logic customers’ spending in fiscal 2025 increased driven primarily by higher customer investments in leading-edge manufacturing technologies" (MD&A).
- **Advanced packaging**: High-bandwidth memory (HBM) and chiplet architectures require AMAT’s etch/deposition tools.
- **China self-sufficiency**: Despite U.S. export controls, AMAT’s non-leading-edge tools (e.g., 200mm) are still sold to Chinese foundries.

**Risks**:
- **Cyclicality**: Foundry/logic revenue fell 8% YoY in Q1 FY2026 (MD&A: "customer facility readiness and timeline for installation").
- **Export controls**: U.S. restrictions on advanced tools to China (30% of FY2024 revenue vs. 16% in FY2025).
- **Competition**: ASML (lithography), Lam Research (etch), and Tokyo Electron (deposition) are gaining share in EUV and high-NA tools.

---

### Applied Global Services (AGS)
**What it does**: Provides spares, upgrades, and factory automation software for AMAT’s installed base.
- **FY2025**: $6.4B revenue (23% of total), $1.8B operating income (22% of total), 28.1% margin.
- **3-year revenue CAGR**: 5.5% (FY2023–FY2025).
- **Revenue mix**: 60% from long-term service agreements (recurring), 40% from spares/upgrades.

**Growth drivers**:
- **Installed base growth**: AMAT’s tools in the field grew 10% YoY (MD&A).
- **Subscription shift**: "Our strategy is to continue to shift the AGS service and spares business to a subscription agreement model" (MD&A).
- **200mm equipment**: Effective Q1 FY2026, 200mm tools move to Semiconductor Systems, removing a drag on AGS margins.

**Risks**:
- **Margin compression**: AGS margins fell 100 bps YoY in FY2025 due to "higher excess and obsolete inventory charges" (MD&A).
- **Customer concentration**: Top 5 customers account for 50% of AGS revenue (TSMC, Samsung, Intel).

---

## Growth Trajectory
- **Total revenue**: $28.4B in FY2025 (+4.4% YoY), 3-year CAGR of 3.5% (FY2023–FY2025).
- **Segment drivers**:
  - **Semiconductor Systems**: +4% YoY in FY2025, driven by foundry/logic (+18% YoY in Q2 FY2026) and NAND (+75% YoY).
  - **AGS**: +3% YoY, but margins compressed 100 bps.
- **Structural vs. cyclical**:
  - **Structural**: AI, advanced packaging, and edge computing are "secular drivers" (MD&A).
  - **Cyclical**: Memory spending (DRAM/NAND) is volatile (DRAM revenue fell 7% YoY in FY2025).
- **M&A**: $29M spent on acquisitions in FY2025 (immaterial).

---

## Margin Profile
- **Gross margin**: 48.7% in FY2025 (+120 bps YoY), driven by "favorable customer and product mix" (MD&A).
- **Operating margin**: 29.2% in FY2025 (+30 bps YoY).
- **Segment margins**:
  - **Semiconductor Systems**: 35.5% (franchise segment).
  - **AGS**: 28.1% (down 100 bps YoY).
- **Operating leverage**: Positive—SG&A fell 5% YoY in FY2025 despite revenue growth.

---

## Capital Allocation & Strategy
**Priorities (MD&A)**:
1. **Organic growth**: R&D spend of $3.6B (12.6% of revenue) in FY2025, up 10% YoY.
2. **Shareholder returns**: $4.9B in buybacks and $1.4B in dividends in FY2025.
3. **M&A**: Minimal activity ($29M in FY2025), but "investments in technology to access new market opportunities" (MD&A).

**Targets**:
- **Long-term**: "Secular drivers such as data center AI, edge AI, and electric vehicles will expand our served market" (MD&A).
- **Financial**: No formal targets, but management expects "continued demand for AI and data center computing" (MD&A).

---

## Key Risks
1. **Export controls**: U.S. restrictions on advanced tools to China (30% of FY2024 revenue vs. 16% in FY2025).
2. **Cyclicality**: Semiconductor capex is volatile (foundry/logic revenue fell 8% YoY in Q1 FY2026).
3. **Customer concentration**: Top 5 customers (TSMC, Samsung, Intel) account for 60% of revenue.
4. **Geographic concentration**: Asia-Pacific is 86% of revenue (Taiwan + Korea + China = 74%).
5. **Tech disruption**: EUV and high-NA lithography (ASML’s domain) could reduce AMAT’s share in leading-edge logic.
