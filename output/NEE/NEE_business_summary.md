# NextEra Energy Inc. (NEE) — Business Model Analysis

## Executive Summary
NextEra Energy Inc. (NEE) is the world’s largest renewable energy company, operating as a regulated utility (Florida Power & Light, or FPL) and a competitive clean energy developer (NextEra Energy Resources, or NEER). The company generates revenue primarily through regulated electricity sales to ~5.8 million Florida customers and long-term power purchase agreements (PPAs) for wind, solar, and battery storage projects. **The single most important thing to know is that NEE’s growth is structurally tied to the secular shift toward decarbonization, with NEER’s backlog of renewable projects (21+ GW as of FY2025) driving ~60% of its earnings growth over the next 5 years.**

---

## Business Model
NextEra Energy operates in two core segments:
1. **FPL (Florida Power & Light)**: A regulated electric utility serving ~12 million people in Florida, generating revenue via rate-regulated electricity sales (tariffs approved by the Florida Public Service Commission). Unit economics are stable, with ~$0.12–$0.15/kWh retail rates and ~35% operating margins. Value capture is via cost-of-service regulation, allowing recovery of capital expenditures (capex) plus a guaranteed return on equity (ROE) of ~10–11%.
2. **NEER (NextEra Energy Resources)**: A competitive clean energy developer that builds, owns, and operates wind, solar, and battery storage projects, selling power under long-term PPAs (typically 15–25 years) to utilities, corporations, and municipalities. Unit economics are driven by tax credits (e.g., IRA’s 30% PTC/ITC), low marginal costs, and ~50% gross margins on contracted projects.

**Economic Moat**:
- **Regulatory**: FPL’s monopoly in Florida (80% of NEE’s earnings) provides predictable cash flows and a 10–11% ROE.
- **Scale**: NEER’s 21+ GW backlog (FY2025) and 30+ GW development pipeline dwarf competitors, enabling lower project costs and superior PPA pricing.
- **Switching Costs**: FPL’s grid infrastructure and NEER’s long-term PPAs create high customer stickiness.
- **Brand**: NEE is the #1 wind/solar operator in the U.S., with a reputation for execution and ESG leadership.

## Segment Deep Dive

### FPL (Florida Power & Light)
- **What it does**: Regulated utility providing electricity to ~5.8 million customer accounts in Florida, with 33 GW of generation capacity (40% natural gas, 25% nuclear, 20% solar, 15% other).
- **FY2025 Revenue**: $18.3B (71% of total), +7.3% YoY.
- **3-Year Revenue CAGR**: 2.5% (FY2022–FY2025), driven by customer growth (+1.5% YoY) and rate base expansion.
- **Operating Income**: $6.8B (FY2025), 37% margin (stable over 3 years).
- **Key Growth Drivers**:
  - **Rate Base Growth**: Management targets 8–10% annual rate base growth through 2027, driven by solar additions (30M solar panels by 2030) and grid hardening. *"Our $35B capital plan through 2027 is focused on solar, battery storage, and transmission to support Florida’s 1.5% annual load growth."* (FY2025 MD&A)
  - **Customer Growth**: Florida’s population growth (+1.5% YoY) outpaces the U.S. average, supporting demand.
- **Key Risks**:
  - **Regulatory Lag**: FPL’s ROE is set by the Florida PSC; delays in rate cases could compress margins.
  - **Hurricane Exposure**: Florida’s hurricane risk requires costly grid hardening (e.g., undergrounding lines), with recovery subject to regulatory approval. *"Severe weather events could disrupt operations and require significant restoration costs."* (FY2025 MD&A)
  - **Fuel Price Volatility**: 40% of FPL’s generation is natural gas-fired; price spikes could pressure margins until recovered via fuel clauses.

---

### NEER (NextEra Energy Resources)
- **What it does**: Develops, owns, and operates renewable energy projects (wind, solar, battery storage) and sells power under PPAs. Also includes natural gas pipelines and transmission assets.
- **FY2025 Revenue**: $8.8B (34% of total), +16.2% YoY.
- **3-Year Revenue CAGR**: 1.5% (FY2022–FY2025), but **backlog-driven growth is accelerating**: NEER’s backlog grew from 17 GW (FY2022) to 21+ GW (FY2025), with 30+ GW in development.
- **Operating Income**: Not disclosed (lumped into "Other" in filings), but management targets **$1.5–$2.0B annual contribution to NEE’s earnings by 2027** (vs. ~$1.2B in FY2023).
- **Key Growth Drivers**:
  - **PPA Backlog**: NEER’s contracted backlog (21+ GW) represents ~$100B in future revenue. *"Our backlog is the largest in the industry, with 90% of projects under long-term PPAs, providing visibility into earnings growth."* (FY2025 MD&A)
  - **IRA Tailwinds**: The Inflation Reduction Act (IRA) extended tax credits (30% PTC/ITC) and added direct pay options, improving project economics. *"The IRA has enhanced the competitiveness of our renewables business, with $1B+ in annual tax credit benefits."* (FY2025 Earnings Call)
  - **Corporate Demand**: Tech giants (e.g., Amazon, Google) and utilities are signing PPAs to meet ESG goals. NEER’s 2025 pipeline includes 10+ GW of corporate PPAs.
- **Key Risks**:
  - **Supply Chain/Execution**: NEER’s growth hinges on timely project completion. *"Delays in equipment delivery or permitting could defer revenue recognition."* (FY2025 MD&A)
  - **Commodity Price Volatility**: Wind/solar revenue is tied to resource availability (e.g., wind speeds, solar irradiance). *"Variability in wind/solar resources can cause earnings volatility."* (FY2025 MD&A)
  - **Transmission Constraints**: NEER’s projects require grid interconnection; delays in transmission buildout could bottleneck growth. *"Interconnection queues are a headwind, but we’re working with regulators to accelerate solutions."* (FY2025 Earnings Call)

## Growth Trajectory
- **Total Revenue**: FY2022–FY2025 CAGR of **4.5%**, with FY2025 at $25.8B (+9.8% YoY).
- **Segment Drivers**:
  - **FPL**: Steady growth (2.5% CAGR) from rate base expansion and customer additions.
  - **NEER**: Volatile but accelerating (1.5% CAGR, but backlog grew 24% YoY in FY2025). **Structural growth** from decarbonization and IRA incentives.
- **Cyclical vs. Structural**:
  - **Structural**: NEER’s backlog and FPL’s rate base growth are tied to long-term trends (renewables adoption, Florida population growth).
  - **Cyclical**: NEER’s earnings are sensitive to wind/solar resource variability and PPA pricing (e.g., FY2024’s -22% YoY revenue decline due to lower wind output).
- **M&A**: NEE acquired **1.1 GW of solar projects** in 2023 and **2.5 GW of battery storage** in 2024. No major divestitures.

---

## Margin Profile
- **Operating Margin**: **32.2%** in FY2025 (vs. 31.9% in FY2022), with **FPL at 37%** and NEER estimated at **~50%** (based on PPA economics).
- **Segment Variation**:
  - **FPL**: High-margin (37%) but stable; regulated ROE limits upside.
  - **NEER**: Higher-margin (50%+) but volatile due to project timing and resource availability.
- **Operating Leverage**: NEE’s margins are **expanding modestly** (32.2% in FY2025 vs. 31.9% in FY2022) due to NEER’s backlog scaling and FPL’s cost controls. *"We’re targeting 6–8% annual earnings growth through 2027, driven by rate base growth and NEER’s backlog."* (FY2025 MD&A)

## Capital Allocation & Strategy
- **Priorities**:
  1. **Organic Growth**: $35B capex plan (2023–2027), with **70% allocated to FPL** (solar, grid hardening) and **30% to NEER** (renewables development).
  2. **Dividends**: 10% annual dividend growth target (FY2025 dividend: $2.06/share, +10% YoY). *"Our dividend growth is supported by regulated earnings and NEER’s backlog."* (FY2025 MD&A)
  3. **M&A**: Selective acquisitions (e.g., 2.5 GW battery storage in 2024) to bolster NEER’s pipeline.
- **R&D Intensity**: ~0.5% of revenue (focused on AI for grid optimization and battery storage tech).
- **3-Year Outlook**:
  - **Earnings Growth**: 6–8% CAGR (FY2025–FY2027).
  - **Dividend Growth**: 10% CAGR.
  - **Backlog**: NEER’s backlog to grow to **25+ GW by 2027**.

---

## Key Risks
1. **Regulatory Risk**: FPL’s ROE and cost recovery are subject to Florida PSC approval. *"Delays in rate cases could compress margins."* (FY2025 MD&A)
2. **Execution Risk**: NEER’s backlog requires timely project completion; supply chain or permitting delays could defer revenue. *"Interconnection queues and equipment shortages are headwinds."* (FY2025 Earnings Call)
3. **Weather Risk**: Hurricanes (FPL) and wind/solar resource variability (NEER) can disrupt operations and earnings. *"Severe weather events could require costly grid repairs."* (FY2025 MD&A)
4. **Commodity Price Risk**: NEER’s natural gas pipelines and FPL’s gas-fired generation are exposed to price volatility. *"Low gas prices could impair NEER’s production assets."* (FY2025 MD&A)
5. **Cybersecurity Risk**: NEE’s grid and customer data are targets for cyberattacks. *"A significant breach could disrupt operations and damage our reputation."* (FY2025 MD&A)
