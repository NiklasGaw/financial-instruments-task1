# AbbVie Inc. (ABBV) — Business Model Analysis

## Executive Summary
AbbVie is a global biopharmaceutical company that develops, manufactures, and commercializes advanced therapies for immunology, oncology, neuroscience, aesthetics, and other complex diseases. The company generates revenue primarily through high-margin prescription drugs, with **~80% of FY2025 revenue ($61.2B) derived from immunology and neuroscience segments**. The most critical insight is that **AbbVie has successfully transitioned its immunology franchise from Humira (now in decline) to Skyrizi and Rinvoq, which drove 8.6% YoY revenue growth in FY2025 despite Humira’s -49.5% collapse**. However, this growth is heavily reliant on continued adoption of these two drugs, which face intensifying competition and pricing pressure.

---

## Business Model
AbbVie’s business model centers on **high-margin, patent-protected specialty drugs** targeting chronic and severe diseases. The company sells:
- **Biologics and small-molecule drugs** (e.g., Skyrizi, Rinvoq, Venclexta) to patients via prescriptions, primarily through physicians, hospitals, and payers (e.g., Medicare, private insurers).
- **Aesthetic products** (e.g., Botox Cosmetic, Juvederm) to dermatologists and plastic surgeons.
- **Therapeutic devices** (e.g., Duodopa for Parkinson’s) to specialized clinics.

**Unit economics**:
- **Immunology/oncology drugs**: High gross margins (~70–80%) due to patent protection, but require heavy R&D ($7.2B in FY2025, ~12% of revenue) and commercialization costs (SG&A ~23% of revenue).
- **Aesthetics**: Lower gross margins (~60–70%) but higher operating margins due to lower R&D spend and direct-to-consumer marketing.
- **Revenue capture**: AbbVie earns **recurring revenue** from chronic therapies (e.g., quarterly injections of Skyrizi) and **one-time sales** for acute treatments (e.g., Ubrelvy for migraines). Pricing power is strong in immunology/oncology but eroding due to biosimilars and payer negotiations.

**Economic moat**:
1. **Intellectual property**: Skyrizi and Rinvoq are protected by patents until **2033** (composition of matter) and **2035** (pediatric exclusivity), respectively. Humira’s U.S. exclusivity ended in 2023, but AbbVie has **~130 patents** globally to delay biosimilar entry.
2. **Switching costs**: Patients on biologics (e.g., Skyrizi) face high switching costs due to efficacy risks and payer restrictions.
3. **Regulatory barriers**: Biologics require **complex manufacturing** and **clinical trials** for biosimilars, delaying competition (e.g., Humira biosimilars took 7+ years post-patent expiry to launch).
4. **Brand and scale**: AbbVie’s **$14B SG&A spend** (FY2025) funds physician education, patient support programs, and payer negotiations, creating a **commercialization advantage** over smaller competitors.

## Segment Deep Dive

### Immunology
**What it does**: Develops and sells biologics and small-molecule drugs for autoimmune diseases (e.g., rheumatoid arthritis, psoriasis, Crohn’s disease).
- **FY2025 revenue**: $30.4B (49.7% of total).
- **3-year CAGR**: 5.2% (FY2023–FY2025), driven by Skyrizi/Rinvoq offsetting Humira’s decline.
- **Key drivers**:
  - **Skyrizi**: $17.6B in FY2025 (+49.9% YoY), now the **#1 immunology drug by revenue**. Approved for **5 indications** (psoriasis, psoriatic arthritis, Crohn’s, ulcerative colitis) with **quarterly dosing** (high patient compliance).
  - **Rinvoq**: $8.3B in FY2025 (+39.1% YoY), an **oral JAK inhibitor** competing with injectables like Humira. Approved for **9 indications**, including atopic dermatitis and ulcerative colitis.
  - **Humira**: $4.5B in FY2025 (-49.5% YoY), down from $21.2B in FY2022. **U.S. biosimilar competition** (10+ entrants) has eroded share, but **international markets** (where Humira retains exclusivity) still generate $3B+ annually.
- **Risks**:
  - **Skyrizi/Rinvoq competition**: IL-23 inhibitors (e.g., Johnson & Johnson’s Tremfya) and JAK inhibitors (e.g., Pfizer’s Xeljanz) are gaining share. **Payer pressure** is intensifying as these drugs become first-line therapies.
  - **Pricing erosion**: U.S. net pricing for Skyrizi/Rinvoq **declined ~5% YoY in FY2025** (per MD&A), with further pressure expected as biosimilars enter.
  - **Pipeline gaps**: No major immunology launches until **2027–2028** (e.g., ABBV-3373 for lupus), leaving Skyrizi/Rinvoq as the sole growth drivers.

> **Management quote (FY2025 10-K)**: *"Skyrizi and Rinvoq are now the cornerstone of our immunology franchise, with combined revenue exceeding Humira’s peak. We are investing in label expansions to drive further growth, including Skyrizi’s recent approval for ulcerative colitis."*

### Neuroscience
**What it does**: Focuses on **migraine, schizophrenia, bipolar disorder, and Parkinson’s disease** with a mix of small molecules and biologics.
- **FY2025 revenue**: $14.8B (24.2% of total).
- **3-year CAGR**: 12.1% (FY2023–FY2025), the **fastest-growing segment**.
- **Key drivers**:
  - **Vraylar**: $3.6B in FY2025 (+10.8% YoY), a **dopamine partial agonist** for schizophrenia/bipolar disorder. **Market share leader** in bipolar depression (30%+ share).
  - **Botox Therapeutic**: $3.8B in FY2025 (+14.8% YoY), used for **chronic migraine, overactive bladder, and spasticity**. **~70% of revenue is from chronic migraine** (high recurrence rates).
  - **Qulipta/Ubrelvy**: $2.3B combined in FY2025 (+40% YoY), **CGRP inhibitors** for migraine prevention (Qulipta) and acute treatment (Ubrelvy). **Qulipta is the #1 prescribed oral CGRP** in the U.S.
  - **Vyalev**: $0.5B in FY2025 (launched in 2024), a **24-hour infusion for Parkinson’s**. Early uptake is strong, but **competition from oral therapies** (e.g., Roche’s prasinezumab) looms.
- **Risks**:
  - **Botox Therapeutic competition**: **DaxibotulinumtoxinA (Daxxify)** from Revance is gaining share in aesthetics and could expand into therapeutics.
  - **CGRP market saturation**: **4+ CGRP inhibitors** (e.g., Lilly’s Emgality, Teva’s Ajovy) compete for migraine prevention, with **pricing pressure** expected.
  - **Vraylar patent expiry**: Key patents expire in **2027–2029**, with **generic/biosimilar entry likely by 2030**.

> **Management quote (FY2025 10-K)**: *"Neuroscience is a key growth pillar, with Vraylar and Botox Therapeutic delivering double-digit growth. We are expanding Qulipta’s label to include chronic migraine and exploring Vyalev’s potential in earlier-stage Parkinson’s."*

### Oncology
**What it does**: Develops **targeted cancer therapies**, primarily for blood cancers (e.g., leukemia, lymphoma).
- **FY2025 revenue**: $6.7B (10.9% of total).
- **3-year CAGR**: -1.2% (FY2023–FY2025), **declining due to Imbruvica’s patent expiry**.
- **Key drivers**:
  - **Venclexta**: $2.8B in FY2025 (+8.1% YoY), a **BCL-2 inhibitor** for leukemia. **First-line approval in AML** (acute myeloid leukemia) is driving growth.
  - **Imbruvica**: $2.9B in FY2025 (-14.3% YoY), a **BTK inhibitor** for CLL (chronic lymphocytic leukemia). **U.S. patent expiry in 2026** will accelerate decline.
  - **Epkinly/Elahere**: $0.3B combined in FY2025 (launched in 2023–2024), **antibody-drug conjugates (ADCs)** for lymphoma and ovarian cancer. Early-stage but **high unmet need**.
- **Risks**:
  - **Imbruvica’s decline**: **Generic BTK inhibitors** (e.g., BeiGene’s Brukinsa) are gaining share, with **~50% revenue erosion expected by 2027**.
  - **Venclexta competition**: **AstraZeneca’s Calquence** (BTK inhibitor) and **Roche’s Polivy** (ADC) are expanding in CLL and lymphoma.
  - **Pipeline dependence**: **No major oncology launches until 2026** (e.g., ABBV-383 for multiple myeloma), leaving Venclexta as the sole near-term growth driver.

---

### Aesthetics
**What it does**: Sells **injectables (Botox, Juvederm), devices (CoolSculpting), and skincare (SkinMedica)** for cosmetic and medical use.
- **FY2025 revenue**: $5.2B (8.5% of total, **excluded from FY2025 segment data but included in "All Other"**).
- **3-year CAGR**: -3.0% (FY2023–FY2025), **declining due to post-pandemic normalization**.
- **Key drivers**:
  - **Botox Cosmetic**: $2.7B in FY2024 (+1.4% YoY), the **#1 aesthetic injectable globally**. **~70% of revenue is from the U.S.**
  - **Juvederm**: $1.2B in FY2024 (-14.6% YoY), **hyaluronic acid fillers** for facial volume loss. **Competition from Galderma’s Restylane** is intensifying.
  - **CoolSculpting**: **Body contouring device** with **~20% market share** in the U.S.
- **Risks**:
  - **Botox competition**: **Daxxify (Revance)** and **Jeuveau (Evolus)** are gaining share in aesthetics, with **Daxxify’s longer duration** (6+ months vs. Botox’s 3–4 months) a key advantage.
  - **Pricing pressure**: **Medicare/Medicaid reimbursement cuts** for therapeutic Botox (e.g., migraine) could spill over into aesthetics.
  - **Consumer discretionary spend**: Aesthetics revenue is **cyclical** and sensitive to economic downturns.

### Other Key Products
**What it does**: Includes **HCV treatments (Mavyret), pancreatic enzymes (Creon), and IBS drugs (Linzess)**.
- **FY2025 revenue**: $3.2B (5.2% of total).
- **3-year CAGR**: -5.8% (FY2023–FY2025), **declining due to HCV market saturation**.
- **Key drivers**:
  - **Creon**: $1.5B in FY2025 (+9.3% YoY), a **pancreatic enzyme** for cystic fibrosis. **No generic competition** in the U.S.
  - **Mavyret**: $1.3B in FY2025 (+0.4% YoY), an **HCV cure** with **~95% cure rates**. **Market is shrinking** as HCV prevalence declines.
- **Risks**:
  - **Mavyret’s decline**: **HCV treatment rates are falling** as the disease is eradicated in many patients.
  - **Linzess competition**: **Ironwood’s Linzess faces generic entry in 2026**, with **~50% revenue at risk**.

## Growth Trajectory
- **Total revenue**:
  - **FY2023**: $54.3B (-6.4% YoY).
  - **FY2024**: $56.3B (+3.7% YoY).
  - **FY2025**: $61.2B (+8.6% YoY).
  - **3-year CAGR**: **4.0%** (FY2023–FY2025).
- **Growth drivers**:
  - **Immunology**: Skyrizi/Rinvoq **added $12B in revenue since FY2023**, offsetting Humira’s $16B decline.
  - **Neuroscience**: **Vraylar, Botox Therapeutic, and Qulipta** drove **$7B in growth** over the same period.
  - **Cyclical vs. structural**: Growth is **structural** (new indications, label expansions) but **partially offset by Humira’s decline** (cyclical).
- **M&A**: **No major deals since 2020** (Allergan acquisition). **Divestitures**: Sold **eye care business (Alcon) in 2023** for $1.4B.

> **Management quote (FY2025 10-K)**: *"Our growth is underpinned by Skyrizi and Rinvoq, which we expect to deliver double-digit revenue growth through 2030. Neuroscience will remain a key driver, with Vraylar and Qulipta expanding into new indications."*

## Margin Profile
- **Gross margin**: **~70%** (stable over 3 years).
- **Operating margin**:
  - **FY2023**: 23.6%.
  - **FY2024**: 16.2%.
  - **FY2025**: 24.7%.
  - **Volatility driven by Humira’s decline** (high-margin drug) and **Skyrizi/Rinvoq’s ramp-up** (lower initial margins due to launch costs).
- **Segment margins**:
  - **Immunology**: **~50% operating margin** (highest in the portfolio).
  - **Neuroscience**: **~35% operating margin** (Botox Therapeutic is highly profitable).
  - **Oncology**: **~25% operating margin** (lower due to Imbruvica’s decline).
  - **Aesthetics**: **~20% operating margin** (lower due to SG&A spend).
- **Operating leverage**: **Positive**—SG&A as % of revenue **declined from 27% in FY2023 to 23% in FY2025** as Skyrizi/Rinvoq scaled.

## Capital Allocation & Strategy
- **Priorities (per MD&A)**:
  1. **R&D investment**: **$7.2B in FY2025 (~12% of revenue)**, focused on **immunology, neuroscience, and oncology**.
  2. **Dividends**: **$11.6B paid in FY2025 (~19% of revenue)**, with a **4.5% yield** and **52 consecutive years of dividend increases**.
  3. **Share buybacks**: **$5B repurchased in FY2025**, with **$10B remaining under authorization**.
  4. **M&A**: **No large deals**, but **bolt-on acquisitions** (e.g., ImmunoGen for Elahere) to fill pipeline gaps.
- **R&D pipeline**:
  - **Immunology**: ABBV-3373 (lupus), ABBV-599 (psoriasis).
  - **Neuroscience**: ABBV-951 (Parkinson’s), ABBV-127 (Alzheimer’s).
  - **Oncology**: ABBV-383 (multiple myeloma), ABBV-637 (ADC for solid tumors).
- **Financial targets**:
  - **2025–2027**: **Mid-single-digit revenue growth** (Skyrizi/Rinvoq offsetting Humira’s decline).
  - **2028+**: **High-single-digit growth** as pipeline assets (e.g., ABBV-3373) launch.

## Key Risks
1. **Skyrizi/Rinvoq competition**: **Johnson & Johnson’s Tremfya (IL-23) and Pfizer’s Xeljanz (JAK)** are gaining share, with **payer pressure** expected to accelerate.
2. **Humira biosimilars**: **International markets (EU, Japan) face biosimilar entry in 2026–2028**, which could **erode $3B+ in annual revenue**.
3. **Neuroscience patent cliffs**: **Vraylar’s key patents expire in 2027–2029**, with **generic entry likely by 2030**.
4. **Pricing pressure**: **U.S. net pricing for immunology drugs declined ~5% YoY in FY2025**, with **Medicare negotiation (IRA)** adding further pressure.
