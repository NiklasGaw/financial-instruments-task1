# Procter & Gamble Co. (PG) — Business Model Analysis

## Executive Summary
Procter & Gamble (PG) is the world’s largest consumer packaged goods company, selling everyday essentials like Tide (detergent), Gillette (razors), Pampers (diapers), and Crest (toothpaste) to 5 billion consumers across 180 countries. The company makes money by selling high-margin branded products through retail channels, leveraging its unmatched scale, brand equity, and innovation pipeline. The single most important thing to know right now is that PG is a **margin-driven, cash-generative franchise** where **Fabric & Home Care (35% of revenue, 37% of operating income in FY2025) and Baby, Feminine & Family Care (24% of revenue, 25% of operating income) carry the business**, while growth is increasingly concentrated in **Health Care (14% of revenue, 15% of operating income, +1.7% YoY in FY2025)**.

---

## Business Model
PG sells **branded consumer staples**—products with inelastic demand (e.g., diapers, toothpaste, detergent)—to households globally. The unit economics are built on **high-margin, repeat-purchase transactions**: a typical U.S. household spends ~$1,000/year on PG products, with gross margins of **51% (FY2025)** and operating margins of **24% (FY2025)**. Value is captured through **brand premiums** (e.g., Tide commands a 20% price premium over private-label detergents) and **scale advantages** (PG’s $84B in revenue dwarfs competitors like Unilever’s $65B or Colgate-Palmolive’s $20B).

PG’s economic moat is **multi-layered**:
1. **Brand equity**: 20+ billion-dollar brands (e.g., Pampers, Gillette, Always) with 90%+ household penetration in key markets.
2. **Scale**: $2.1B in R&D (FY2025) and $22.7B in SG&A (FY2025) are spread across 65 manufacturing plants and 180 markets, creating cost advantages competitors cannot match.
3. **Switching costs**: Consumers rarely switch brands for staples (e.g., Pampers’ 30% global diaper market share has held steady for decades).
4. **Distribution**: PG’s products are sold in **90% of U.S. households** and **5 million retail outlets globally**, with direct relationships with Walmart (15% of revenue), Amazon, and other major retailers.

## Segment Deep Dive

### Fabric & Home Care
**What it does**: Sells laundry detergents (Tide, Ariel), fabric softeners (Downy), dishwashing liquids (Dawn), and surface cleaners (Mr. Clean).
- **FY2025**: $29.6B revenue (35% of total), $7.5B operating income (37% of total), 25.3% operating margin.
- **3-year revenue CAGR**: +2.0% (FY2023–FY2025).
- **Key drivers**: Premiumization (Tide Hygienic Clean, Dawn Powerwash) and emerging markets (Ariel in India grew double-digits in FY2025). MD&A notes: *"Fabric Care delivered mid-single-digit organic sales growth, driven by pricing and mix, partially offset by volume declines in developed markets."*
- **Risks**: Private-label competition in developed markets (e.g., Walmart’s Great Value brand) and commodity cost volatility (e.g., palm oil for surfactants).

### Baby, Feminine & Family Care
**What it does**: Sells diapers (Pampers), feminine hygiene products (Always, Tampax), and paper towels (Bounty).
- **FY2025**: $20.2B revenue (24% of total), $5.2B operating income (25% of total), 25.7% operating margin.
- **3-year revenue CAGR**: +0.1% (FY2023–FY2025).
- **Key drivers**: Pampers’ market share gains in China (+200bps in FY2025) and premium innovation (Always Discreet for incontinence). MD&A: *"Baby Care grew low-single-digits, with strong performance in Greater China offset by volume declines in North America."*
- **Risks**: Birth rate declines in developed markets (U.S. birth rate fell 2% in 2024) and private-label competition in feminine care.

### Health Care
**What it does**: Sells oral care (Crest, Oral-B), vitamins (Metamucil), and respiratory products (Vicks).
- **FY2025**: $12.0B revenue (14% of total), $3.1B operating income (15% of total), 25.8% operating margin.
- **3-year revenue CAGR**: +3.6% (FY2023–FY2025).
- **Key drivers**: Oral-B iO electric toothbrush (launched in 2020, now a $1B+ brand) and Crest Whitening Emulsions. MD&A: *"Health Care delivered high-single-digit organic sales growth, led by Oral Care and Personal Health Care."*
- **Risks**: Competition from Colgate-Palmolive (50% global toothpaste share vs. PG’s 30%) and regulatory scrutiny on health claims (e.g., FDA warnings on whitening products).

### Beauty
**What it does**: Sells hair care (Pantene, Head & Shoulders), skin care (Olay, SK-II), and deodorants (Old Spice, Secret).
- **FY2025**: $15.0B revenue (18% of total), $3.5B operating income (17% of total), 23.3% operating margin.
- **3-year revenue CAGR**: -0.2% (FY2023–FY2025).
- **Key drivers**: SK-II (a $3B brand) growth in China (+15% in FY2025) and Olay’s premiumization (Regenerist line). MD&A: *"Beauty organic sales declined low-single-digits, with growth in Skin Care offset by declines in Hair Care."*
- **Risks**: High competition in hair care (Unilever’s Dove, L’Oréal) and SK-II’s dependence on China (30% of sales).

### Grooming
**What it does**: Sells razors (Gillette, Venus), shaving cream, and electric appliances (Braun).
- **FY2025**: $6.7B revenue (8% of total), $2.0B operating income (10% of total), 29.9% operating margin.
- **3-year revenue CAGR**: +1.1% (FY2023–FY2025).
- **Key drivers**: Gillette Labs (launched in 2023, +20% YoY in FY2025) and Venus’ market share gains in women’s razors. MD&A: *"Grooming organic sales grew low-single-digits, driven by innovation and pricing."*
- **Risks**: Direct-to-consumer disruption (Harry’s, Dollar Shave Club) and declining shaving frequency (U.S. men shave 3.5x/week vs. 5x in 2010).

## Growth Trajectory
- **Total revenue 3-year trend**: +1.7% CAGR (FY2023: $82.0B → FY2025: $84.3B).
- **Growth drivers**:
  - **Structural**: Health Care (+3.6% CAGR) and premium innovation (e.g., Oral-B iO, Tide Hygienic Clean).
  - **Cyclical**: Emerging market recovery (China +8% in FY2025) and commodity cost deflation (palm oil prices fell 15% in 2024).
- **Declines**: Beauty (-0.2% CAGR) and Baby, Feminine & Family Care (-0.1% CAGR) due to developed-market volume softness.
- **M&A**: No major deals in the period; PG divested its prescription drug business in 2021.

---

## Margin Profile
- **Gross margin**: 51.1% (FY2025), stable over 3 years (FY2023: 47.8%).
- **Operating margin**: 24.3% (FY2025), up from 22.1% in FY2023, driven by **pricing power** (PG raised prices 5% in FY2024) and **productivity savings** ($1.5B in FY2025).
- **Segment margins**:
  - **Highest**: Grooming (29.9%), Health Care (25.8%).
  - **Lowest**: Beauty (23.3%), reflecting higher marketing spend (20% of revenue vs. 12% for Fabric & Home Care).
- **Operating leverage**: Positive—SG&A as % of revenue fell from 28.2% (FY2023) to 26.9% (FY2025).

## Capital Allocation & Strategy
**Priorities (per MD&A)**:
1. **Dividends**: 67 consecutive years of increases; $9.9B paid in FY2025 (62% of net income).
2. **Buybacks**: $6.0B repurchased in FY2025 (4% of shares outstanding).
3. **R&D**: $2.1B (2.5% of revenue in FY2025), focused on **premium innovation** (e.g., Tide Hygienic Clean, Oral-B iO).
4. **Capex**: $3.5B (4.2% of revenue in FY2025), primarily for **supply chain automation** and **sustainability** (e.g., renewable energy projects).
5. **Outlook**: Management targets **"balanced growth"** (organic sales +3–4%, EPS +4–9% annually) with **margin expansion** (200–300bps by 2027).

---

## Key Risks
1. **Macroeconomic sensitivity**: 55% of revenue is outside the U.S.; FX headwinds (e.g., strong USD in 2024) can compress margins.
2. **Private-label competition**: Walmart’s Great Value and Amazon Basics are gaining share in diapers and detergents.
3. **China dependence**: 10% of revenue (SK-II, Pampers) is exposed to geopolitical risks (e.g., boycotts, tariffs).
4. **Innovation risk**: Failure to launch successful premium products (e.g., Gillette’s Fusion ProGlide flop in 2010) could erode margins.
5. **Regulatory scrutiny**: FDA warnings on whitening products (Crest) and ESG mandates (e.g., EU plastic packaging taxes).
