# Merck & Co. Inc. (MRK) — Business Model Analysis

## Executive Summary
Merck & Co. Inc. (MRK) is a global biopharmaceutical company that discovers, develops, manufactures, and markets human and animal health products. The company generates revenue primarily through the sale of patented pharmaceuticals (90% of FY2025 revenue) and animal health products (10%). **The single most important thing to know is that Merck’s business is overwhelmingly dependent on Keytruda (55% of Pharmaceutical segment revenue in FY2025), whose growth trajectory and patent cliff (U.S. exclusivity expires in 2028) will dictate the company’s long-term performance.**

---

## Business Model
Merck sells **high-margin, patent-protected pharmaceuticals** to healthcare providers, governments, and insurers, and **animal health products** (vaccines, parasiticides) to veterinarians and livestock producers. The unit economics are defined by **blockbuster drugs with multi-year exclusivity**, where a single product (e.g., Keytruda) can generate $30B+ in annual revenue at **~80% gross margins**.

- **Value capture**: Merck earns **gross margins of 75–80%** on pharmaceuticals (FY2025: 74.8%) and **~60% on Animal Health** (FY2025: 60.9%), driven by **IP protection, regulatory barriers, and high switching costs** for prescribers. Revenue is recognized at **list price minus rebates/discounts** (net sales), with pricing power varying by geography (stronger in U.S., weaker in Europe/China).
- **Economic moat**: Merck’s moat stems from **patent exclusivity (Keytruda’s U.S. composition-of-matter patent expires in 2028)**, **scale in R&D ($15.8B in FY2025, 24% of revenue)**, and **regulatory expertise** (e.g., FDA approvals for 20+ Keytruda indications). However, **biosimilar/generic competition** (e.g., Januvia’s 32.6% YoY decline in FY2024) and **pricing pressure** (e.g., China’s NRDL negotiations) erode this moat over time.

## Segment Deep Dive

### Pharmaceutical
**What it does**: Develops and sells patented human drugs, including oncology (Keytruda, Lynparza), vaccines (Gardasil), and chronic disease treatments (Januvia).
- **FY2025 revenue**: $58.1B (90% of total), +1.3% YoY.
- **3-year revenue CAGR**: 4.1% (FY2022–2025).
- **Operating income**: $45.8B (78.8% margin).

**Key growth drivers**:
- **Keytruda**: $31.7B in FY2025 (+7.4% YoY), driven by **label expansions** (e.g., adjuvant melanoma, lung cancer) and **combination therapies** (e.g., with Welireg). Management notes: *"Keytruda remains the cornerstone of our oncology portfolio, with ongoing trials in 30+ tumor types."*
- **Winrevair**: $1.4B in FY2025 (+244.6% YoY), a first-in-class PAH treatment with **$5B+ peak sales potential** (per MD&A).
- **Lynparza/Lenvima alliances**: $2.6B combined in FY2025 (+8.1% YoY), leveraging AstraZeneca’s commercialization.

**Headwinds/risks**:
- **Gardasil**: $5.2B in FY2025 (-39.1% YoY), due to **China’s VBP program** (volume-based procurement) and **competition from domestic HPV vaccines**. Management warns: *"Sales in China will not materially increase in 2026."*
- **Januvia/Janumet**: $2.5B in FY2025 (+12.2% YoY), but **long-term decline** (-25.4% CAGR FY2022–2025) as generics erode share.
- **Patent cliffs**: Keytruda’s U.S. exclusivity ends in **2028**, with biosimilars expected by 2030. Management is **diversifying with Winrevair and pipeline assets** (e.g., sotatercept for PAH).

---

### Animal Health
**What it does**: Sells vaccines, parasiticides (e.g., Bravecto), and pharmaceuticals for livestock and companion animals.
- **FY2025 revenue**: $6.9B (10% of total), flat YoY.
- **3-year revenue CAGR**: 3.6% (FY2022–2025).
- **Operating income**: $2.1B (30.4% margin).

**Key growth drivers**:
- **Companion Animal**: $2.4B in FY2024 (+5.6% YoY), led by **Bravecto** (flea/tick treatment).
- **Livestock**: $3.5B in FY2024 (+3.7% YoY), driven by **African swine fever vaccines** and **poultry health products**.

**Headwinds/risks**:
- **Cyclical demand**: Tied to **livestock prices** (e.g., pork, poultry) and **veterinary clinic traffic**.
- **Regulatory hurdles**: Vaccine approvals (e.g., U.S. FDA, EU) can delay launches.
- **Competition**: Zoetis and Elanco dominate the space, with **lower-cost generics** emerging in livestock.

---

## Growth Trajectory
- **Total revenue**: $65.0B in FY2025 (+1.3% YoY), with **3-year CAGR of 4.0%** (FY2022–2025).
- **Growth drivers**:
  - **Structural**: Keytruda’s label expansions (e.g., adjuvant melanoma) and **Winrevair’s launch** (PAH market).
  - **Cyclical**: Gardasil’s recovery in **ex-China markets** (e.g., U.S. pediatric vaccination rates).
- **Decline drivers**: Gardasil in China (-39.1% YoY in FY2025) and **Januvia’s generic erosion**.
- **M&A**: No major deals in the period, but **bolt-on acquisitions** (e.g., Prometheus Biosciences in 2023) support pipeline diversification.

**MD&A quote on outlook**:
*"Our focus remains on executing our late-stage pipeline, including Winrevair and sotatercept, while preparing for Keytruda’s patent expiration in 2028 through lifecycle management and next-generation assets."*

---

## Margin Profile
- **Gross margin**: 74.8% in FY2025 (stable vs. 76.3% in FY2022).
- **Operating margin**: 34.0% in FY2025 (vs. 5.0% in FY2023, distorted by R&D write-offs).
  - **Pharmaceutical**: 78.8% operating margin (FY2025), the **profit engine** of the company.
  - **Animal Health**: 30.4% operating margin (FY2025), lower due to **commoditized livestock products**.
- **Operating leverage**: **SG&A spend is stable** (~16% of revenue), while **R&D intensity is rising** (24% of revenue in FY2025 vs. 20% in FY2022), reflecting **pipeline investments** (e.g., Winrevair, sotatercept).

---

## Capital Allocation & Strategy
**Priorities (per MD&A)**:
1. **Organic R&D**: $15.8B in FY2025 (24% of revenue), focused on **oncology (Keytruda follow-ons), vaccines (e.g., V116 for pneumococcal disease), and cardiopulmonary (Winrevair)**.
2. **Dividends**: 11 consecutive years of increases; **3.5% yield** (FY2025).
3. **M&A**: Targeting **bolt-on deals** (e.g., $10.8B acquisition of Prometheus in 2023) to fill pipeline gaps.
4. **Buybacks**: $10B authorization in 2023; **$2.5B repurchased in FY2025**.

**Strategic initiatives**:
- **Keytruda lifecycle management**: Expanding into **adjuvant settings** (e.g., melanoma) and **combination therapies** (e.g., with Welireg).
- **China strategy**: Shifting focus from Gardasil to **oncology (Keytruda) and innovative vaccines** (e.g., V116).
- **ESG**: Committed to **carbon neutrality by 2040**, with $1B allocated to sustainability initiatives.

**Financial targets (MD&A)**:
- **2025–2027 revenue CAGR**: Mid-single digits (excluding Gardasil China).
- **Operating margin**: 35%+ (long-term).

---

## Key Risks
1. **Keytruda patent cliff**: U.S. exclusivity expires in **2028**, with biosimilars expected by 2030. **Mitigation**: Pipeline assets (e.g., Winrevair, sotatercept) and **label expansions**.
2. **China exposure**: Gardasil sales collapsed (-39.1% YoY in FY2025) due to **VBP pricing and domestic competition**. **Mitigation**: Shifting focus to **oncology (Keytruda) and innovative vaccines**.
3. **Pricing pressure**: U.S. **IRA negotiations** (e.g., Medicare price cuts) and **global austerity measures** (e.g., EU, Japan) could erode margins.
4. **Pipeline execution**: **Winrevair’s commercialization** and **sotatercept’s approval** (PAH) are critical to offsetting Keytruda’s decline.
5. **Geopolitical risks**: **U.S.-China tensions** could disrupt supply chains (e.g., Wuxi Apptech) or **market access** (e.g., NRDL negotiations).
