# **Lam Research Corp. (LRCX) — Business Model Analysis**

## **Executive Summary**
Lam Research is a leading supplier of **wafer fabrication equipment and services** to the semiconductor industry, specializing in **etch, deposition, and clean processes** critical for advanced chip manufacturing. The company generates revenue through **high-margin equipment sales** and **recurring customer support services**, with **~70% of FY2025 revenue ($18.4B) coming from systems sales** and the remainder from services. The most important takeaway is **LRCX’s structural growth tailwinds**—driven by **AI, 3D scaling, and memory recovery**—but its **cyclical exposure to semiconductor capex** remains a key risk.

---

## **Business Model**
### **Products & Customers**
LRCX sells **semiconductor manufacturing equipment** (primarily **etch, deposition, and clean tools**) and **customer support services** (spare parts, upgrades, and process optimization) to **leading chipmakers** (e.g., TSMC, Samsung, SK Hynix). Its tools are used in **logic, DRAM, and NAND memory** production, with **AI-driven demand** (e.g., high-bandwidth memory, advanced logic nodes) driving recent growth.

### **Unit Economics**
- **Equipment Sales**: High-ticket transactions ($5M–$20M per tool) with **gross margins ~45–50%** (FY2025: 48.7%). Customers commit to multi-year capex plans, but orders are **lumpy and cyclical**.
- **Customer Support**: Recurring revenue (~30% of total) with **higher margins (~55–60%)** and **lower volatility**. Services include **spare parts, upgrades, and process optimization**, tied to LRCX’s **installed base of ~100,000 tools**.

### **Value Capture**
- **Hardware Margins**: LRCX earns **~50% gross margins** on equipment, leveraging **IP, scale, and customer lock-in** (switching costs are high due to tool qualification requirements).
- **Service Annuitization**: Post-sale services generate **stable cash flows** and **higher margins** than equipment, with **FY2025 support revenue growing 12.8% YoY** to $5.9B.
- **R&D as Moat**: **~11% of revenue** (FY2025: $2.1B) is reinvested in R&D to maintain **technological leadership** in **3D scaling, EUV patterning, and advanced materials**.

### **Economic Moat**
1. **Switching Costs**: Customers face **high costs** to requalify alternative tools, creating **stickiness** (e.g., TSMC’s 3nm node relies on LRCX’s etch tools).
2. **Scale & IP**: LRCX holds **~3,000 patents** and benefits from **economies of scale** in R&D and manufacturing (e.g., **Korea and Malaysia hubs**).
3. **Customer Lock-In**: **~70% of revenue** comes from **top 5 customers** (TSMC, Samsung, etc.), but concentration risk is mitigated by **long-term partnerships**.
4. **Secular Demand**: **AI, 5G, and automotive chips** require **advanced packaging and 3D scaling**, where LRCX leads in **etch and deposition**.

## **Segment Deep Dive**
### **1. Systems (Semiconductor Equipment)**
- **What it does**: Sells **etch, deposition, and clean tools** for wafer fabrication. **Etch** (removing material) and **deposition** (adding material) are critical for **3D scaling** (e.g., gate-all-around transistors, 3D NAND).
- **FY2025 Revenue**: **$12.5B (68% of total)**, +**48% YoY** (Q1 FY2026: $3.5B).
- **3-Year Trajectory**: **FY2023–FY2025 CAGR: 13.5%**, driven by **memory recovery (NAND/DRAM) and AI logic demand**.
- **Operating Income**: **~35% margin** (FY2025: $4.3B OI on $12.5B revenue).
- **Growth Drivers** (per MD&A):
  - **"AI-driven demand for high-bandwidth memory (HBM) and advanced logic nodes"** (e.g., TSMC’s 3nm/2nm).
  - **"3D scaling inflections"** (e.g., **gate-all-around transistors, 3D NAND stacking**).
  - **"Memory recovery"** (Samsung/SK Hynix ramping DRAM/NAND capex).
- **Risks**:
  - **Cyclicality**: **FY2024 revenue fell 14.5% YoY** due to memory downturn.
  - **Customer Concentration**: **Top 2 customers (TSMC, Samsung) = ~50% of revenue**.
  - **Geopolitical Risks**: **U.S. export controls** on China could limit ~**20% of revenue** (per prior filings).

### **2. Customer Support-Related Revenue & Other**
- **What it does**: Provides **spare parts, upgrades, and process optimization services** for LRCX’s installed base.
- **FY2025 Revenue**: **$5.9B (32% of total)**, +**12.8% YoY** (Q3 FY2026: $5.9B).
- **3-Year Trajectory**: **FY2023–FY2025 CAGR: 8.2%**, driven by **installed base growth** and **higher tool utilization**.
- **Operating Income**: **~55–60% margin** (not separately disclosed, but **higher than systems**).
- **Growth Drivers** (per MD&A):
  - **"Maximizing installed equipment performance"** (e.g., **predictive maintenance, upgrades**).
  - **"Operational efficiency services"** (e.g., **process optimization for AI chips**).
- **Risks**:
  - **Dependence on Installed Base**: Growth slows if **new tool sales decline** (e.g., FY2024).
  - **Pricing Pressure**: Customers may **delay upgrades** during downturns.
