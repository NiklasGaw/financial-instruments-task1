# Novo Nordisk A/S (NOVO-B.CO) — Business Model Analysis

## Executive Summary
Novo Nordisk is a global leader in diabetes, obesity, and rare disease therapeutics, generating **$34.1B in FY2025 revenue (+31% YoY)**. The company’s economic engine is its **GLP-1 receptor agonist franchise (Ozempic, Wegovy, Rybelsus)**, which accounted for **$33.6B (98% of total revenue)** in FY2025. The single most important dynamic is the **structural demand tailwind for obesity and diabetes treatments**, offset by **intensifying competition (Eli Lilly’s Zepbound/Mounjaro) and pricing pressure in the U.S. (Medicare negotiations, PBM rebates)**.

---

## Business Model
Novo Nordisk sells **high-margin injectable and oral therapeutics** to patients via healthcare providers and payers (insurers, governments). Key products:
- **GLP-1 agonists**: Ozempic ($18.7B in FY2025, +36% YoY for Wegovy), Saxenda, Rybelsus.
- **Insulins**: Modern (e.g., Tresiba) and human (e.g., Levemir) insulins.
- **Rare disease**: Growth hormone (Norditropin) and hemophilia (NovoSeven) therapies.

**Unit economics**:
- **Recurring revenue model**: Patients require lifelong treatment (e.g., diabetes, obesity), driving **~90% gross margins** on GLP-1 drugs.
- **Pricing power**: List prices for Ozempic/Wegovy exceed **$1,000/month** in the U.S., though net prices are lower due to rebates.
- **Moats**:
  - **IP protection**: Patents on semaglutide (Ozempic/Wegovy) expire in **2031–2032 (U.S.)**, but formulation patents extend exclusivity.
  - **Manufacturing scale**: Novo Nordisk’s **$10B+ capex program** (2023–2026) expands production capacity to meet demand.
  - **Brand loyalty**: Ozempic/Wegovy are **#1 prescribed GLP-1s globally**, though Lilly’s Zepbound gained U.S. share in 2025.
  - **Regulatory barriers**: FDA/EMA approvals require **10+ years of clinical trials**, limiting competition.

## Segment Deep Dive

### Obesity and Diabetes Care
**What it does**: Develops and markets GLP-1 agonists (Ozempic, Wegovy), insulins, and other diabetes/obesity drugs.
- **FY2025 revenue**: $34.5B (**98% of total**).
- **3-year CAGR**: **36%** (FY2022–FY2025).
- **Operating margin**: ~55% (implied by $18.9B operating income on $34.1B revenue).

**Key drivers**:
- **Wegovy (obesity)**: $11.6B in FY2025 (+36% YoY), driven by **U.S. demand (70% of sales)** and launches in **50+ countries**.
- **Ozempic (diabetes)**: $18.7B in FY2025, though **U.S. volume share declined to Lilly in 2025** (per MD&A).
- **Rybelsus (oral GLP-1)**: $3.3B in FY2025, growing as a **lower-cost alternative** to injectables.

**Risks**:
- **Competition**: Lilly’s Zepbound (tirzepatide) gained **U.S. obesity market share** in 2025 (MD&A: "Wegovy’s leading position was overtaken").
- **Pricing pressure**: U.S. Medicare negotiations (Inflation Reduction Act) will cut prices by **~30% for Ozempic/Wegovy in 2027**.
- **Supply constraints**: Periodic shortages in 2024–2025 due to **manufacturing bottlenecks** (MD&A: "Supply situation improved in 2025").
- **Compounding pharmacies**: Unapproved copies of GLP-1s eroded **~$1B in U.S. sales** in 2025 (MD&A).

---

### Rare Disease
**What it does**: Sells growth hormone (Norditropin) and hemophilia (NovoSeven) therapies.
- **FY2025 revenue**: ~$2.9B (**8% of total**).
- **3-year CAGR**: **18%** (FY2022–FY2025).
- **Operating margin**: ~40% (implied by $0.9B operating income on $2.1B revenue in FY2023).

**Key drivers**:
- **Growth hormone**: Norditropin grew **5% YoY** in FY2023, driven by **pediatric and adult growth disorders**.
- **Hemophilia**: NovoSeven grew **10% YoY** in FY2023, benefiting from **new indications**.

**Risks**:
- **Small market**: Limited addressable population (~**500K patients globally**).
- **Competition**: Roche’s Hemlibra (hemophilia) and Pfizer’s Genotropin (growth hormone) pressure pricing.

## Growth Trajectory
- **Total revenue**: **$34.1B in FY2025 (+31% YoY)**, up from **$25.7B in FY2023 (3-year CAGR: 30%)**.
- **Segment drivers**:
  - **Obesity/Diabetes**: +36% CAGR (FY2022–2025), driven by **GLP-1s (50% YoY growth in FY2023)**.
  - **Rare Disease**: +18% CAGR, but **immaterial to overall growth**.
- **Structural vs. cyclical**:
  - **Structural**: **Obesity/diabetes prevalence** (1 in 10 adults globally) and **GLP-1 adoption** (only **5% of eligible patients** treated).
  - **Cyclical**: **U.S. pricing pressure** (Medicare negotiations, PBM rebates) and **supply chain bottlenecks**.
- **M&A**: Acquired **Akero Therapeutics ($4.7B in Dec 2025)** for MASH (fatty liver disease) pipeline.

---

## Margin Profile
- **Gross margin**: **83.6% in FY2025** (up from 81.6% in FY2023), driven by **GLP-1 scale**.
- **Operating margin**: **55.4% in FY2025** (up from 50.1% in FY2023), reflecting **operating leverage** (SG&A grew **14% YoY vs. 31% revenue growth**).
- **Segment variation**:
  - **Obesity/Diabetes**: ~55% operating margin (highest).
  - **Rare Disease**: ~40% operating margin (lower due to **higher R&D spend**).

---

## Capital Allocation & Strategy
**Priorities (per MD&A)**:
1. **Organic growth**: **$10B+ capex (2023–2026)** to expand **GLP-1 manufacturing** (e.g., new Denmark/NC plants).
2. **M&A**: Acquired **Akero Therapeutics ($4.7B)** to enter **MASH market**.
3. **R&D**: **$4.8B in FY2025 (14% of revenue)**, focused on **next-gen GLP-1s (e.g., oral semaglutide)** and **obesity pipeline**.
4. **Dividends**: **DKK 11.5B paid in 2025 (~3% yield)**, with **payout ratio of 50% of net income**.

**Outlook (MD&A)**:
- **2026 guidance**: **Low-double-digit revenue growth** (slower than 2025 due to **U.S. pricing pressure**).
- **Long-term targets**: **Sustain 10%+ revenue growth** via **GLP-1 expansion** and **new indications (e.g., MASH, Alzheimer’s)**.

---

## Key Risks
1. **Competition**: Lilly’s **Zepbound (tirzepatide)** gained U.S. obesity share in 2025, and **biosimilars** may enter post-2031.
2. **U.S. pricing pressure**: **Medicare negotiations** will cut Ozempic/Wegovy prices by **~30% in 2027**.
3. **Supply chain**: **Manufacturing capacity constraints** could limit growth (MD&A: "Supply improved in 2025").
4. **Regulatory**: **FDA/EMA delays** for new indications (e.g., MASH) or **patent challenges** (e.g., compounding pharmacies).
5. **Cybersecurity**: **IT breaches** could disrupt production (MD&A: "Reliance on global IT systems").
