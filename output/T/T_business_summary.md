# AT&T Inc. (T) — Business Model Analysis

## Executive Summary
AT&T Inc. is a diversified telecommunications and technology holding company that generates revenue primarily through wireless mobility services, business wireline solutions, and consumer broadband. The company operates on a subscription-based model, leveraging its extensive network infrastructure (wireless, fiber, and legacy copper) to deliver connectivity and value-added services to consumers and enterprises. **The single most important takeaway is that AT&T’s Mobility segment is the profit engine (FY2025: $27.2B operating income, 30.4% margin), while its fiber expansion and 5G deployment are critical growth levers—but execution risks and legacy declines in Business Wireline pose challenges.**

---

## Business Model
AT&T monetizes connectivity through **recurring subscription fees** (wireless plans, broadband) and **equipment sales** (smartphones, routers). Key customer segments include:
- **Consumers**: Wireless mobility (postpaid/prepaid) and home broadband (fiber/copper).
- **Enterprises**: Business wireline (fiber, SD-WAN, cloud connectivity) and wireless solutions.

**Unit Economics**:
- **Wireless**: Average Revenue Per User (ARPU) for postpaid wireless was ~$55/month in FY2025 (up 3.1% YoY), with equipment sales (e.g., iPhones) generating ~$1,000/device at 10–15% gross margins.
- **Broadband**: Fiber ARPU ~$60/month (FY2025: $12.2B revenue, +8.7% YoY), with 50–60% EBITDA margins on mature builds.

**Value Capture**: AT&T’s moat stems from **scale** (160M+ wireless subscribers, 24M+ broadband locations), **switching costs** (contracts, bundled services), and **regulatory barriers** (spectrum licenses, right-of-way access). However, competition from Verizon, T-Mobile, and cable providers (e.g., Comcast) pressures pricing.

## Segment Deep Dive

### Mobility
**What it does**: Wireless voice/data services (postpaid/prepaid) and device sales (smartphones, tablets).
- **FY2025 Revenue**: $89.5B (71.2% of total), +5.0% YoY.
- **3-Year CAGR**: 3.2% (FY2023–2025).
- **Operating Income**: $27.2B (30.4% margin).
- **Drivers**:
  - **Service revenue** ($67.4B, +3.1% YoY) driven by higher ARPU and subscriber growth (postpaid net adds: +1.2M in FY2025).
  - **Equipment revenue** ($22.1B, +11.1% YoY) from device upgrades (e.g., 5G phones).
  - **MD&A**: *"Our simplified go-to-market strategy for 5G in underpenetrated markets will continue to drive wireless subscriber and service revenue growth."*
- **Risks**:
  - **Pricing pressure**: T-Mobile’s aggressive promotions (e.g., "Un-carrier" pricing) erode margins.
  - **Churn**: Postpaid churn was 0.89% in Q4 2025 (vs. 0.85% in Q4 2024), reflecting competitive intensity.

---

### Business Wireline
**What it does**: Enterprise connectivity (fiber, SD-WAN, cloud), legacy voice/data, and equipment.
- **FY2025 Revenue**: $17.2B (13.7% of total), **-8.4% YoY**.
- **3-Year CAGR**: -7.8% (FY2023–2025).
- **Operating Income**: -$0.8B (negative margin).
- **Drivers**:
  - **Fiber/advanced services** ($7.3B, +5.2% YoY) offset by **legacy declines** ($9.2B, -17.4% YoY).
  - **MD&A**: *"We are decommissioning our legacy copper network and shifting to fiber and wireless connectivity."*
- **Risks**:
  - **Legacy erosion**: Copper-based services (e.g., TDM voice) are in structural decline.
  - **Competition**: Cable providers (e.g., Comcast Business) and cloud hyperscalers (AWS, Azure) encroach on enterprise spend.

---

### Consumer Wireline
**What it does**: Home broadband (fiber/copper), legacy voice, and equipment.
- **FY2025 Revenue**: $14.2B (11.3% of total), +4.5% YoY.
- **3-Year CAGR**: 3.8% (FY2023–2025).
- **Operating Income**: $1.5B (10.6% margin).
- **Drivers**:
  - **Broadband** ($12.2B, +8.7% YoY) fueled by fiber expansion (10M+ fiber locations in FY2025).
  - **Legacy voice/data** ($1.0B, -19.9% YoY) continues to shrink.
  - **MD&A**: *"Expansion of our fiber reach will drive greater demand for broadband services."*
- **Risks**:
  - **Cable competition**: Comcast and Charter dominate non-fiber markets.
  - **Satellite broadband**: Starlink and other LEO providers threaten rural markets.

---

## Growth Trajectory
- **Total Revenue**: FY2025: $125.6B (+2.7% YoY); 3-year CAGR: 1.3% (FY2023–2025).
- **Growth Drivers**:
  - **Mobility**: 5G adoption and converged bundles (wireless + broadband).
  - **Fiber**: 10M+ fiber locations by FY2025 (up from 6M in FY2023), driving broadband ARPU growth.
- **Structural vs. Cyclical**:
  - **Structural**: Fiber expansion and 5G are secular trends, but legacy declines (copper, TDM) are permanent.
  - **Cyclical**: Equipment sales (e.g., iPhone cycles) and enterprise spending (recession-sensitive).
- **M&A**: Recent spectrum acquisitions (e.g., 3.45 GHz auction) and fiber builds (e.g., BlackRock JV) support growth.

---

## Margin Profile
- **Operating Margin**: FY2025: 19.3% (vs. 15.6% in FY2024).
- **Segment Margins**:
  - **Mobility**: 30.4% (franchise segment).
  - **Consumer Wireline**: 10.6% (fiber-driven improvement).
  - **Business Wireline**: Negative (legacy drag).
- **Operating Leverage**: Efficiency gains from IT modernization and AI-driven cost cuts (e.g., network automation) are offset by fiber/5G capex.

---

## Capital Allocation & Strategy
- **Priorities (MD&A)**:
  1. **Capex**: $24B in FY2025 (fiber, 5G, Open RAN).
  2. **Dividends**: $1.11/share in FY2025 (6.5% yield).
  3. **Buybacks**: $10B authorization in 2024 (72M shares repurchased in Q4 2025).
  4. **Debt Reduction**: Targeting $130B gross debt by 2026 (down from $169B in 2022).
- **R&D**: ~$1B/year (focused on Open RAN, AI, and cloud-native networks).
- **Outlook**: *"We expect revenue growth in wireless and broadband as customers demand higher speeds and converged services."*

---

## Key Risks
1. **Cybersecurity**: *"Cyberattacks could have a material adverse effect on operations"* (e.g., 2024 call data breach).
2. **Legacy Decline**: Business Wireline’s copper erosion outpaces fiber growth.
3. **Competition**: T-Mobile’s pricing aggression and cable’s broadband dominance.
4. **Debt Load**: $127B long-term debt (FY2025) limits financial flexibility.
5. **Regulation**: Spectrum auctions, net neutrality, and state-level broadband mandates.
