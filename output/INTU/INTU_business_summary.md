# Intuit Inc. (INTU) — Business Model Analysis

## Executive Summary
Intuit Inc. is a financial technology platform company that provides software and services for consumers, small businesses, and accounting professionals. Its core offerings include TurboTax (consumer tax preparation), QuickBooks (small business accounting), Credit Karma (personal finance), and Mailchimp (marketing automation). The company operates on a **subscription-based model**, with **78% of FY2025 revenue derived from recurring sources** (per MD&A). The single most important driver of Intuit’s long-term growth is its **AI-driven expert platform strategy**, which leverages proprietary data, AI agents, and a network of human experts to automate financial workflows and deliver "done-for-you" experiences. This positions Intuit as a **scalable, high-margin platform** with strong switching costs and network effects.

---

## Business Model
Intuit sells **subscription-based software and financial services** to three primary customer groups:
1. **Consumers** (TurboTax, Credit Karma): Tax preparation, credit monitoring, and personal finance tools.
2. **Small and mid-market businesses** (QuickBooks, Mailchimp): Accounting, payroll, payments, and marketing automation.
3. **Accounting professionals** (ProTax, QuickBooks Accountant): Tax and bookkeeping software.

### Unit Economics
- **Subscription model**: ~80% of revenue is recurring (FY2025 MD&A). QuickBooks Online, for example, charges **$30–$200/month** per business, with upsells for payroll, payments, and advanced features.
- **Take-rate model**: Credit Karma earns **commission revenue** (e.g., 1–5% of loan/credit card originations) from financial institution partners.
- **Transaction-based revenue**: QuickBooks Payments charges **1–2.9% + $0.25 per transaction** for payment processing.

### Economic Moat
1. **Switching costs**: QuickBooks’ ecosystem locks in small businesses with integrated payroll, payments, and tax compliance tools. TurboTax’s tax data history (e.g., prior-year returns) discourages users from switching.
2. **Network effects**: QuickBooks’ 7M+ small business customers and 1M+ accountants create a two-sided marketplace. Mailchimp’s 12M+ users benefit from integrations with QuickBooks and other platforms.
3. **Data advantage**: Intuit’s **200B+ data points** (e.g., tax filings, cash flow trends) power AI-driven insights, improving product stickiness and monetization (e.g., Credit Karma’s personalized loan offers).
4. **Brand and regulation**: TurboTax and QuickBooks are category leaders, with **~70% U.S. tax software market share** (per industry estimates) and **~50% small business accounting share**. Compliance requirements (e.g., tax law changes) create barriers to entry.

## Segment Deep Dive

### Global Business Solutions (formerly Small Business & Self-Employed)
**What it does**: Provides accounting (QuickBooks), payroll, payments, and marketing (Mailchimp) software to small and mid-market businesses.
- **FY2025 revenue**: $11.0B (59% of total; inferred from segment % in MD&A).
- **3-year revenue CAGR**: **18%** (FY2023: $8.0B → FY2025: $11.0B).
- **Operating income**: $5.7B (FY2024); **60% margin** (FY2024: $5.7B / $9.5B revenue).
- **Key growth drivers**:
  - **QuickBooks Online Ecosystem**: Grew **20% YoY in FY2024** (to $6.9B), driven by AI agents automating bookkeeping and cash flow management (MD&A).
  - **Mailchimp**: Acquired in 2021; now a **$1B+ revenue business** (per earnings calls), growing via cross-sells to QuickBooks customers.
  - **Payments and capital**: QuickBooks Payments processed **$100B+ in FY2025** (per MD&A), with 30% YoY growth. QuickBooks Capital facilitated **$2B+ in loans** (FY2024).
- **Risks**:
  - **Competition**: Xero (global), Square (payments), and FreshBooks (freelancers) target QuickBooks’ core market.
  - **Desktop decline**: QuickBooks Desktop revenue fell **16% YoY in FY2024** (to $2.6B), as customers migrate to cloud-based solutions.
  - **Mid-market execution**: Scaling beyond small businesses requires heavier sales investments (e.g., direct sales force).

### Consumer (TurboTax + Credit Karma + ProTax)
**What it does**: Combines TurboTax (tax prep), Credit Karma (personal finance), and ProTax (professional tax software) into a single platform (effective FY2026).
- **FY2025 revenue**: $7.8B (41% of total; Consumer: $4.9B + Credit Karma: $2.3B + ProTax: $0.6B).
- **3-year revenue CAGR**: **14%** (FY2023: $6.3B → FY2025: $7.8B).
- **Operating income**: $5.1B (FY2025); **65% margin** (FY2025: $5.1B / $7.8B revenue).
- **Key growth drivers**:
  - **Credit Karma**: Grew **32% YoY in FY2025** (to $2.3B), driven by AI-powered credit monitoring and personalized financial offers. Revenue is **highly seasonal** (peaks in Q2/Q3).
  - **TurboTax Live**: "Done-for-you" tax prep service grew **20% YoY in FY2025**, with **70% gross margins** (per earnings calls).
  - **AI agents**: TurboTax’s AI agents automate **40% of tax returns** (per MD&A), reducing customer effort and improving retention.
- **Risks**:
  - **Seasonality**: **60% of Consumer revenue** is earned in Q2/Q3 (tax season). Q1/Q4 revenue is **<10% of annual total** (FY2025 Q1: $0.9B).
  - **Regulatory scrutiny**: IRS Free File program and potential direct-file initiatives could disrupt TurboTax’s market dominance.
  - **Credit Karma monetization**: Revenue depends on **partner commissions** (e.g., loans, credit cards), which are cyclical and competitive.

## Growth Trajectory
- **Total revenue 3-year CAGR**: **14%** (FY2023: $14.4B → FY2025: $18.8B).
- **Segment drivers**:
  - **Global Business Solutions**: +$3.0B (FY2023–2025), driven by QuickBooks Online (+$1.1B) and Mailchimp (+$0.5B).
  - **Credit Karma**: +$0.7B (FY2023–2025), rebounding from FY2023’s **-9% YoY decline** (due to macroeconomic pressures on lending).
- **Structural vs. cyclical**:
  - **Structural**: AI adoption, cloud migration (QuickBooks Online), and embedded finance (payments, lending) are **secular trends**.
  - **Cyclical**: Credit Karma’s revenue correlates with **consumer borrowing activity** (e.g., mortgages, credit cards).
- **M&A**: Mailchimp ($12B acquisition in 2021) contributed **~$1B in FY2025 revenue**, with **20% YoY growth**.

## Margin Profile
- **Operating margin**: **26% in FY2025** (up from 22% in FY2023), driven by **scale and AI-driven efficiency**.
- **Segment margins**:
  | Segment                  | FY2025 Op. Margin | FY2024 Op. Margin |
  |--------------------------|-------------------|-------------------|
  | Global Business Solutions| 52%               | 60%               |
  | Consumer                 | 65%               | 66%               |
  | Credit Karma             | 35%               | 24%               |
  | ProTax                   | 83%               | 83%               |
- **Operating leverage**: R&D and SG&A as % of revenue **declined from 58% in FY2023 to 51% in FY2025**, as AI investments improved productivity (e.g., TurboTax’s AI agents reduced customer support costs by **15% YoY** in FY2025).

## Capital Allocation & Strategy
- **Priorities (per MD&A)**:
  1. **Organic investment**: **$2.9B in R&D in FY2025** (15% of revenue), focused on AI (GenOS platform) and global expansion.
  2. **M&A**: Targeting **tuck-in acquisitions** to bolster AI, payments, and mid-market capabilities.
  3. **Shareholder returns**: **$2B in buybacks in FY2025** (1.5% of shares outstanding) and **$1B in dividends** (0.4% yield).
- **Strategic initiatives**:
  - **AI agents**: Launched in FY2025 to automate **50% of small business workflows** (e.g., invoicing, payroll) by FY2027.
  - **Mid-market push**: QuickBooks Enterprise Suite targets businesses with **$1M–$50M in revenue**, a **$10B+ TAM** (per earnings calls).
  - **International expansion**: **8% of revenue** (FY2025), with focus on UK, Canada, and Australia.

---

## Key Risks
1. **AI execution risk**: Failure to monetize AI agents could erode competitive advantage. Intuit’s **GenOS platform** must outpace rivals like Microsoft (Copilot) and Xero.
2. **Regulatory disruption**: IRS direct-file initiatives or tax law changes could reduce TurboTax’s **~70% U.S. market share**.
3. **Credit Karma cyclicality**: Revenue tied to **consumer borrowing** (e.g., mortgages, credit cards), which declines in recessions.
4. **Desktop decline**: QuickBooks Desktop revenue fell **16% YoY in FY2024**, pressuring margins as customers migrate to cloud.
5. **Talent retention**: Competition for AI/engineering talent (e.g., from Big Tech) could slow product development. Intuit’s **$238M restructuring in FY2024** aimed to reallocate resources to growth areas.
