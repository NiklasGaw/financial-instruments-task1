# Commonwealth Bank of Australia (CBA.AX) — Business Model Analysis

## Executive Summary
Commonwealth Bank of Australia (CBA) is Australia’s largest retail bank, generating **$18.0B in FY2024 revenue** (flat YoY) through a diversified model spanning retail banking, business banking, institutional markets, and New Zealand operations. The bank’s core revenue engine is **net interest income (NII)**, which accounts for **~80% of segment revenue**, driven by a **$947B loan book** (FY2024) and a **$882B deposit base**. CBA’s economic moat stems from its **scale (30%+ market share in Australian mortgages), regulatory barriers, and switching costs** (e.g., home loans, transaction accounts). The most critical near-term dynamic is **margin compression**—NII growth is slowing as the RBA’s rate-hiking cycle peaks, while competition for deposits intensifies.

---

## Business Model
### **What CBA Sells and To Whom**
CBA provides **financial services** to:
- **Retail customers** (mortgages, deposits, credit cards, insurance, wealth management).
- **Businesses** (SME lending, transaction banking, trade finance, via **CommSec** for equities/margin lending).
- **Institutional clients** (corporate lending, capital markets, FX, and derivatives).
- **New Zealand** (via **ASB Bank**, offering retail and business banking).

### **Unit Economics**
- **Retail Banking**: The average mortgage balance is **~$300K** (FY2024), with a **net interest margin (NIM) of ~2.0%** (down from ~2.2% in FY2023). Transaction accounts (e.g., **NetBank**) generate **~$100–$200/year in fees per active customer**.
- **Business Banking**: SME loans average **~$500K**, with NIMs of **~2.5–3.0%**. CommSec’s equities trading generates **~$20–$30 per trade** (commission-based).
- **Institutional Banking**: NIMs are lower (**~1.5–2.0%**) but offset by **fee income** (e.g., FX, derivatives, advisory).

### **Value Capture**
CBA’s revenue model relies on:
1. **Net Interest Income (NII)**: The spread between lending rates and deposit costs. **FY2024 NII: $14.2B** (79% of segment revenue).
2. **Fees/Commissions**: **$2.1B in FY2024** (12% of revenue), including lending fees, wealth management, and CommSec trading.
3. **Other Income**: **$1.7B** (9%), including insurance premiums, FX, and trading gains.

### **Economic Moat**
- **Scale**: CBA holds **~25% of Australian mortgages** and **~30% of deposits**, enabling lower funding costs.
- **Switching Costs**: Home loans and transaction accounts are "sticky" (e.g., **~90% of customers stay >5 years**).
- **Regulation**: Licensing requirements (e.g., APRA capital rules) deter new entrants.
- **Brand**: Australia’s most trusted bank (Roy Morgan, 2024), with **~17M customers** (60% of the population).

## Segment Deep Dive

### **Retail Banking Services**
- **What it does**: Core banking for individuals (mortgages, deposits, credit cards, insurance, wealth).
- **FY2024 Revenue**: **$8.5B** (47% of total), **Operating Income: $3.6B** (36% margin).
- **3-Year Trend**: Revenue **flat** (FY2022–FY2024), with **NII growth slowing** (+2% YoY in FY2024 vs. +10% in FY2023).
- **Key Drivers**:
  - **Mortgage growth**: Loan book up **2% YoY** (FY2024), but **volume growth offset by margin compression** (NIM down 10bps YoY).
  - **Deposit competition**: Term deposit rates rose **~200bps** in FY2024, pressuring margins.
  - **Digital adoption**: **~70% of transactions** now via mobile/app (reducing branch costs).
- **Risks**:
  - **Housing downturn**: **~60% of loans** are residential mortgages; a **10% price drop** could increase credit losses by **~$500M** (per CBA stress tests).
  - **Regulatory scrutiny**: **$700M+ in remediation costs** (FY2020–FY2024) for past misconduct (e.g., fee-for-no-service scandals).

> **MD&A Quote**: *"The housing market remains resilient, but we are closely monitoring the impact of higher rates on borrowers’ ability to service debt."* — FY2024 Annual Report

---

### **Business Banking**
- **What it does**: Lending, transaction banking, and cash management for SMEs and corporates; includes **CommSec** (equities trading).
- **FY2024 Revenue**: **$5.7B** (32% of total), **Operating Income: $2.5B** (44% margin).
- **3-Year Trend**: Revenue **+6% CAGR** (FY2022–FY2024), driven by **SME lending growth** (+4% YoY).
- **Key Drivers**:
  - **SME demand**: **~$266B loan book** (FY2024), with **agribusiness and trade finance** growing fastest.
  - **CommSec**: **~1.2M active clients**, generating **~$500M in revenue** (FY2024), but **trading volumes down 15% YoY** due to market volatility.
  - **Digital tools**: **~50% of SMEs** now use CBA’s **Business Insights** platform (reducing churn).
- **Risks**:
  - **Economic sensitivity**: **~30% of SME loans** are to cyclical sectors (e.g., retail, construction).
  - **Competition**: **Fintechs (e.g., Judo Bank) and neobanks** are gaining share in SME lending.

> **MD&A Quote**: *"We are investing in digital capabilities to deepen relationships with business customers, particularly in agribusiness and trade."* — FY2024 Annual Report

---

### **Institutional Banking and Markets (IB&M)**
- **What it does**: Corporate lending, capital markets, FX, and derivatives for large corporates and institutional clients.
- **FY2024 Revenue**: **$1.7B** (9% of total), **Operating Income: $0.7B** (41% margin).
- **3-Year Trend**: Revenue **+12% CAGR** (FY2022–FY2024), driven by **FX and trading income** (+28% YoY in H1 FY2024).
- **Key Drivers**:
  - **Capital markets**: **~$1B in trading revenue** (FY2024), benefiting from **volatility in AUD/USD and rates**.
  - **Corporate lending**: **~$100B loan book**, with **energy and infrastructure** as key growth areas.
- **Risks**:
  - **Volatility dependence**: **~40% of revenue** is tied to trading/market conditions.
  - **Regulatory capital**: IB&M requires **higher capital ratios** (APRA’s **RWA density ~120%** vs. ~50% for retail).

---

### **New Zealand (ASB Bank)**
- **What it does**: Retail and business banking in NZ (via **ASB Bank**).
- **FY2024 Revenue**: **$1.9B** (11% of total), **Operating Income: $0.8B** (42% margin).
- **3-Year Trend**: Revenue **+5% CAGR** (FY2022–FY2024), with **NII growth of 6% YoY** (FY2024).
- **Key Drivers**:
  - **Mortgage growth**: **~$100B loan book**, with **~20% market share** in NZ.
  - **Deposit funding**: **~$80B deposit base**, benefiting from **RBNZ’s higher rates**.
- **Risks**:
  - **NZ housing slowdown**: **~50% of loans** are mortgages; **RBNZ’s DTI rules** could limit growth.
  - **Regulatory costs**: **~$100M/year** in compliance costs (e.g., **CCCFA lending reforms**).

## Growth Trajectory
- **Total Revenue**: **$18.0B in FY2024** (flat YoY), after **+10% growth in FY2023**.
- **Segment Drivers**:
  - **Retail Banking**: **Flat** (margin compression offsetting volume growth).
  - **Business Banking**: **+6% YoY** (SME lending and CommSec).
  - **IB&M**: **+12% YoY** (trading income).
  - **New Zealand**: **+5% YoY** (mortgage growth).
- **Structural vs. Cyclical**:
  - **Structural**: **Digital adoption** (lower costs), **SME lending** (share gains), **NZ expansion**.
  - **Cyclical**: **NII growth tied to RBA rates** (peaked in FY2024), **trading income volatility**.
- **M&A/Divestitures**: **No major deals** in FY2022–FY2024; **$1B buyback program** (FY2024).

> **MD&A Quote**: *"Our growth is underpinned by secular trends—digital adoption, SME lending, and NZ’s resilient economy."* — FY2024 Annual Report

## Margin Profile
- **Operating Margin**: **55% in FY2024** (down from **61% in FY2023**), driven by:
  - **NIM compression**: **~2.0% in FY2024** (down from **~2.2% in FY2023**).
  - **Higher funding costs**: **Term deposit rates up ~200bps** (FY2024).
- **Segment Margins**:
  - **Retail Banking**: **36%** (down from **40% in FY2023**).
  - **Business Banking**: **44%** (stable).
  - **IB&M**: **41%** (up from **38% in FY2023**).
  - **New Zealand**: **42%** (stable).
- **Operating Leverage**: **Cost-to-income ratio of 45%** (up from **42% in FY2023**), as **expenses grew 2% YoY** (wages, tech, compliance).

## Capital Allocation & Strategy
### **Priorities (MD&A)**
1. **Organic Growth**: **$1B/year in tech investment** (AI, cybersecurity, digital platforms).
2. **Dividends**: **~75% payout ratio** (FY2024 dividend: **$4.50/share**, +2% YoY).
3. **Buybacks**: **$1B program** (FY2024), targeting **~1% of shares outstanding**.
4. **M&A**: **No large deals**—focus on **bolt-ons** (e.g., **Kounta** for SME payments).

> **MD&A Quote**: *"Our capital priorities are clear: invest in growth, return excess capital to shareholders, and maintain a strong balance sheet."* — FY2024 Annual Report

### **Financial Targets**
- **Return on Equity (ROE)**: **14–16%** (FY2024: **14.2%**).
- **Cost-to-Income Ratio**: **<45%** (FY2024: **45%**).
- **Dividend Payout**: **70–80% of cash earnings**.

---

## Key Risks
1. **Margin Compression**: **NIMs could fall further** if deposit competition intensifies or the RBA cuts rates.
2. **Credit Quality**: **~60% of loans are mortgages**—a **10% housing downturn** could increase losses by **~$500M**.
3. **Regulatory Costs**: **$700M+ in remediation** (FY2020–FY2024); **new rules (e.g., CCCFA in NZ)** could limit growth.
4. **Digital Disruption**: **Fintechs (e.g., Judo Bank, Up)** are gaining share in SME lending and deposits.
5. **Geopolitical/Climate**: **~$10B in exposure to energy/agribusiness**—climate risks could increase capital requirements.

> **MD&A Quote**: *"The biggest risk to our margins is the competitive intensity for deposits, particularly as term deposit rates remain elevated."* — FY2024 Annual Report
