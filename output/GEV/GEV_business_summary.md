# GE Vernova Inc. (GEV) — Business Model Analysis

## Executive Summary
GE Vernova Inc. (GEV) is a global leader in the electric power industry, providing technologies and services that generate, transfer, convert, and store electricity. The company operates across three core segments: **Power** (gas, nuclear, hydro, and steam power), **Wind** (onshore and offshore wind turbines), and **Electrification** (grid solutions, power conversion, and software). GEV makes money primarily through **long-term equipment sales, service contracts, and project-based solutions**, with a growing emphasis on **digital and software-enabled offerings** to enhance recurring revenue.

The **single most important thing to know** is that **Electrification is the growth engine**—revenue surged **28% YoY in FY2025** (to $9.6B) and now represents **25% of total revenue**, driven by secular demand for grid modernization and energy transition infrastructure. Meanwhile, **Wind remains a drag** (revenue down **6% YoY** to $9.1B) due to execution challenges in offshore wind and blade manufacturing.

---

## Business Model
GE Vernova sells **capital-intensive, mission-critical equipment and services** to utilities, independent power producers (IPPs), governments, and industrial customers. Key products include:
- **Power**: Gas turbines (HA/FA models), nuclear reactors (e.g., BWRX-300 small modular reactors), hydro turbines, and steam power systems.
- **Wind**: Onshore/offshore wind turbines (e.g., Haliade-X offshore platform) and blade manufacturing (LM Wind Power).
- **Electrification**: Grid automation software (e.g., GridOS), transformers, power conversion systems (e.g., inverters for renewables), and energy storage solutions.

**Unit economics**:
- **Equipment sales** (55% of revenue in FY2025) are **lumpy, project-based**, with **gross margins of ~20-25%** (higher for gas turbines, lower for wind). Contracts often include **multi-year service agreements** (45% of revenue), which provide **higher-margin, recurring cash flows** (services margins ~30-40%).
- **Digital/software** (e.g., GridOS) is a **high-margin growth lever** (software margins >50%), though still a small portion of revenue ($1B in FY2025).
- **Take rate**: GEV captures value through **aftermarket services** (e.g., outage optimization, parts replacement) and **digital subscriptions** (e.g., predictive maintenance analytics).

**Economic moat**:
1. **Scale and installed base**: GEV’s equipment generates **~25% of global electricity**, creating **high switching costs** for customers (e.g., utilities rely on GEV for spare parts and service expertise).
2. **Regulatory and IP barriers**: Nuclear and grid technologies require **decades-long certifications** and proprietary designs (e.g., HA gas turbines, BWRX-300 SMRs).
3. **Network effects**: Grid software (GridOS) benefits from **data aggregation** across GEV’s installed base, improving predictive algorithms.
4. **Brand and relationships**: Long-term contracts with **top-tier utilities** (e.g., NextEra, EDF, State Grid Corporation of China) and governments.

## Segment Deep Dive

### Power
**What it does**: Designs, manufactures, and services gas turbines (HA/FA models), nuclear reactors (BWRX-300 SMRs), hydro turbines, and steam power systems for utilities and IPPs.
- **FY2025 revenue**: $19.8B (**52% of total**), +9% YoY.
- **3-year CAGR**: **4.5%** (FY2023–FY2025).
- **Operating income (FY2024)**: $2.3B (**12.7% margin**).

**Growth drivers**:
- **Gas Power** ($16B in FY2025, +11% YoY) is the **cash cow**, benefiting from **global demand for reliable baseload power** and **hydrogen-ready turbines** (e.g., HA turbines can burn up to 50% hydrogen). Management notes:
  > *"Gas Power remains the backbone of our portfolio, with strong demand for our HA turbines in regions transitioning from coal to gas."* (FY2025 10-K)
- **Nuclear Power** ($1B, +24% YoY) is growing due to **SMR orders** (e.g., Ontario Power Generation’s BWRX-300 deployment) and **fuel services**.
- **Hydro Power** ($0.8B, +3% YoY) is stable, with demand for **modernization projects** in emerging markets.

**Risks**:
- **Steam Power** ($1.9B, -6% YoY) is **declining** as coal plants retire.
- **Geopolitical exposure**: ~40% of revenue is outside the U.S. (e.g., Middle East, Asia), with **FX volatility** and **tariffs** ($250M impact in FY2025).
- **Hydrogen transition**: While HA turbines are hydrogen-ready, **commercial-scale hydrogen supply** remains a bottleneck.

---

### Wind
**What it does**: Manufactures onshore/offshore wind turbines (e.g., Haliade-X) and blades (LM Wind Power), with services for maintenance and upgrades.
- **FY2025 revenue**: $9.1B (**24% of total**), **-6% YoY**.
- **3-year CAGR**: **-1.5%** (FY2023–FY2025).
- **Operating income (FY2024)**: **-$0.6B** (**-6.2% margin**).

**Growth drivers**:
- **Onshore Wind** ($8.2B, +6% YoY) is the **only bright spot**, driven by **U.S. IRA incentives** and **repowering projects** in Europe.
- **Services revenue** (not separately disclosed) is growing as the installed base ages, but **not enough to offset equipment losses**.

**Risks**:
- **Offshore Wind** ($0.7B, -53% YoY) is **collapsing** due to **supply chain cost overruns**, **permitting delays**, and **canceled projects** (e.g., Ørsted’s U.S. pullback). Management admits:
  > *"Offshore Wind faced significant headwinds in 2025, including project cancellations and cost pressures, which we are addressing through portfolio rationalization."* (FY2025 10-K)
- **LM Wind Power** ($0.2B, -60% YoY) is **unprofitable**, with **high fixed costs** and **low blade prices**.
- **Execution challenges**: **Quality issues** (e.g., blade cracks) and **project delays** have led to **warranty reserves** and **customer disputes**.

---

### Electrification
**What it does**: Provides grid automation software (GridOS), transformers, power conversion systems (inverters, storage), and electrification software for utilities and industrial customers.
- **FY2025 revenue**: $9.6B (**25% of total**), **+28% YoY**.
- **3-year CAGR**: **22%** (FY2023–FY2025).
- **Operating income (FY2024)**: $0.7B (**9.2% margin**).

**Growth drivers**:
- **Grid Solutions** ($6.6B, +34% YoY) is the **standout performer**, driven by **grid modernization** (e.g., U.S. DOE grants, EU Green Deal) and **Prolec GE acquisition** (transformer JV, closing Feb 2026 for $5.3B). Management highlights:
  > *"Grid Solutions is our fastest-growing business, with demand for grid hardening, renewable integration, and digital substations."* (FY2025 10-K)
- **Power Conversion & Storage** ($2B, +22% YoY) benefits from **battery storage projects** and **EV charging infrastructure**.
- **Software** ($1B, +6% YoY) is growing via **GridOS subscriptions** and **AI-driven grid optimization**.

**Risks**:
- **Cyclicality**: Grid spending is tied to **government policies** (e.g., U.S. IRA, EU Green Deal), which could slow if political priorities shift.
- **Execution risk**: The **Prolec GE acquisition** ($5.3B) is **debt-funded** and requires **cost synergies** to justify the valuation.
- **Competition**: Siemens, Hitachi, and Schneider Electric are **aggressively investing** in grid software and automation.

## Growth Trajectory
- **Total revenue**: **$38.1B in FY2025** (+9% YoY), with a **3-year CAGR of 7%** (FY2023–FY2025).
- **Segment drivers**:
  - **Electrification** contributed **~60% of total growth** in FY2025 (up $2B YoY).
  - **Power** grew **$1.7B YoY**, driven by **Gas Power** and **Nuclear**.
  - **Wind** **dragged growth** (down $0.6B YoY).
- **Structural vs. cyclical**:
  - **Electrification** is **structural** (grid modernization, renewables integration, digitalization).
  - **Power** is **cyclical** (gas turbine demand tied to **energy security** and **coal retirements**).
  - **Wind** is **cyclical and execution-dependent** (offshore wind recovery hinges on **cost reductions** and **policy support**).
- **M&A**: **Prolec GE acquisition** ($5.3B) will **boost Electrification** but adds **integration risk**.

## Margin Profile
- **Gross margin**: **19.7% in FY2025** (vs. 17.5% in FY2024), driven by **higher-margin services** and **Electrification growth**.
- **Operating margin**: **3.7% in FY2025** (vs. 1.4% in FY2024), with **Wind still unprofitable** (-6.6% margin).
- **Segment margins**:
  | Segment        | FY2025 Margin | FY2024 Margin | Trend          |
  |----------------|---------------|---------------|----------------|
  | Power          | ~14.7%*       | 12.7%         | Improving      |
  | Wind           | ~-6.6%*       | -6.2%         | Worsening      |
  | Electrification| ~14.9%*       | 9.0%          | **Biggest lift** |
  *Organic margins (non-GAAP).
- **Operating leverage**: **SG&A as % of revenue fell to 12.9% in FY2025** (vs. 13.2% in FY2024), reflecting **cost discipline** and **scale benefits** in Electrification.

## Capital Allocation & Strategy
**Management priorities (per FY2025 10-K)**:
1. **Organic investment**: **R&D spend was $1.2B in FY2025** (3.2% of revenue), focused on **hydrogen-ready turbines**, **SMRs**, and **grid software**.
2. **M&A**: **Prolec GE acquisition** ($5.3B) to **double down on grid infrastructure**.
3. **Shareholder returns**: **$1.1B in buybacks in Q4 2025** (1.9M shares), with a **target to return 1/3 of cash generation** to shareholders.
4. **Debt reduction**: **Net debt/EBITDA target of ~2.5x** (currently ~3.0x post-Prolec GE).

**Strategic initiatives**:
- **Hydrogen**: Investing in **HA turbine upgrades** for 100% hydrogen combustion by 2030.
- **Grid modernization**: **GridOS software** is a **key differentiator**, with **AI-driven predictive maintenance** and **autonomous grid control**.
- **Wind turnaround**: **Exiting unprofitable offshore projects** and **focusing on services** to improve margins.

**Outlook**:
- **Management expects** **mid-single-digit revenue growth** and **margin expansion** in FY2026, driven by **Electrification** and **Power services**.
- **Wind is expected to stabilize** but remains a **wildcard** depending on **policy support** and **cost reductions**.

## Key Risks
1. **Wind execution**: **Offshore wind cancellations** and **blade quality issues** could lead to **further write-downs** and **customer disputes**.
2. **Grid cyclicality**: **Electrification growth is tied to government spending** (e.g., U.S. IRA, EU Green Deal), which could **slow if policies change**.
3. **Geopolitical exposure**: **~40% of revenue is international**, with **FX volatility** and **tariffs** ($250M impact in FY2025).
4. **Hydrogen transition**: **HA turbines are hydrogen-ready**, but **commercial-scale hydrogen supply** is **years away**.
5. **Prolec GE integration**: **$5.3B acquisition** adds **debt and execution risk**, with **synergies critical** to justify the valuation.
