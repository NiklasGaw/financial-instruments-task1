# Amgen Inc. (AMGN) — Business Model Analysis

## Executive Summary
Amgen Inc. is a leading biotechnology company that discovers, develops, manufactures, and commercializes innovative human therapeutics, focusing on areas of high unmet medical need such as oncology, cardiovascular disease, bone health, and inflammation. The company generates revenue primarily through the sale of branded biologics and small-molecule drugs, with **~95% of FY2025 revenue ($35.1B) derived from product sales**. The single most important thing to know is that **Amgen’s growth is increasingly driven by a handful of high-growth assets (e.g., Repatha, TEZSPIRE, EVENITY, BLINCYTO) offsetting steep declines in legacy franchises (e.g., ENBREL, XGEVA)**, while patent expirations (e.g., Prolia, XGEVA in 2025) and Medicare price negotiations (e.g., Otezla in 2027) pose near-term risks.

---

## Business Model
Amgen sells **specialty pharmaceuticals**—primarily biologics (e.g., monoclonal antibodies, recombinant proteins) and small-molecule drugs—to **hospitals, clinics, specialty pharmacies, and government/private payers** (e.g., Medicare, commercial insurers). The unit economics revolve around **high-margin, high-price therapies** with long treatment durations (e.g., Repatha for cholesterol, Prolia for osteoporosis) or acute interventions (e.g., BLINCYTO for leukemia). Revenue is captured via **direct sales to providers/payers**, with net pricing influenced by rebates, discounts, and government programs (e.g., 340B, IRA).

Amgen’s economic moat stems from **intellectual property (IP)**, **regulatory exclusivity**, and **high switching costs**:
- **IP/Regulation**: Patents (e.g., Repatha’s PCSK9 inhibitor composition patents) and orphan drug exclusivities (e.g., TEPEZZA for thyroid eye disease) protect key franchises.
- **Switching Costs**: Biologics like ENBREL require complex manufacturing and administration, deterring rapid biosimilar adoption.
- **Scale**: Amgen’s global commercial infrastructure (e.g., 20,000+ employees, 100+ markets) and manufacturing network (e.g., next-gen facilities in Ohio, Rhode Island) enable efficient distribution and supply chain resilience.

## Segment Deep Dive
Amgen operates as a **single reportable segment (Human Therapeutics)**, with revenue concentrated in **14 principal products** accounting for ~79% of FY2025 sales. Below is a breakdown of the **top 5 products by revenue** and **key growth/decline drivers**:

### Product Sales (FY2025: $35.1B, 95% of total)
#### 1. **Prolia** (FY2025: $4.4B, 12% of total)
- **What it does**: Monoclonal antibody for osteoporosis and bone loss (inhibits RANKL).
- **Trajectory**: Flat YoY (+1% in FY2025) after 8% CAGR (FY2022–FY2024). U.S. sales grew 3% YoY, while ROW declined 4%.
- **Key drivers**:
  - **Volume growth** (2% in FY2025) from expanded use in high-risk patients (e.g., cancer-related bone loss).
  - **Favorable sales deductions** (2% benefit in FY2025) due to rebate adjustments.
- **Headwinds**:
  - **Patent expiration**: U.S. patents expired in **February 2025**; European patents expired in **November 2025**. Multiple biosimilars launched in 2025, accelerating sales erosion in 2026.
  - **Pricing pressure**: Net selling price declined in FY2025; further declines expected in 2026.
  - **Litigation**: Ongoing disputes with biosimilar manufacturers (see Note 20, Contingencies).

#### 2. **Repatha** (FY2025: $3.0B, 8% of total)
- **What it does**: PCSK9 inhibitor for cardiovascular disease (lowers LDL cholesterol).
- **Trajectory**: **36% YoY growth in FY2025** (FY2023–FY2025 CAGR: 36%), driven by volume expansion.
- **Key drivers**:
  - **Volume growth** (primary driver) from broader adoption in high-risk patients (e.g., familial hypercholesterolemia, secondary prevention).
  - **Guideline updates**: 2022 ACC/AHA guidelines expanded PCSK9 inhibitor recommendations.
- **Headwinds**:
  - **Net pricing**: Expected to decline **mid-single digits in 2026** due to payer negotiations.
  - **Competition**: Regeneron’s Praluent (alirocumab) competes in the same class.

#### 3. **Otezla** (FY2025: $2.3B, 6% of total)
- **What it does**: Small-molecule PDE4 inhibitor for psoriasis and psoriatic arthritis.
- **Trajectory**: **7% YoY growth in FY2025** (FY2023–FY2025 CAGR: 2%), recovering from a 3% decline in FY2024.
- **Key drivers**:
  - **Volume growth** (3% in FY2025) from expanded use in mild-to-moderate psoriasis.
  - **Favorable sales deductions** (2% benefit in FY2025).
- **Headwinds**:
  - **Medicare price negotiation**: Selected by CMS for **2027 price setting**, which will reduce net selling price.
  - **Competition**: Faces pressure from biologics (e.g., Humira, Skyrizi) and JAK inhibitors (e.g., Rinvoq).

#### 4. **ENBREL** (FY2025: $2.2B, 6% of total)
- **What it does**: TNF inhibitor for autoimmune diseases (e.g., rheumatoid arthritis, psoriasis).
- **Trajectory**: **33% YoY decline in FY2025** (FY2023–FY2025 CAGR: -22%), accelerating from -10% in FY2024.
- **Key drivers**:
  - **Volume growth** (4% in FY2025) offset by **36% net price decline** due to:
    - **340B Program mix**: Higher sales to 340B-covered entities (which receive discounts).
    - **Medicare Part D redesign**: Lower reimbursement rates.
    - **Commercial discounts**: Increased rebates to maintain formulary access.
- **Headwinds**:
  - **Biosimilar competition**: Multiple biosimilars launched in the U.S. (e.g., Erelzi, Eticovo), eroding market share.
  - **Payer pressure**: Preferential formulary placement for lower-cost alternatives.

#### 5. **EVENITY** (FY2025: $2.1B, 6% of total)
- **What it does**: Sclerostin inhibitor for osteoporosis (stimulates bone formation).
- **Trajectory**: **34% YoY growth in FY2025** (FY2023–FY2025 CAGR: 35%), driven by volume expansion.
- **Key drivers**:
  - **Volume growth** (primary driver) from adoption in high-risk patients (e.g., post-menopausal women with severe osteoporosis).
  - **Guideline support**: Recommended for patients with very high fracture risk.

### Other Revenues (FY2025: $1.6B, 5% of total)
- **What it does**: Includes **royalties, collaboration revenue, and contract manufacturing**.
- **Trajectory**: **15% YoY growth in FY2025**, recovering from a 16% decline in FY2023.
- **Key drivers**:
  - **Collaboration revenue**: Payments from partners (e.g., AstraZeneca for TEZSPIRE, BeiGene for cancer therapies).
  - **Contract manufacturing**: Production for third parties (e.g., COVID-19 vaccine components).

## Growth Trajectory
- **Total revenue**: **FY2023–FY2025 CAGR: 15%** ($26.9B → $36.8B), driven by:
  - **High-growth assets**: Repatha (+36% CAGR), TEZSPIRE (+52% CAGR), EVENITY (+35% CAGR), BLINCYTO (+28% CAGR).
  - **Acquisitions**: Horizon Therapeutics (closed October 2023) added **TEPEZZA ($1.9B in FY2025)** and **KRYSTEXXA ($1.3B in FY2025)**.
- **Structural vs. cyclical**:
  - **Structural**: Growth in **cardiovascular (Repatha), oncology (BLINCYTO), and rare diseases (TEPEZZA, KRYSTEXXA)** reflects secular demand for innovative biologics.
  - **Cyclical**: **ENBREL’s decline** is tied to biosimilar competition and payer dynamics (e.g., 340B, IRA).
- **MD&A quote**:
  > *"In 2025, we generated strong sales growth across our product portfolio and regions; advanced our innovative pipeline; and continued to expand and enhance our world-class manufacturing network."* — Amgen FY2025 10-K.

## Margin Profile
- **Gross margin**: **67.4% in FY2025** (stable vs. 67.3% in FY2024), reflecting high-margin biologics.
- **Operating margin**: **24.7% in FY2025** (vs. 21.9% in FY2024), driven by **operating leverage** (revenue grew 10% YoY while operating expenses grew 5%).
- **Segment margins**:
  - **Product Sales**: ~70% gross margin (biologics like Repatha, Prolia).
  - **Other Revenues**: Lower-margin (e.g., royalties, contract manufacturing).
- **Efficiency**: **SG&A as % of revenue declined from 21.3% in FY2023 to 19.0% in FY2025**, reflecting cost discipline.

## Capital Allocation & Strategy
- **Priorities** (per MD&A):
  1. **Organic investment**: **R&D intensity = 15–17% of revenue** (FY2025: $5.5B, +12% YoY). Focus on **cardiovascular, oncology, and inflammation**.
  2. **M&A**: **Horizon Therapeutics acquisition ($27.8B, 2023)** added TEPEZZA and KRYSTEXXA.
  3. **Shareholder returns**:
     - **Dividends**: $3.00/share in FY2025 (4.2% yield), with a **target payout ratio of 40–50%**.
     - **Buybacks**: $0 in FY2025 (paused to fund Horizon acquisition), but **$6.8B remaining under authorization**.
  4. **Debt reduction**: Retired **$6.0B of debt in FY2025** (leverage ratio: 2.1x net debt/EBITDA).
- **Strategic initiatives**:
  - **Manufacturing**: Next-gen facilities in **Ohio ($3.5B investment)** and **Rhode Island** to support pipeline.
  - **AI/ML**: AI Governance Council established to integrate AI into R&D and commercial operations.
- **Outlook**:
  - **2026 guidance**: Expect **low-single-digit revenue growth**, with **high-growth assets offsetting ENBREL/Prolia declines**.
  - **Long-term**: Target **mid-single-digit revenue CAGR** and **expanding operating margins**.

## Key Risks
1. **Patent expirations**: **Prolia and XGEVA** lost U.S. exclusivity in 2025, with biosimilars accelerating sales erosion in 2026.
   - *MD&A quote*: *"For 2026, we expect accelerated sales erosion driven by increased competition, as multiple biosimilars have launched in the United States and ROW."*
2. **Pricing pressure**: **Medicare price negotiation (IRA)** will impact Otezla (2027) and other products (2028+).
3. **Biosimilar competition**: **ENBREL** faces ongoing erosion from biosimilars (e.g., Erelzi, Eticovo).
4. **Regulatory risks**: **FDA approvals** for pipeline assets (e.g., tarlatamab for SCLC) are critical for growth.
5. **Tax liabilities**: **IRS dispute** over $7.1B in potential tax liabilities (see Note 7, Income Taxes).
