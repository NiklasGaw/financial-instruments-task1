# Verizon Communications (VZ) — Business Model Analysis

## Executive Summary
Verizon Communications is the largest U.S. wireless carrier, generating **$138.2B in FY2025 revenue** (up 2.5% YoY) primarily through **subscription-based wireless and wireline services** for consumers and businesses. The **Consumer segment drives 77% of revenue and 92% of operating income**, with wireless service revenue (65% of Consumer revenue) underpinned by **high-margin recurring subscriptions** (ARPA of **$147.31 in FY2025**, up 2.3% YoY). The company’s economic moat stems from **scale (115.9M retail connections), spectrum ownership (C-Band), and switching costs** (postpaid churn of **1.15%**). The key near-term focus is **5G Fixed Wireless Access (FWA) growth** (3.4M connections, +25.5% YoY), which is offsetting declines in legacy wireline services.

---

## Business Model
Verizon sells **wireless connectivity (voice/data), broadband (Fios/FWA), and enterprise solutions** to **consumers (77% of revenue) and businesses (21%)**. The core unit economics revolve around **postpaid wireless contracts** (95.7M connections), where customers commit to **24-month service plans** with embedded device financing (e.g., $1,000 iPhones bundled into monthly bills). Value capture is **subscription-driven** (wireless service margins ~50%), with **equipment sales** (20% of Consumer revenue) acting as a lower-margin hook to drive service adoption.

**Economic moats**:
1. **Scale**: Verizon’s **4G/5G network covers 99% of the U.S. population**, with **C-Band spectrum** (3.5GHz) enabling high-speed FWA. This creates a **cost advantage** (network costs are largely fixed) and **barriers to entry** (spectrum licenses are finite).
2. **Switching costs**: Postpaid churn is **1.15%**, reflecting **contractual lock-in** (early termination fees) and **brand loyalty** (Verizon ranks #1 in customer satisfaction for network quality).
3. **Regulatory barriers**: The FCC’s **Universal Service Fund (USF)** subsidizes rural broadband, but Verizon’s **incumbent status** (e.g., legacy copper networks) limits competition in wireline.
4. **Network effects**: More users improve spectrum efficiency (e.g., **5G mmWave** requires dense deployments), while **wholesale partnerships** (e.g., MVNOs like Visible) monetize excess capacity.

## Segment Deep Dive

### Consumer
**What it does**: Wireless (postpaid/prepaid), Fios broadband/video, and **5G Fixed Wireless Access (FWA)**.
- **FY2025 revenue**: **$106.8B (77% of total)**, +3.8% YoY.
- **3-year revenue CAGR**: **1.7%** (FY2023–2025).
- **Operating income**: **$29.6B (92% of total)**, margin of **27.7%**.

**Growth drivers**:
- **Wireless service revenue**: **+2.1% YoY** (FY2025) driven by **higher ARPA ($147.31, +2.3% YoY)** from premium plans (e.g., **MyPlan perks**) and **FWA adoption** (3.4M connections, +25.5% YoY). Management notes:
  > *"Postpaid revenue growth was driven by pricing actions, higher adoption of premium MyPlan offerings, and a 26% increase in our FWA subscriber base."*
- **Wireless equipment revenue**: **+11.1% YoY** (FY2025) due to **16% more upgrades** and a shift to higher-priced devices (e.g., iPhone 15).

**Headwinds/Risks**:
- **Fios video decline**: **-9.1% YoY** (2.4M connections) as cord-cutting accelerates.
- **Prepaid weakness**: Core prepaid connections fell **-1.7% YoY** (FY2024) due to the **Affordable Connectivity Program (ACP) termination**.
- **Churn pressure**: Postpaid phone churn rose to **0.92%** (vs. 0.83% in FY2024), signaling **competitive intensity** (e.g., T-Mobile’s aggressive promotions).

---

### Business
**What it does**: Enterprise networking (SD-WAN, cloud), public sector contracts, and **wholesale wireless** (MVNOs).
- **FY2025 revenue**: **$29.1B (21% of total)**, **-1.6% YoY**.
- **3-year revenue CAGR**: **-1.7%** (FY2023–2025).
- **Operating income**: **$2.5B (8% of total)**, margin of **8.6%**.

**Growth drivers**:
- **Business Markets (SMB)**: **+3.7% YoY** (FY2025) driven by **cloud and security services**.
- **FWA for enterprise**: Growing but not yet offsetting declines in **legacy wireline**.

**Headwinds/Risks**:
- **Enterprise/Public Sector**: **-4.8% YoY** (FY2025) due to **budget constraints** and **competition from AT&T and Lumen**.
- **Wholesale**: **-11.7% YoY** (FY2025) as MVNOs (e.g., Visible) **optimize their own networks**.
- **Margin compression**: Operating income fell **-13.8% YoY** (FY2025) due to **higher sales commissions** and **restructuring charges**.

---

## Growth Trajectory
- **Total revenue 3-year CAGR**: **0.8%** (FY2023–2025), with **Consumer (+1.7% CAGR) offsetting Business (-1.7% CAGR)**.
- **Structural growth**: **FWA** (3.4M connections, +25.5% YoY) is the **bright spot**, leveraging 5G to compete with cable broadband. Management highlights:
  > *"FWA is a key growth driver, with 693K net additions in FY2025, though growth is slowing as we saturate early-adopter markets."*
- **Cyclical pressures**: **Enterprise spending** remains weak, and **wireless equipment revenue** is volatile (e.g., **-5.1% YoY in FY2024** due to lower upgrades).
- **M&A/Divestitures**: No major deals in the period, but **capex is focused on C-Band deployment** (5G expansion).

---

## Margin Profile
- **Operating margin**: **21.2% in FY2025** (flat YoY), with **Consumer at 27.7%** and **Business at 8.6%**.
- **Consumer margin resilience**: Despite **higher equipment costs (+12.6% YoY)**, service margins remain stable (~50%) due to **ARPA growth** and **FWA adoption**.
- **Business margin erosion**: Operating income fell **-13.8% YoY** (FY2025) due to **lower enterprise sales** and **restructuring charges**.
- **Operating leverage**: **SG&A costs declined 0.9% YoY** (FY2025) due to **workforce reductions**, but **depreciation rose 2.6%** (C-Band rollout).

---

## Capital Allocation & Strategy
**Priorities (per MD&A)**:
1. **Capex**: **$18.3B in FY2025** (13.2% of revenue), focused on **C-Band 5G deployment** and **FWA expansion**.
   > *"Our capital spending is prioritized to enhance network quality and expand FWA, which is critical to offsetting wireline declines."*
2. **Dividends**: **$10.4B paid in FY2025** (60% of net income), with a **4.6% yield** (as of March 2026).
3. **Debt reduction**: **$3B in debt repayments** (FY2025), targeting **$147B average debt** (down from $150B in FY2024).
4. **Share buybacks**: **$2B authorized** (FY2025), but **paused in Q1 2026** due to **spectrum auction costs**.

**R&D intensity**: Not disclosed, but **5G patents** (e.g., mmWave, network slicing) are a **key differentiator**.

**3-year outlook**:
- **Revenue growth**: Low-single digits, driven by **FWA and ARPA expansion**.
- **EBITDA margin**: **~36%** (FY2025 Adjusted EBITDA margin), with **cost discipline** offsetting **C-Band amortization**.

---

## Key Risks
1. **5G monetization**: **FWA growth is slowing** (net adds down **-18.1% YoY in FY2025**), and **enterprise 5G adoption** remains sluggish.
   > *"We are seeing early signs of FWA saturation in urban markets, which may limit future growth."*
2. **Competitive intensity**: **T-Mobile’s aggressive promotions** (e.g., free iPhones) are pressuring **churn (0.92% in FY2025 vs. 0.83% in FY2024)**.
3. **Regulatory headwinds**: **FCC broadband subsidies** (e.g., ACP termination) hurt **prepaid growth**, and **spectrum auctions** (e.g., 3.45GHz) could **increase capex**.
4. **Legacy wireline decline**: **Fios video connections fell -9.1% YoY**, and **copper network decommissioning** could **strand enterprise customers**.
5. **Pension liabilities**: **$453M remeasurement loss in FY2025** (vs. $657M gain in FY2024) adds **volatility to net income**.
