# Abbott Laboratories (ABT) — Business Model Analysis

## Executive Summary
Abbott Laboratories is a diversified healthcare company operating in four core segments: **Established Pharmaceuticals**, **Nutritional Products**, **Diagnostic Products**, and **Medical Devices**. The company generates revenue primarily through the sale of branded medical products, with **Medical Devices** (FY2025: $21.4B, 48% of total revenue) and **Diagnostics** (FY2025: $8.9B, 20%) driving the bulk of earnings. Abbott’s economic moat stems from **regulatory barriers, IP protection, and scale** in high-growth therapeutic areas like diabetes care and structural heart devices. The most critical near-term dynamic is the **structural growth in Medical Devices** (12.7% YoY in FY2025), offsetting cyclical declines in Diagnostics (post-COVID normalization) and margin pressures in Nutritionals.

---

## Business Model
Abbott sells **regulated healthcare products** to hospitals, clinics, pharmacies, and consumers globally. Its business model relies on:
- **High-margin branded goods**: Medical devices (e.g., FreeStyle Libre glucose monitors) and diagnostics (e.g., Alinity lab systems) command premium pricing due to clinical differentiation and regulatory exclusivity.
- **Recurring revenue streams**: Diabetes care (e.g., continuous glucose monitors) and nutritional products (e.g., Ensure) generate sticky customer relationships with repeat purchases.
- **Geographic diversification**: 61% of FY2025 revenue came from outside the U.S., with emerging markets (e.g., India, China) driving growth in Established Pharmaceuticals.

**Value capture**: Abbott earns **gross margins of ~56%** (FY2025) via direct sales to providers and distributors, with operating margins of **18.3%** (FY2025). Its moat is reinforced by:
- **Regulatory approvals**: FDA/EMA clearances create high barriers to entry (e.g., 510(k) for devices).
- **IP portfolio**: 10,000+ patents protect products like the FreeStyle Libre (Diabetes Care).
- **Switching costs**: Clinicians and patients rely on Abbott’s integrated systems (e.g., lab diagnostics + devices).

## Segment Deep Dive

### Medical Devices
**What it does**: Develops and sells devices for cardiovascular, neuromodulation, and diabetes care (e.g., pacemakers, glucose monitors).
- **FY2025 revenue**: $21.4B (48% of total), +12.7% YoY.
- **3-year CAGR**: 13.2% (FY2023–2025).
- **Operating margin**: ~30% (FY2024: 32.6%).
- **Key drivers**:
  - **Diabetes Care** ($8.0B, +17.5% YoY): FreeStyle Libre adoption (1.5M+ global users) and expansion into Type 2 diabetes.
  - **Structural Heart** ($2.5B, +12.3% YoY): Growth in transcatheter aortic valve replacement (TAVR) and mitral valve therapies.
  - **Electrophysiology** ($2.8B, +12.0% YoY): Demand for ablation catheters and cardiac mapping systems.
- **Risks**:
  - **Pricing pressure**: U.S. Medicare reimbursement cuts for diabetes devices (e.g., 2023 CMS rule).
  - **Competition**: Dexcom (CGMs), Medtronic (cardiac devices), and emerging AI-driven diagnostics.

> **MD&A Quote**: *"Our Medical Devices business delivered another year of double-digit growth, driven by strong performance in Diabetes Care and Structural Heart. We continue to invest in innovation to expand our leadership in these high-growth areas."*

---

### Diagnostic Products
**What it does**: Provides lab-based and point-of-care diagnostic tests (e.g., COVID-19 tests, Alinity lab systems).
- **FY2025 revenue**: $8.9B (20% of total), **-4.3% YoY** (post-COVID normalization).
- **3-year CAGR**: -5.5% (FY2023–2025).
- **Operating margin**: ~22% (FY2024: 22.6%).
- **Key drivers**:
  - **Core Laboratory** ($5.4B, +2.4% YoY): Stable demand for routine lab tests (e.g., chemistry, immunoassay).
  - **Point of Care** ($0.6B, +3.1% YoY): Growth in rapid infectious disease tests.
- **Headwinds**:
  - **Rapid Diagnostics** ($2.5B, -18.1% YoY): Decline in COVID-19 testing revenue (FY2023: $3.7B → FY2025: $2.5B).
  - **Molecular** ($0.5B, -0.7% YoY): Lower demand for PCR tests post-pandemic.

> **MD&A Quote**: *"While COVID-19 testing revenue declined significantly, our Core Laboratory and Point of Care businesses showed resilience, with growth in infectious disease and oncology testing."*

---

### Nutritional Products
**What it does**: Sells pediatric and adult nutritionals (e.g., Similac, Ensure, Pedialyte).
- **FY2025 revenue**: $8.5B (19% of total), +0.5% YoY.
- **3-year CAGR**: 1.2% (FY2023–2025).
- **Operating margin**: ~18% (FY2024: 17.9%).
- **Key drivers**:
  - **Adult Nutritionals** ($4.5B, +2.0% YoY): Demand for Ensure and Glucerna in aging populations.
  - **International markets**: Growth in China and Latin America.
- **Headwinds**:
  - **Pediatric Nutritionals** ($4.0B, -1.2% YoY): U.S. birth rate decline and competitive pressure from private-label brands.
  - **Supply chain costs**: Inflationary pressures on raw materials (e.g., dairy, soy).

> **MD&A Quote**: *"We are focused on innovation in adult nutrition and expanding our presence in emerging markets to offset softness in the U.S. pediatric segment."*

---

### Established Pharmaceutical Products
**What it does**: Sells branded generics and mature drugs in emerging markets (e.g., hypertension, pain management).
- **FY2025 revenue**: $5.5B (12% of total), +6.6% YoY.
- **3-year CAGR**: 4.0% (FY2023–2025).
- **Operating margin**: ~23% (FY2024: 23.1%).
- **Key drivers**:
  - **Key Emerging Markets** ($4.2B, +8.0% YoY): Growth in India, China, and Brazil.
  - **Portfolio diversification**: 1,000+ products across 150+ countries.
- **Risks**:
  - **Pricing pressure**: Government cost-containment efforts in Europe and Asia.
  - **Currency volatility**: FX headwinds in Latin America and Russia.

## Growth Trajectory
- **Total revenue**: FY2025: $44.3B (+5.7% YoY); 3-year CAGR: 3.3% (FY2023–2025).
- **Growth drivers**:
  - **Medical Devices**: Structural growth in diabetes care (+17.5% YoY) and structural heart (+12.3% YoY).
  - **Established Pharmaceuticals**: Emerging market expansion (+8.0% YoY in "Key Markets").
- **Cyclical declines**: Diagnostics (-4.3% YoY) due to COVID-19 testing normalization.
- **M&A**: FY2025 acquisitions included **CardioMEMS** (heart failure monitoring) and **Bigfoot Biomedical** (diabetes management).

> **MD&A Quote**: *"Our growth is underpinned by secular trends in diabetes, cardiovascular disease, and emerging market healthcare access. We expect Medical Devices to remain the primary growth engine."*

---

## Margin Profile
- **Gross margin**: FY2025: 56.4% (stable vs. FY2024: 55.5%).
- **Operating margin**: FY2025: 18.3% (vs. FY2024: 16.2%; FY2023: 16.2%).
- **Segment margins**:
  - **Medical Devices**: 30%+ (highest).
  - **Diagnostics**: ~22%.
  - **Nutritionals**: ~18%.
  - **Established Pharmaceuticals**: ~23%.
- **Operating leverage**: SG&A as % of revenue declined from 27.2% (FY2023) to 27.8% (FY2025), while R&D intensity remained stable at **6.5% of revenue**.

---

## Capital Allocation & Strategy
- **Priorities** (per MD&A):
  1. **Organic growth**: $2.9B R&D spend (FY2025: 6.5% of revenue).
  2. **M&A**: Targeted bolt-ons (e.g., Bigfoot Biomedical) to fill portfolio gaps.
  3. **Dividends**: 51 consecutive years of increases; FY2025 dividend payout: $4.1B (63% of net income).
  4. **Buybacks**: $893M in FY2025 (vs. $1.3B in FY2024).
- **Capex**: $2.2B (FY2025), focused on manufacturing capacity for diabetes devices.
- **Outlook**: Management targets **mid-single-digit revenue growth** and **expanding operating margins** through 2027.

> **MD&A Quote**: *"We are committed to disciplined capital allocation, balancing growth investments with returning cash to shareholders. Our dividend remains a top priority."*

---

## Key Risks
1. **Regulatory and litigation risk**:
   - **Infant formula lawsuits**: 1,760+ pending cases alleging links to necrotizing enterocolitis (NEC); potential for material damages (e.g., $495M jury award in 2024).
   - **DOJ investigation**: Criminal probe into powdered infant formula manufacturing practices.
2. **Pricing pressure**:
   - U.S. and international cost-containment efforts (e.g., Medicare reimbursement cuts for CGMs).
3. **Supply chain disruptions**:
   - Reliance on global suppliers for raw materials (e.g., semiconductors for devices).
4. **FX volatility**:
   - 61% of revenue from international markets; USD strength could pressure earnings (e.g., FY2025: $1.6B FX loss in OCI).
5. **Competition**:
   - Diabetes Care: Dexcom (G7 CGM) and Medtronic (MiniMed pumps).
   - Structural Heart: Edwards Lifesciences (TAVR) and Boston Scientific (mitral valves).
