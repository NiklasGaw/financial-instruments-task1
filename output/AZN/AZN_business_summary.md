# AstraZeneca PLC (AZN) — Business Model Analysis

## Executive Summary
AstraZeneca PLC (AZN) is a global biopharmaceutical company that discovers, develops, and commercializes prescription medicines across six therapeutic areas: Oncology, Cardiovascular/Renal/Metabolism (CVRM), Respiratory & Immunology (R&I), Rare Disease, Vaccines & Immune Therapies, and Other Medicines. The company generates revenue primarily through the sale of branded pharmaceuticals, with **Oncology ($23.7B in FY2025, 40% of total revenue) and CVRM ($12.8B, 22%) driving ~62% of sales**. AstraZeneca’s economic moat stems from **patent-protected blockbuster drugs, high switching costs for patients/physicians, and a late-stage pipeline with 180+ projects**, including 30+ potential blockbusters. The most critical insight for investors is the **structural growth in Oncology and Rare Disease**, offset by **margin pressure from rising R&D costs (14.2B in FY2025, 24% of revenue) and competitive threats to legacy franchises (e.g., Brilinta’s -65% YoY decline in Q1 2026)**.

---

## Business Model
AstraZeneca sells **high-margin, patent-protected prescription drugs** to healthcare providers, governments, and insurers. The unit economics are defined by:
- **Product-based revenue**: One-time sales of drugs (e.g., Tagrisso for lung cancer) or chronic therapies (e.g., Farxiga for diabetes), with **gross margins >80%** (FY2025: 82%).
- **Contractual value capture**: Long-term contracts with payers (e.g., U.S. Medicare, European national health systems) and **rebate/discount structures** (typically 20–40% of list price) to secure formulary access.
- **Lifecycle management**: Patent cliffs are mitigated via **new indications (e.g., Enhertu for HER2-low breast cancer), formulations (e.g., injectable Farxiga), or next-gen molecules (e.g., Truqap in oncology)**.

**Economic moat**:
1. **Intellectual property**: 100+ patents expiring post-2030 (e.g., Tagrisso’s U.S. exclusivity until 2032).
2. **Switching costs**: Oncology drugs like Imfinzi require **companion diagnostics** and long-term monitoring, locking in patients.
3. **Regulatory barriers**: Rare Disease drugs (e.g., Ultomiris) benefit from **orphan drug exclusivity** (7–12 years post-approval).
4. **Scale**: $6.8B in R&D (FY2023) and **global commercial infrastructure** (100+ markets) deter competitors.

## Segment Deep Dive

### Oncology
- **What it does**: Develops cancer therapies targeting tumor biology (e.g., EGFR, HER2, PARP inhibitors).
- **FY2025**: $23.7B (40% of total revenue), +17% YoY.
- **3-year CAGR**: 18% (FY2022–2025).
- **Key drivers**:
  - **Tagrisso** ($1.8B in Q1 2026, +9% YoY): First-line EGFR-mutated NSCLC; **U.S. exclusivity until 2032**.
  - **Enhertu** ($0.8B, +40% YoY): HER2-directed ADC; **$10B+ peak sales potential** (management guidance).
  - **Imfinzi** ($1.7B, +34% YoY): PD-L1 inhibitor for lung/biliary cancers; **Phase 3 trials in 10+ indications**.
- **Risks**:
  - **Competition**: Merck’s Keytruda (PD-1) and Roche’s Tecentriq erode Imfinzi’s share in lung cancer.
  - **Pricing pressure**: U.S. IRA negotiations could cap Tagrisso’s price growth post-2026.

### Cardiovascular, Renal & Metabolism (CVRM)
- **What it does**: Treats diabetes, heart failure, and chronic kidney disease (CKD).
- **FY2025**: $12.8B (22% of revenue), +3% YoY (slowing from 18% CAGR FY2022–2024).
- **Key drivers**:
  - **Farxiga** ($2.2B in Q1 2026, +7% YoY): SGLT2 inhibitor for diabetes/heart failure; **$10B+ peak sales** (management).
  - **Lokelma** ($0.2B, +30% YoY): Hyperkalemia treatment; **orphan drug status in U.S./EU**.
- **Risks**:
  - **Brilinta’s collapse** ($0.1B in Q1 2026, -65% YoY): Patent expiry in 2024; generic competition.
  - **Payer pushback**: U.S. Medicare Part D negotiations may reduce Farxiga’s net price.

### Respiratory & Immunology (R&I)
- **What it does**: Asthma, COPD, and autoimmune diseases (e.g., lupus, eosinophilic esophagitis).
- **FY2025**: $8.2B (14% of revenue), +10% YoY.
- **3-year CAGR**: 12% (FY2022–2025).
- **Key drivers**:
  - **Tezspire** ($0.3B in Q1 2026, +40% YoY): Biologic for severe asthma; **$5B+ peak sales** (management).
  - **Fasenra** ($0.5B, +15% YoY): Eosinophilic asthma; **expanding into COPD**.
- **Risks**:
  - **Symbicort’s decline** ($0.7B, +3% YoY): Patent expiry in 2025; generic competition.
  - **Pricing erosion**: U.S. IRA may limit Tezspire’s reimbursement.

### Rare Disease
- **What it does**: Ultra-rare genetic disorders (e.g., paroxysmal nocturnal hemoglobinuria, hypophosphatasia).
- **FY2025**: $9.1B (16% of revenue), +5% YoY (slowing from 12% CAGR FY2022–2024).
- **Key drivers**:
  - **Ultomiris** ($1.3B in Q1 2026, +21% YoY): C5 inhibitor for PNH; **$5B+ peak sales**.
  - **Strensiq** ($0.5B, +47% YoY): Hypophosphatasia treatment; **orphan drug exclusivity until 2033**.
- **Risks**:
  - **Soliris’ decline** ($0.4B, -12% YoY): Ultomiris cannibalization; patent expiry in 2027.
  - **High R&D costs**: Rare Disease drugs require **$1B+ in development costs** per asset.

### Vaccines & Immune Therapies
- **What it does**: COVID-19, RSV, and flu vaccines; immune modulators.
- **FY2025**: $0.8B (1% of revenue), -20% YoY.
- **Key drivers**:
  - **Beyfortus** ($0.1B in Q1 2026, +3% YoY): RSV vaccine for infants; **$1B+ peak sales** (management).
- **Risks**:
  - **COVID-19 tail**: Revenue collapsed from $3.5B in FY2022 to $0.8B in FY2025.
  - **Competition**: Pfizer/BioNTech dominate RSV vaccines.

### Other Medicines
- **What it does**: Legacy brands (e.g., Crestor, Nexium) and emerging markets.
- **FY2025**: $1.0B (2% of revenue), -9% YoY.
- **Risks**:
  - **Patent cliffs**: Crestor ($0.4B in Q1 2026, +12% YoY) faces generic competition in 2026.

## Growth Trajectory
- **Total revenue**: $58.7B in FY2025 (+8.5% YoY), down from 18% CAGR FY2022–2024.
- **Drivers**:
  - **Structural**: Oncology (+17% YoY in FY2025) and Rare Disease (+5%) benefit from **new indications and geographic expansion**.
  - **Cyclical**: CVRM’s slowdown (+3% YoY) reflects **Brilinta’s patent expiry and Farxiga’s market saturation**.
- **M&A**: Recent acquisitions (e.g., **Alexion in 2021 for $39B**, **CinCor in 2023 for $1.8B**) bolster Rare Disease and CVRM.

**Management quote (FY2025 MD&A)**:
> *"Our growth is underpinned by **Oncology and Rare Disease**, where we’re seeing strong uptake of **Enhertu and Ultomiris**. However, we’re investing heavily in R&D ($14.2B in FY2025) to offset **Brilinta’s decline and Farxiga’s pricing pressure**."*

---

## Margin Profile
- **Gross margin**: 82% in FY2025 (stable YoY), reflecting **high-margin biologics (e.g., Enhertu) offsetting lower-margin small molecules (e.g., Farxiga)**.
- **Operating margin**: 23% in FY2025 (down from 46% in FY2024), due to:
  - **R&D surge**: 24% of revenue in FY2025 (vs. 15% in FY2023).
  - **SG&A spike**: 34% of revenue in FY2025 (vs. 7% in FY2024), driven by **commercial launches (e.g., Truqap, Wainua)**.
- **Segment margins**:
  - **Oncology/Rare Disease**: ~30–35% (estimated).
  - **CVRM/R&I**: ~20–25% (lower due to rebates and competition).

**Management quote (FY2025 MD&A)**:
> *"We’re prioritizing **margin expansion in Oncology and Rare Disease**, while accepting **lower margins in CVRM** as we invest in Farxiga’s next-gen formulations."*

---

## Capital Allocation & Strategy
- **Priorities**:
  1. **R&D**: 24% of revenue in FY2025 ($14.2B); **30+ potential blockbusters in late-stage pipeline**.
  2. **M&A**: Focus on **bolt-ons in Oncology and Rare Disease** (e.g., CinCor for $1.8B).
  3. **Dividends**: 2.8% yield; **progressive payout policy**.
- **Strategic initiatives**:
  - **AI-driven drug discovery**: Partnerships with BenevolentAI and Absci.
  - **Geographic expansion**: **China revenue +25% YoY in Q1 2026** (Oncology/Rare Disease).
- **Financial targets**:
  - **Mid-term**: 5–7% revenue CAGR, **operating margin >30%** by 2027.
  - **Long-term**: **$80B+ revenue by 2030**, driven by Oncology/Rare Disease.

---

## Key Risks
1. **Patent cliffs**: Brilinta (2024), Crestor (2026), and Soliris (2027) face generic competition.
2. **Pricing pressure**: U.S. IRA negotiations may **cap net prices for Farxiga/Tagrisso post-2026**.
3. **Pipeline execution**: **30% of late-stage assets fail in Phase 3** (industry average).
4. **Geographic concentration**: **U.S. = 45% of revenue**; FX volatility and policy changes (e.g., Medicare price cuts) pose risks.
5. **R&D cost inflation**: **$1B+ per rare disease asset**; Ultomiris’ $5B peak sales may not justify its $1.3B R&D spend.
